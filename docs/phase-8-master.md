# Phase 8 — The Stair Beneath Tarnsmere — Master GM Walkthrough

*A spoon-fed run guide. Read top to bottom; the order on this page is the order at the table. Phase 8 is a location crawl, not a scene sequence — but the locations resolve in a roughly fixed shape: surface, threshold, door, descent.*

---

## What this phase is for

Phase 8 is the **threshold dungeon** — the last surface-facing phase of Blooming Rot 2 and the bridge into the descent that drives the campaign's second half. The party has triangulated to a single site: a ruined toll station above a mining spur in the Cairn Hills, marked by a low cairn with seven cuts in its lid stone. **Tarnsmere Tollhouse.** The phase is about proving the route exists and crossing the line beyond which surface courts, ledgers, and friends can no longer protect the party.

Four jobs:

1. **Turn a phrase into a place.** *"No sail. No road. Toll paid beneath."* has been a line of fragments through Phases 6 and 7. Here the party finds it carved into stone above a door that responds to it.
2. **Reveal that the descent is administered.** The conspiracy did not build the route. They *rented* it from an older Aerdy bureaucracy — the Permissive Survey, Class VII — that wrote a procedure for going beneath the world and filed it.
3. **Open the Permission Door.** Six recognised methods plus Elle's iron-key seventh. The door is paperwork, not magic. Forcing it works and costs.
4. **Get the party across the final threshold.** Down the Stair Gallery, past a lintel that reads *Accounts above. Witness below.*, into the older, wider passage where Phase 9 begins.

Phase 8 is **expansion content** past the original five-phase arc, but it is the *first* dungeon, not a bonus dungeon. Tier 3, levels 8–11. One to two sessions. **Tone**: old-stone dread, surface silence, buried bureaucracy — *administrative horror becomes architectural.*

Hard player-facing constraint: **do not use the word "Underdark"** in read-aloud, handouts, or NPC dialogue. Use *below*, *deep road*, *descent*, *under-road*, *the road under roads*. Do not name Eclavdra, House Eilserv, or the Vault. Do not confirm that "E." is non-human.

- `packs/_source/phase-8-journals/journal-phase-8-endings.json` — six outcome packages, per-PC thread continuations.

You need to have decided six things before play:

- **Which opening branch (A–F)** you'll use, based on Phase 7's outcome. Default to A or B if you don't have a clean outcome.
- **Whether any captured NPC is with the party** (Haskur, Kethren, Korre) and what they will and won't do. Opening E covers the prisoner case; F the recently-escaped case.
- **Which descent tokens are still in play.** Cairn and Toll Vault are both options; if Phase 7 already produced a token, they may need fewer here.
- **How aggressively you'll advance the clocks.** Track them in front of the party — overt tension, not hidden mechanism.
- **Whether Kitty's chthonic-recognition or Elle's iron-key thread is live.** Either or both unlock private moments at the door.
- **Pacing.** One efficient session if the party reads the wall and opens the door cleanly; two if they investigate every location. Don't run a third.

## Location-by-location, in order

The party can investigate the surface in any order, but the threshold complex below the hearth is laid out for a clean front-to-back sweep. I've grouped the locations into six beats. Run them in this rough order.

### Beat 1 — Arrival at the Cairn (morning, day 1)

The party has camped upslope from the Seven-Cut Cairn through Phase 7's closing night. **Read the route-neutral opening read-aloud** (Arrival journal). The key beats: morning at the cairn is the same colour as paper, the road that brought them here ends at the cairn, *they have come to do what was done in 374 CY by a party with one fewer survivor.* If Elle is in the party, leave the 374 CY line in. If not, drop it.

Then **read the opening for whichever branch (A–F) you've chosen.** That establishes whether the cairn is intact or defaced; whether route-cleaners are present, arriving, or just left; the Lockdown Clock starting value (0 in A, 2 in B, 3 in C, 4 in D, varies in F); Structural Failure starts at 0.

Hand out **Handout 1 — Caelith's Threshold Note** late afternoon day 1, regardless of branch. Caelith's courier will wait three days at the dry ford; he has closed the party's office in Greyhawk; he warns them in three numbered points that *someone walks that road*, that *the door is paperwork, not power*, and that *the lintel says "Witness below" — go down to see, not to settle.* He cannot meet them at Tarnsmere. If they don't come back, he will speak their names in public. The note should land. It is the campaign telling the players that their anchor NPC is finite.

### Beat 2 — Surface Investigation (day 1, four to six hours)

Six surface locations, visitable in any order. Careful examination yields the credentials the door wants; force yields the same outcome at higher cost.

**Old Mining Spur Road** (Location 1). Narrow recent bootprints, narrow cart tracks, a flake of dark wax matching Aldea's recipe-family, iron survey nails on the verge — *someone has been quietly maintaining an officially abandoned road.* Survival DC 13 / Investigation DC 14 / History DC 15. Gianni's geographic-erasure thread fires here: *this is one of her four missing spurs.*

**Seven-Cut Cairn** (Location 2). Revisited from Phase 7. The cairn's east face aligns with the tollhouse hearth at sunrise (Survival/History DC 14). A loose wedge stone at the south face conceals a small crevice with a Tarnsmere descent token (**Handout 5**) — Investigation DC 13. *This is the cleanest path to opening the door.* If Selvara's stone-dream thread is active, the seventh cut is exactly her dream-image; tell her player privately. Hand out **Handout 2** on arrival.

**Collapsed Survey Station** (Location 3). Downslope. The party can recover a survey map fragment with a tunnel scratched out in iron-gall ink (Gianni: *Garrold Eft scratched this out*), a ledger fragment matching Phase 7's missing-surveyor records, and a half-burned scrap reading *"permission not passage."* Investigation DC 15 / DC 13.

**Ruined Tarnsmere Tollhouse** (Location 4). Main ruin and entrance to the threshold complex. Three details matter:

- **The toll window faces the wrong way** (Survival/Investigation DC 13). Set to watch arrivals *coming back up* the spur road. *The tollhouse was watching for descenders coming back.*
- **Hidden tally cuts** behind a vanished coat-rack — Investigation DC 14. Seventeen cuts; the bottom three recent. Each cut is one admission.
- **The hearth plate.** A 4-foot fitted-stone square in front of the hearth with a central recessed socket sized for a Tarnsmere descent token. Insert a token: plate sinks and slides aside, revealing the stairhouse. No token: thieves' tools DC 15 (clean), Strength DC 18 (loud — +1 Structural Failure, audible through the complex).

**Dry Well** (Location 5). Not a well — a 35-foot ventilation shaft with iron rungs. **Alternate entry** to the Cistern Drain and (by detour) the Old Guardroom. Athletics DC 14. Especially useful for halfling-sized scouting (Cam). Cleaners use this as an escape route.

**Tollhouse Yard** (Location 6). **Where surface confrontations happen.** Triggers scale with Lockdown: a silent scout on the ridge at 1+ (Passive Perception 14 to spot; letting him flee advances Lockdown +1); two route-cleaner agents burning registry scraps at 3+; and always one unrelated local passing through (a charcoal-burner, a shepherd's child looking for a lamb, a retired surveyor). Cover is limited; combat is *visible from the ridge*.

### Beat 3 — Subsurface Threshold Complex (day 1 evening into day 2)

A narrow stair drops 18 steps to a small landing with three exits — forward to the **Toll Vault** (8), left to the **Old Guardroom** (10), right to the **Broken Registry Chamber** (9). The **Cistern Drain** (11) skirts the eastern edge. The **Collapsed Side Passage** (12) branches off near the descent corridor and is a *dead end*.

**Hidden Stairhouse Entry** (7). The landing wall carries the same seven cuts and blackened-hill mark, sharper than the cairn. Scrap of grey-and-bone cloth — Kethren's uniform. **Pressure trap (Lockdown 3+)** on the third step: Dex DC 12 or 2d6 bludgeoning. Investigation DC 14 to spot, Thieves' Tools DC 15 to disarm.

**Toll Vault.** Token impressions in the dust — seven circular spots, six untouched for a century, one disturbed within the last two weeks. A ledger fragment in a crack: *"Permitted descent, third quarter 487 CY. Class VII tokens issued: 12. Returned: 3. Outstanding: 9."* A **second intact descent token** behind a wall-shelf, Investigation DC 15. Bronze casting-mold on a back workbench (smith's tools DC 17, 6–8 hours).

**Broken Registry Chamber.** The party's most important find of the surface phase. **Hand out Handout 3** on Investigation DC 13. The fragment carries six 518 CY admissions including *"Return: nil"* entries, the registry phrase *"No sail. No road. Toll paid beneath."*, the fee-waiver line, and a hurried note signed *"K."* (Korre).

Two entries read *"halfling cousin-of-record"* with names burned to partial — *"…mab Cain…"* and *"…ren of Hookwood…"*. **This is Cam's payoff.** He has been chasing Hesp Olfair's missing-list cousins since Phase 6; *these are them*. None have a return value. Don't editorialise; let him read the page. If Lockdown is 2+, cleaners have been burning packets (Arcana/Investigation DC 15 for heat-reactive ink residue). If Haskur was admitted in the last six months, his entry appears: *"H.V.A. — admitted, 11th of Patchett, second hour. Korre channel. Return: nil."*

**Old Guardroom.** Forward operating position for cleaners. Contents scale with Lockdown (empty at 0–1; dozing scout at 2–3; two agents + scout at 4–5; full team at 6). Regardless: a sharpened **dark fibrous stone sliver** in the hearth mortar (worked stone from below the Spur — Kethren dropped it), and a folded paper in Korre's hand: *"Three days. No traffic from above. Stand down at fifth bell."*

**Cistern Drain.** **The bypass past the Permission Door** (Method F) and the cleaners' escape. Knee-high cold water. Hazards: a water surge (Dex DC 14) and a foul air pocket at the south end (Survival DC 13 to notice, Con DC 13 or poisoned). A waxed packet caught against a stone holds a **third descent token** (Investigation DC 14).

**Collapsed Side Passage.** A dead-end warning, *not a route*. Past a fifty-year-old roof collapse: the body of **Garrold Eft**, Yeomanry surveyor, killed in the collapse in 542 CY. His satchel holds a survey map and a leather pocket journal — **Handout 6 — Surveyor's Warning**. Eft's last entry is one of the most important pieces of paper in the campaign:

> *"The site is not a tollhouse. It is a witness-post … The Aerdy did not make the descent. The Aerdy permitted the descent. They were not the first. They are not the last."*

He writes that he has heard footsteps on the lower stair *going up* twice in the last week, *turning aside before arriving at the landing*. And the Phase 8 mantra, scratched into the iron grate where he died: *"The stair admits. The shaft only swallows."* If Gianni's thread is active, a brass tag names him outright. *The campaign owes Eft's mother a letter.*

**Make sure the party reads Eft's journal before the door.** It is the GM's permission to let them feel small at the lintel.

### Beat 4 — The Permission Door (the load-bearing scene of Phase 8)

**Location 13.** Read the player-facing description from the Permission Door journal. The slab is seven feet by five, fitted flush with no visible joint. Seven shallow cuts in a row at chest height (leftmost slightly deeper). Below: the blackened-hill symbol, soot recently refreshed. Below that: a coin-sized recessed socket. Right: four lines of faint script. Left: six pale shapes the size of palm-sized stones. *Above the slab, in old Aerdy commercial script, four small words the party has to lean close to read:*

> **Accounts above. Witness below.**

The dust in those cuts is fresh. *Someone is maintaining this.* The four-line script on the right wall is the door's documentation:

> *No sail. No road.*  
> *Toll paid beneath.*  
> *Road fee waived for under-road permissions.*  
> *Show token. Speak condition. Lay tally. Witness given.*

**The door is paperwork, not magic.** The horror is that this is correct: someone wrote a procedure for descending into the dark, and the procedure works. It does not glow. It does not speak. It does not test the soul. The mechanism is a balanced-weight system — four internal counters released by token, phrase, tally, and witness; three of four lifts the slab silently into a lintel recess.

#### The six methods, plus Elle's seventh

| # | Method | What it requires | Clocks |
|---|---|---|---|
| A | **Token (Clean)** | Tarnsmere descent token in socket, registry phrase, six palm-sized stones at the base, witness response | None |
| B | **Registry (Skill)** | Investigation DC 15 for the registry phrase, History DC 15 for the substitution form *"Permission attested; account standing; toll deemed paid"*, plus phrase, tally, witness | None |
| C | **Kethren Channel** | Kethren alive, present, cooperating; palm to seventh cut + *"Channel passage. Courier and witness"* + her courier mark | None, but Kethren now has leverage |
| D | **Korre Papers** | Forged channel authorization from a Lead, the Guardroom, or Phase 7 trade; placed against the door + phrase + tally-substitution scrap + witness | Lockdown +1 unless the listening tube is disabled (Investigation DC 16, Thieves' Tools DC 15) |
| E | **Force** | Athletics DC 22 (with up to 3 Helpers), or pulverising the wall (10 minutes, loud), or battering ram; *Knock* releases one counter but doesn't open alone | Structural Failure +2, Lockdown +1, **stone-witness wakes** |
| F | **Bypass (Cistern Drain)** | Crawl the cistern shaft (Athletics DC 13, possible water surge, gas pocket Con DC 12); emerges in the Stair Gallery | None unless Structural Failure is already 2+ |
| Elle | **Iron Key** | Elle unscrews the brass-and-tin ring on her order's iron key; functionally a 484 CY token | None; *the door treats it as expected* |

Token is cleanest. Registry rewards parties that took notes. Kethren is fast and clean *but she now has a credible threat to hold over the party* — she has opened a Permissive Survey door on their behalf and is a named witness. Korre's Papers trip the listening tube unless they think of it. Force always works, always costs. Bypass feels like sneaking, not entering.

**Elle's key is the campaign's seventh-method reveal.** Across Phases 4–7, her player has carried an iron key her order received in 374 CY from a deceased Aerdy surveyor who joined them in late life. The key's brass-and-tin ring is the size and shape of a Tarnsmere descent token. When Elle approaches the door for the first time, **the ring warms against her skin** for the first time ever. Investigation DC 10 notices the threading; unscrewing yields a working 484 CY token. *Elle is the seventh of her order to carry the key. The previous six did not know what it opened.* Pause the action. Ask: *does she use it?* Either choice is character-defining.

**Kitty and the seventh cut.** If Kitty is in the party, the seventh cut warms distinctly when she comes within five feet. The threshold — not the door, the threshold the door regulates — has a recognition for certain patterns of descent. *This is not a chosen-one beat.* The threshold doesn't know her name; it knows the shape of her descent. If she places her hand on the cut while another PC runs the procedure, **one wrong-phrase attempt per encounter does not lock a counter**, and the slab rises faster. If she uses the option, the Final Threshold Landing's lintel will, after they pass, be cut with a small notch on its underside that no one in the party will see.

**Wrong attempts.** Each wrong token, phrase, tally, or witness phrasing locks a counter. If three lock, **only Force and Bypass remain.** Prompt Insight DC 13 to flag wrong-shaped phrases. Encourage rereading the wall. Target 15–35 minutes of real table time. By the time they are through, the party should feel two things: **quiet pride** (they solved a real puzzle) and **cold unease** (someone wrote a procedure for going beneath the world, and it works).

### Beat 5 — Descent through the Stair Gallery

**Location 14.** Beyond the door. About 80 feet of descending gallery, 8 feet wide, with low stone benches every 20 feet — *as if for resting porters or waiting parties of admitted descenders.* The first space where architecture stops looking surface-civic. Blocks join mortarless in unfamiliar patterns. **Black mineral seams** appear in the rock walls. Pillars are square above the floor line and **cylindrical at the base** — whoever built this changed styles at the floor.

**Surface sound dies.** Within ten paces the party notices: their footsteps echo, but echoes from above stop arriving.

Seven landings down. The seven-cut symbol appears repeatedly; *each instance more severe than the last*. Cuts deepen, blackening darkens, the hill silhouette becomes more abstract. By the gallery's end, the cuts have been worked **by hands working below, not above.**

Recent traces: a single set of bootprints descends and does not return; a dried blood-smear on the third landing; an ash smudge at the fifth; Kethren's narrow prints descending fresh.

**Alicia's thread fires here.** By the fourth landing, her pact-mark goes **completely cold** — not patron-is-displaced cold but *patron-is-not-reachable-at-all* cold. She is the first to notice that surface contracts are no longer in force.

If Lockdown is high, the Stair Gallery is the most likely place for **the final Phase 8 combat**. The fight is meaningful but not climactic; the climax was the door.

### Beat 6 — Final Threshold Landing

**Location 15.** The stair widens into a chamber 15 feet square, dressed in stone older than anything above. The far wall is the mouth of a wider passage continuing downward — wide enough for four to walk abreast, 12-foot ceiling. The air that comes up is *different* — not cold, just different, the way the air in a deep cellar is different from the room above.

Set into the lintel, in a script older than the Aerdy commercial hand the party has been reading all day: **Handout 7 — The Final Lintel Inscription.**

> *This place was opened in the year that the road was first paid for.*  
> *This place was already old.*  
> *It is not for those who arrive.*  
> *It is for those who come back.*

Read the **Closing Read-Aloud** from the Endings journal. Verbatim, the final passage:

> *Above you, the world keeps its accounts. Below, something has been keeping yours.*

Hand the Final Lintel handout to the table. Pause. Let it sit.

Three small finds at the landing itself: a folded paper near the lintel, half-burned at the corner, reading *"courier takes second page"*; a **waxed token on a thin silver chain** that someone wore through the door and unstrapped here (Kethren's or a Kethren-equivalent courier's marker of passage); a single black-and-grey fibre on the lintel's underside, head height. **This is the end of Phase 8.** Surface time begins to feel different past this lintel. The Threshold Lockdown and Structural Failure clocks **stop advancing**; they no longer apply below.

## NPCs to know in Phase 8

- **Caelith Dunivar.** Off-stage. The party's anchor NPC has reached the limit of his help. One threshold note (Handout 1), one courier waiting three days at the dry ford. *He is finite.* That should land.
- **Kethren Ilvath.** If alive and cooperative, she opens the door via Method C. If alive and uncooperative, she's the most likely fugitive on site. Her courier mark — a thin vertical stroke crossed at three-quarter height — appears on every registry fragment. She has been a route courier *longer than the Blooming Rot operation*.
- **Haskur Vandrell.** May be captured (Opening E), escaped (F), or absent. If captured, he refuses to descend awake — he was meant to be moved through *sleeping* and will die of seeing if forced across the threshold.
- **Veyra Korre.** Off-site through Phase 8. Her hand is on the registry's late margin, on Route-Cleaner Lead instructions (Handout 4), and on the Guardroom food packet. If captured at Phase 7's end, she begs not to be taken across — *"my account ended at the marker."*
- **Route-Cleaner Lead** (NEW). Korre's senior on-site supervisor. Mid-forties, clean hands, polished cudgel, discreet rapier, leather case of paperwork. Speaks Aerdy commercial-court formal register. **Will parley before fighting** — does not want a body on Tarnsmere ground that has to be explained. Captured alive, he trades information (door's listening-tube location, two senior cleaner names in Greyhawk, the route name *"the First Under-Road"*) for safe passage — *he has a family to retire to.* CR 2, HP 65, AC 15. Tell: he taps the brass clasp of his paperwork case; three taps means a runner is about to be dispatched.
- **Deep-Route Courier** (NEW). A specialised courier who walks the under-road. Reinforced boots, leather route-mask with smoked-quartz lenses (darkvision 60 ft when worn). Transient; will disengage rather than fight. CR 3, HP 58, AC 14. **Knows things about Phase 9 nobody else here does.** If captured and pressed, gives the road's name (*"the courier-road"*), an estimate (sixty miles east-southeast under the Cairn Hills before branching), the first junction (*"ECN/IV"*), and the standing advice: *"Greet stone politely. Do not name what passes you. Do not bring a torch on the third day."* If killed, the brass-clasped writing case contains a sealed letter — *if Lockdown is 4+, addressed to a recipient with the single initial "E."* — and route notes. *The handwriting on the envelope is plain. The seal is unmarked.*
- **Stone-Witness** (NEW). The construct that wakes only if the door is forced. Three feet tall, dressed-stone body matching the complex's masonry, a stone tablet face bearing the blackened-hill mark. **Mute. Does not attack first. It observes.** CR 2, HP 38, AC 16, blindsight 60 ft. Once per encounter, when a PC drops to 0 HP within 5 feet, it uses its reaction to **witness** — pressing its tablet to the PC's forehead. The PC is unhurt. *The construct is fulfilling its function.* The PC is now marked, invisible to mundane senses but detectable by *Detect Magic* at the Final Threshold Landing and below.
- **Garrold Eft.** Off-stage; long dead. The Yeomanry surveyor whose journal tells the party what the site really is. *His mother is still alive. Someone owes her a letter.*

Stat blocks for the three new actors live in `packs/_source/phase-8-actors/`. Returning enemies use Phase 7 sheets. Surface forces scale with Lockdown per the *Enemy Presence Sliders* page.

## How Phase 8 ends

The trigger is **the Final Threshold Landing**. The phase closes when the party steps onto Location 15, the closing read-aloud lands, Handout 7 hits the table, and the table sits with it. Phase 9 begins the next session.

Before you end the session, run the **per-PC thread continuations** from the Endings journal:

- **Cam** has the registry fragment in his coat pocket and Hesp's missing-list in his head. He files the names. He does not speak yet.
- **Gianni** takes the brass tag from Eft's satchel and tucks it under her belt. *She is going to bring it to the Aerdy clerks one day.*
- **Kitty** has her hand on the lintel without seeing the small mark cut on its underside after she passed. She closes her fist on what she did not feel.
- **Alicia** tests a small invocation; her pact-mark does not refuse — it *does not hear*. *She is going to walk down and find out who she is when her patron is not listening.*
- **Selvara** remembers her dream did not stop at the cairn. *She has been pointed down her entire life.* She is the first to walk down the passage.
- **Elle** holds the iron key. She will not put it back in the archive without first talking to her abbess.

For any PC without a tracked thread, offer a small closing beat — weighing a blade, feeling for a spellbook, a quiet line of order's prayer, one slow breath. *The air is different here. They walk down.*

## Branches & contingencies

The Endings journal lists **six outcome packages (A–F)**. Don't narrate the package name; internalise it and let it inform Phase 9's opening atmosphere.

- **A — Quiet Descent.** Clean methods, Lockdown 0–2, Structural Failure 0–1. The descent is invisible. Phase 9 can run as careful exploration.
- **B — Watched Descent.** Clean opening but Lockdown 3–4. The cleaners know. *The surface return will be the hard part.*
- **C — Forced Descent.** Method E used. The door is broken behind them. The site cannot be re-used cleanly. Phase 9 atmosphere sharpens: less *welcomed by paperwork*, more *shouldered your way in.*
- **D — Bypassed Descent.** Method F used. The door is still sealed. *No one knows the party is here.* Loneliest of the packages.
- **E — Lockdown Descent.** Lockdown 5–6. The site is being burned behind them. *They cannot come back the way they came.* Phase 9 must provide an alternate surface return.
- **F — Held at the Threshold.** Party chose not to descend. Phase 9 reframes as a deliberate expedition with full preparation. The threshold waits.

Worth pre-deciding:

- **The party never finds a token.** Method B, F, E still available. If Elle is present, her key is always there. No party reaches this door with literally no options.
- **The party kills the Lead before searching him.** Fine. Handout 4 is on his body.
- **Capturing the Deep-Route Courier alive.** Encourage it. They are the GM's most valuable Phase 9 prep NPC.
- **A PC drops in front of the stone-witness.** Run the *Witnessing* reaction. The PC is unhurt. Mark them on your notes.
- **The party wastes time on the Collapsed Side Passage.** Eft's iron-grate scratch and his journal redirect them. If they don't read the warning, let them dig fruitlessly for thirty minutes, then put Garrold's body in front of them.
- **A PC speaks "Witness given" thoughtlessly.** Eft warns against this in Handout 6. If they have read it and still speak the phrase, the door opens — but in Phase 9 that PC should carry a small private recognition. *They have witnessed something they have not yet seen.*
- **The party leaves a marker at the Final Threshold Landing.** Encourage it. Phase 9 may give it back to them in unexpected ways.

## Carry to Phase 9

Make a private note of the following — seeds that mature in Phase 9 (*"Below the Clean Paper"* — the First Under-Road) and beyond:

- **Which method opened the door** (A–F or Elle). Carries into Phase 9's opening atmosphere and surface-return options.
- **Final Lockdown Clock.** Whether the surface return is hostile.
- **Final Structural Failure Clock.** If it hit 6, the door is sealed from above; Phase 9 must find an alternate way back.
- **Kethren's status.** If she opened the door, she has leverage *and* the cleaners can use her against the party.
- **Was the listening tube disabled?** If not (and Method D used), an Aerdy clerk opens a quiet investigation in Phase 9 or 10.
- **Did Kitty touch the seventh cut?** The descent below has formally noted her shape of descent.
- **Did Elle use her key?** Her order's awareness is now in motion; a letter from her abbess may reach her via unexpected channels.
- **Did Cam read the registry fragment?** His halfling-network thread mobilises. Phase 9 opens with a strong morale push.
- **Did Gianni recover Eft's brass tag?** It becomes significant at the first under-road milestone.
- **Did Alicia descend the Stair Gallery?** She begins Phase 9 without easy recourse from her patron.
- **Did the Stone-Witness witness any PC?** That PC carries an invisible mark detectable below. The descent now knows their name as a record.
- **Did the party meet the Deep-Route Courier?** Captured: rich Phase 9 NPC. Killed: the sealed letter, possibly addressed to *"E."*, is one of the campaign's quietest tells about the mastermind layer. **Do not surface what "E." is.**

**Phase 9 in three words:** *Older. Wider. Witness.* Phase 8 was bureaucracy in stone. Phase 9 is the road the bureaucracy was a forgery of. The party will spend Phase 9 learning to read evidence that does not come on paper.

Above them, the world keeps its accounts. Below, something has been keeping theirs.

# Phase 4 — The Rel Astra Confrontation — Master GM Walkthrough

*A spoon-fed run guide. Read top to bottom; the order on this page is the order at the table.*

---

## What this phase is for

Phase 4 is the long climax of **Blooming Rot, Part 2**. The party arrives in Rel Astra carrying Veska Maelan's notarized opinion from Phase 3 — the document that names **Tarlith Vhal Sereth** — and walks into the city the conspiracy actually operates from. Phase 4 ends the **Aerdi-visible** thread of the conspiracy. It does not end the campaign-level architect. The phase has three jobs:

1. Show the players the **shape of the conspiracy in operation** — name the three commissioner candidates the conspiracy intends to seat on Rel Astra's harbor commission, identify which of the three is the weak link, and let the party draw a clean diagram of who pays whom for what.
2. Run the **Sereth confrontation** — the climactic political/social-combat scene of the main arc. The party has three viable resolutions: indictment in Aerdi commercial court (Path 1), private removal (Path 2), or kinetic removal (Path 3). All three are legitimate. Path 3 is the worst outcome.
3. Plant the **fourth and fifth observations of the "E." annotation hand** — without naming the writer, without resolving the species, without resolving the location. Players should leave Phase 4 with a question, not an answer.

Phase 4 is investigative and political; the only large combat is the Path-3 contingency, which you should hope you never run. **Investigation DCs cap at 18, social DCs at 17.** Party tier: **2–3 (levels 7–11).** Pressure Clock: **four segments**, faster and harder than Phase 3's.

---

## Before you run it

Read these in this order:

- `packs/_source/phase-4-journals/journal-phase-4-arrival.json` — the three arrival variants (sea / overland / diplomatic carriage), and Caelith's continuing Letters 4–6.
- `packs/_source/phase-4-journals/journal-phase-4-rel-astra-npcs.json` — **Therion Halask** (your gateway NPC), **Tarlith Vhal Sereth** (the named villain), **Belven Astor** (the party's civic ally), and **Lord Galenix Naelax** (the Aerdy claimant).
- `packs/_source/phase-4-journals/journal-phase-4-three-seats.json` — the harbor commission overview and the three candidate dossiers (Mirelth, Vesh, Tenrel).
- `packs/_source/phase-4-journals/journal-phase-4-sereth-confrontation.json` — the third-floor geography and the three resolution paths.
- `packs/_source/phase-4-journals/journal-phase-4-finale.json` — the Pressure Clock, the "E." thread instances, and the departure read-aloud.

You need to have decided five things before play:

- **Which arrival approach the party took** (Quiet Pursuit by sea / Commercial Cover by land / Official Delegation by carriage). This is set by their Phase 3 exit. It determines the read-aloud, the initial Pressure Clock state, and whether they have a pre-scheduled audience with Sereth.
- **Whether Halask survives the phase.** The default is yes. If the party has been visibly reckless and the Pressure Clock hits segment 3, killing Halask is the consequence — but it closes Path 1 permanently, so use it as a real threat, not a casual one.
- **Which path you expect at the Sereth confrontation.** All three are supported; Path 1 is the longest at the table but the most procedurally satisfying. Path 2 is the fastest. Path 3 should only happen if Paths 1 and 2 have been visibly closed off.
- **How much of the "E." thread to surface.** Aim for **two more observations beyond Veska's three** — instance 4 at the imperial registry, instance 5 either in Sereth's office (Path 2) or in a small late-phase registry detail. Do not name the writer. Do not let the writing be confirmed as non-human.
- **Whether to pursue the Galenix briefing.** Optional. The party may not even meet him. If they do, the cleanest outcome is to brief him honestly and let him repudiate the conspiracy on his own initiative.

## Scene-by-scene, in order

### Scene 1 — Arrival in Rel Astra (day 1 or day 5 or day 8)

Pick the read-aloud that matches the party's approach. All three are on the *Arrival* journal.

- **By sea (Lacquer Wave, Quiet Pursuit):** they arrive on the seventh morning at a working berth on the merchant quay. Captain Yorra Mel pays back half-fares without watching them leave. Harbor clerk takes their names, hands them silver return-tags, waves them up the quay. **The Goldsmiths' Quarter clock chimes the eleventh hour, and every clock in Rel Astra chimes with it.** The party arrives unobserved; they have about twelve hours before C&V Rel Astra is told the Lacquer Wave brought passengers from Hardby.
- **Overland (West Gate, Commercial Cover):** they crest the last ridge five days out and Rel Astra opens below them in the late-afternoon light. The Aerdi imperial gate-watch is fast and disinterested. The seventh-hour bell chimes as they step inside. They arrived clean; the conspiracy in Hardby knows they tried the road but does not yet know they made it.
- **Diplomatic carriage (Customs Quay, Official Delegation):** they arrive on the morning of the eighth day under formal accreditation. An Aerdi delegation has been waiting since dawn. The councilman steps down, bows are exchanged, and the clerks hand him a folded card listing the audiences arranged for the next nine days — including, **on the morning of the ninth day, a private interview with Tarlith Vhal Sereth at his offices.** They are then escorted to the **Hall of the Compass**, a three-story Aerdi guest residence on the Customs Quay, two streets from the harbor commission and three streets from Sereth's office. They are under observation by every faction in the city.

The three approaches set very different Pressure Clock starting points (see *Branches & contingencies* below). The diplomatic-carriage approach starts at **+2 immediate** — the conspiracy has had three weeks to prepare.

### Scene 2 — The Brass Sextant (first morning)

Within twenty-four hours of arrival, the party needs to find **Therion Halask** at the **Brass Sextant coffee-house on the third quay**. He keeps morning hours there between the seventh and ninth bells. He is mid-sixties, Aerdi, dark-skinned, white close-cropped hair, pale linen advocate's robe with the disused color sash of his old bench, reading lenses on a chain, a heavy silver advocate's-seal ring on his right hand.

The approach phrase from Caelith's letter of introduction is unique: ***"By the wheatsheaf and the open scroll."*** Halask will receive any party member who speaks it. He responds in formal Aerdi register; the elaborate honorifics ("*esteemed mistress of the bread, honored sir of the pastry*") are not affectation, they are how he speaks. **When he drops the elaboration entirely, the conversation has changed register.** Make sure the players notice this when it happens.

What he tells them in this first meeting:

- The harbor commission has **three vacant seats**. Two by death within the last eight months (one stroke, one drowning — the drowning was possibly assisted). One by a ceremonial "leave of absence" that has lasted four months and will not end.
- The three candidates being advanced are **Lord Faren Mirelth** (old-house reformer with private debts), **Magister Andrune Vesh** (respected technocrat with family in difficulty), and **Mistress Coriath Tenrel** (senior commercial advocate, ambitious, willingly aligned).
- The senior partner of Cindren & Vhal Rel Astra is **Tarlith Vhal Sereth**. Halask has met him three times — twice in court as opposing advocate, once at a funeral. He considers Sereth "*precise, patient, and personally cold.*"
- The party's only reliable civic ally is **Councilor Belven Astor** of the Old Harbor Ward Council. The other useful name is **Adra Sayan**, an old colleague of Halask's, who will give one signed statement and no more.
- The Aerdy claimant the conspiracy is positioning behind is **Lord Galenix Naelax**, currently under polite house-arrest at the Naelax embassy in the Old City. Sereth is one of his largest private creditors. **Galenix has never met Sereth in person.**

Halask takes custody of the indictment package once it's offered. After this point, **he will not let it leave his keeping.** This is non-negotiable. He also drafts the procedural framework for whatever path the party chooses, but he will not let them file in haste.

**Insight DC 14:** Halask is doing this carefully, not slowly. He is not delaying. He is sequencing.

### Scene 3 — The Three Seats (days 2–6)

The party's investigative spine for the middle of the phase. The harbor commission has seven seats, four currently sitting and three vacant. **The conspiracy does not need a majority — they need three commissioners voting together first, on the matters that come up first, before the rest of the commission notices.** That bloc plus Veshanen (already Sereth-aligned but not formally part of the conspiracy) delivers control of the **forty thousand gold pieces a year of discretionary harbor revenue**, the registry-certification process, and the dispute-arbitration calendar.

Walk the party through each candidate. Halask provides the dossiers.

#### Lord Faren Mirelth — The Reformer (the weak link)

Late forties. Public face: published reformist, "opaque berth allocation" pamphleteer, chairs civic transparency committees. **Private truth: bankrupt in fact since 1578, after the Mirelth shipping concern collapsed.** He has taken **twelve thousand gp in 1579 and another eight thousand in 1581** from C&V Rel Astra, structured as "*personal advances against future earnings*" — Aerdi commercial-code shorthand for *we own you*. Sereth has not yet called the loans. He has, instead, suggested Mirelth put himself forward for the eighth seat. Mirelth understood.

**Aerdi commercial code §187 (Conflict-of-Interest Disqualification):** no person may sit on the harbor commission while indebted to any counting house with active commercial business before the commission. The C&V loans are a textbook case. **Disqualification is automatic if the loans can be documented.**

Copies of the loan agreements are in **Solen Mereth's compromised correspondence cabinet at C&V Hardby** — the party may already have them from Phase 3 (Path 1 burglary or Path 3 subpoena). If they don't, Halask can compel a deposition from Solen by Aerdi commercial subpoena within ten days.

Three ways to handle Mirelth:

1. **Quiet conversation.** Approach him privately at home or at the Goldsmiths' Hall reading-room. Show him the loan documents. He withdraws his candidacy "*for personal reasons*" within an hour. The conspiracy has a weaker backup ready; delays the seating by at least two months. **Mirelth himself prefers this.**
2. **Public disqualification.** File documents with the commission's procedural clerk; commission votes 4-0 to reject; Mirelth's political career ends. Louder. Accelerates the conspiracy's response and may push them toward kinetic measures. **+1 segment on the Pressure Clock.**
3. **Hold in reserve.** Document but do not file. Use as leverage in the Sereth confrontation: *"agree to our terms or we file these on the morning of the vote."* Politically clever. The one Mirelth himself would choose.

#### Magister Andrune Vesh — The Technocrat (the leveraged honest man)

Late fifties. Author of the four-volume *Codex Mercatum* — the standard Aerdi commercial-procedure reference. Widely respected, reasonably honest, not himself corrupt. **The lever against him is his sister-in-law, Lyla Vesh, his late brother's widow.** Lyla faces twenty years' imperial prison for customs forgeries her late husband filed before his death. Sereth has implied — through intermediaries, never directly — that C&V has private acquaintance with the imperial advocate prosecuting Lyla, and that this acquaintance could result in the case being dropped on a technicality if Vesh accepts the seat. Vesh has not formally agreed; he has, however, accepted the candidacy.

**Strictly speaking, nothing disqualifies Vesh under §187.** The customs case is not a counting-house matter. The party can only persuade him to withdraw by **removing the pressure.**

Halask, given two weeks and roughly **800 gp in filing fees and expert honoraria**, can resolve Lyla's customs case independently — the imperial advocate's case rests on a presumption of spousal knowledge that Halask can demolish on Aerdi precedent grounds. If the party covers the cost, Vesh withdraws cleanly and Halask remains fully committed to the Sereth indictment. If they ask Halask to cover from his own funds, he will, but **he will be reluctant to commit further resources to the indictment** — and that affects Path 1 viability.

#### Mistress Coriath Tenrel — The Advocate (the willing one)

Early forties. Sharp Aerdi commercial-court advocate, specialist in registry-and-chop disputes, married to a senior Goldsmiths' Hall functionary, lives in an expensive house in the high quarter. **She approached Sereth eighteen months ago with a proposal:** a sympathetic vote for ten years in exchange for the firm's support of her ambitions for the senior commissioner's seat when Aldaen Veth retires. Sereth accepted.

Tenrel is not bribed, not pressured. The party cannot remove her by removing pressure (there is none), cannot disqualify her under §187 (she has been careful), cannot persuade her to withdraw (she will not). **The only way to stop Tenrel is to stop the seating itself** — either by indicting Sereth (which suspends the candidate slate pending investigation) or by briefing the imperial seat (which shifts Captain Volin Reach from abstention to opposition; 2-2 with Magister Eled Ruth's procedural irritation in play, and the vote fails).

Halask has noted two procedural vulnerabilities in Tenrel's commercial-court practice that could be used later, but **pursuing them is not Phase 4 work.** Document them and let them rest.

#### How seating actually works

Currently four commissioners sit: **Aldaen Veth** (Senior, sixty-eight, openly skeptical but politically isolated; will vote no on all three), **Hold Veshanen** (Commerce, Sereth-aligned, votes yes on all three), **Eled Ruth** (Civic, independent and irritable, votes no on the first one out of procedural objection — which the conspiracy *wants*), and **Captain Volin Reach** (Imperial, votes the imperial line, which means abstention on discretionary matters).

The conspiracy's plan: bring all three candidates to a vote on the same day with a ceremonial imperial-herald visit scheduled the following day. Veshanen yes, Reach abstain, Ruth no on the first (Mirelth, held over for re-vote later — exactly the conspiracy's outcome), then **Vesh and Tenrel pass 2-1 with Veth alone in dissent.** Old Harbor Ward Council confirms; Astor and possibly Sayan vote against; the other five councilors vote with the commission majority. **Vesh and Tenrel are seated.**

Three lines of attack, **not exclusive**:

1. **Disqualify Mirelth** (above). Delays but does not stop.
2. **Indict Sereth** (next scene). Suspends the slate pending investigation. Stops the seating in the current cycle.
3. **Brief Captain Reach** through Aerdi imperial intelligence (Halask knows the channel). Shifts the imperial seat from abstention to opposition. The vote fails 2-2. **+1 segment on the Pressure Clock** because the embassy leaks the visit within a day.

**Disqualify Mirelth, indict Sereth, and brief the imperial seat — taken together, the conspiracy's harbor-commission play collapses for at least two years.** Encourage the party to do all three.

### Scene 4 — Belven Astor's Chandlery (day 2 or 3)

The Wave-and-Anchor chandlery on the second quay, open during business hours. Walk in, request a private word with the proprietor, he receives the party in his small office above the shop. Mid-fifties, former master mariner, iron-grey beard, weathered hands, small enameled wave-and-anchor pin of a sitting councilor.

Astor will:

- Provide signed statements about the unusual character of the vacancies and the candidates' weaknesses.
- Introduce the party privately to the four uncompromised council members (only **Adra Sayan** will agree to give a statement).
- Brief the party on the Aerdi commercial court's filing procedures and timing constraints.
- Coordinate the post-confrontation aftermath: if Sereth is indicted, Astor moves to suspend candidate selection on the council floor; if Sereth is privately removed, Astor lets the matter rest; **if Sereth is killed, Astor will not be seen with the party again.**

He responds well to professional courtesy and to evidence presented in proper order. He responds badly to threats, bluster, and any suggestion that the council is corrupt *as a body*. **Phrase it that two or three of the seven are compromised, and he will agree.** The councilors he warns the party away from are **Solvard Mein** (the senior councilor, openly Sereth-aligned) and two others.

He will not house the party. The chandlery is too visible.

### Scene 5 — The Imperial Registry (mid-phase — instance 4 of the "E." hand)

If the party investigates the harbor commission's vacancy paperwork through the imperial registry — Halask can arrange access; the registrar is one of his old commercial-court contacts — they find that the leave-of-absence vacancy (the third one, Tenrel's) was created by a single ceremonial document filed by the harbor commission's senior clerk eighteen months ago.

The document itself is unremarkable. The marginal annotation, **in a hand the registrar has never seen before**, reads:

> *"Sufficient. Hold open until the second autumn after the next."*

The hand matches Veska's three Phase-3 observations and adds two more:

- **Fourth observation:** the writer holds the quill in a way that produces uniformly thick downstrokes — implying either a blunted nib used deliberately, or a hand that does not pivot the quill the way Aerdy and Yeomanry hands do. Halask, asked, will recall that *some Suel hands write this way, but only certain very old families.*
- **Fifth observation:** the wax used to seal the original cover of the document is **not Aerdi imperial wax** (beeswax-and-pine-resin tinted with cinnabar). It is a darker, harder wax that left slight black residue on the paper around the seal. Halask cannot identify it. He has seen one similar wax in his career — on a 1571 document filed in Aerdi commercial court by an envoy who claimed to represent "*a southern trading concern*" that no one ever found again.

**Drip-feed.** Do not dwell on this. Let the players notice, ask questions, and walk away with a question they cannot resolve. *Players should leave Phase 4 with a question, not an answer.*

### Scene 6 — The Galenix Audience (optional, days 4–6)

Halask can arrange a private audience at the Naelax embassy, no record kept, no public connection. **The audience advances the Pressure Clock by +1** (the embassy leaks the visit within a day).

Galenix is mid-thirties, House Naelax cousin to the current Overking, tall, fair-skinned for an Aerdy, the Naelax house ring on his left hand and a smaller signet on his right. Soft-spoken; reads constantly; has not been seen drunk in seven years. He has been the de facto Naelax voice in Rel Astra for ten years and is currently under polite house-arrest by a four-house Aerdi coalition.

Critical facts about his relationship to the conspiracy:

- He has **never met Sereth, never met Castrian Vell, never met Solen Mereth, never met Anver Resh, never met anyone the party has named.**
- He owes Cindren & Vhal **approximately 38,000 gp** in private debt, accumulated over six years of embassy operations on inadequate Naelax funding. Some he knows about; some he believes were taken on his behalf by his now-deceased predecessor.
- He believes the debt will be called when his political fortunes turn. **He does not yet understand that the debt has already been called** — it is being held against him as the price of conspiracy support, and the conspiracy expects to be repaid in eastern harbor revenue once the iron crown is on a Naelax head.
- He is, by Aerdy noble standards, an honest man. He believes — incorrectly — that the firm has been a quiet friend to House Naelax.

Three options the party may choose from, none of them wrong:

1. **Brief him honestly** with the indictment package as backing. Galenix privately repudiates the conspiracy and refuses the harbor commission's votes when seated. Cleanest political outcome. **It also makes Galenix a target.**
2. **Leave him alone.** The trial proceeds without his knowledge. Cleaner for the party, harder for Galenix.
3. **Use him.** Hand him the indictment package as a gift; he consolidates his political standing by exposing the conspiracy, presents himself to the four-house coalition as the noble who broke it, and **negotiates an end to his house arrest in exchange for the cooperation of his testimony.** Politically aggressive. Makes him a friend and a debtor of the party for years. Accelerates whatever the conspiracy does next.

### Scene 7 — The Sereth Confrontation (the load-bearing scene of Phase 4)

This is the climactic scene of the main arc. **The room Sereth meets the party in is itself the message.**

#### The building

Four-story brownstone-fronted commercial building on the Goldsmiths' Quarter side of the Old Harbor Ward, two streets from the harbor commission. Brass plate: **CINDREN & VHAL — REL ASTRA OFFICES — EST. 1462 CY**. Two liveried attendants by day; one armed guard at night.

- **Ground floor:** public counting hall, six teller cages.
- **Mezzanine:** clerk bullpen, chop-and-seal workroom.
- **Second floor:** junior partners' offices, records library, formal reception salon, partner dining.
- **Third floor:** Sereth's three rooms — formal audience room, private working office, private study.
- **Strongroom (basement):** three vault chambers behind two doors requiring both Sereth's key and the senior junior partner's key. **Original Mirelth loan documents are here**, along with the firm's internal correspondence dating to 1572 CY. Untouchable without court warrant or suicidal burglary.

#### Which room he picks

- **Formal Audience Room.** Long oak table, twelve chairs, two bodyguards against the south wall. The room he uses for formal delegations. Polite distance.
- **Private Working Office.** Heavy desk facing south, away from the windows so the light falls on the visitor and not on him. Two visitor chairs. A wall of locked correspondence drawers. **This is where he meets people he is dealing with one-to-one. If he meets the party here, the party should understand that they are being treated as a serious problem rather than as a delegation.**
- **Private Study.** Small. A single reading chair by a small fire. A carafe of Aerdi-coastal red wine and a single glass. A volume of the *Codex Mercatum* always open on the chair's arm. **Sereth meets nobody here.** If the party penetrates this room, they have committed a serious breach of Aerdi commercial-court etiquette and Sereth will treat them, from then on, as outlaws.

For a serious party arriving with a real indictment package, **he meets them in the Working Office.** Make the choice visible.

#### Sereth himself

Late fifties. Tall, narrow-shouldered, very thin. Dark Aerdi complexion, hair gone silver-white and worn long tied back. Clipped silver beard. Dark eyes that move slowly and miss nothing. Dark-grey Aerdi advocate's overcoat (admitted to commercial court in 1556, has never represented a client at trial, wears the coat as a credential not a profession). Heavy seal-ring on his right hand: not a personal device — the firm's seal. **He smiles only with his mouth.**

He uses the legal-formal register even in private conversation. He never raises his voice. He never threatens directly. **The threat is always in what he does not say.** Do not let the players bully him into reacting; he is too disciplined to react. Let him deflect, refuse, and outwait them.

#### Path 1 — Indictment in Aerdi Commercial Court

The procedurally clean path. **Halask drives this.** The party provides the package, supports the depositions, stays out of the way. Slow, public, certain. The conspiracy's hardest outcome to recover from.

Prerequisites: the Phase 3 indictment package (Veska's notarized opinion, the C&V Hardby branch records, the Sereth blotter rubbing, the chipped Hardby clearing-stamp), Halask's procedural framework, at least two corroborating depositions (Solen Mereth compelled by subpoena and one other — Vella Tannin, Hesren Vesh, or in extremis Mira Cindren), and the Mirelth disqualification documented.

Timeline:

1. **Days 1–4 — preparation.** Halask works in his rooms, asks the party for one specific document per day. The party readies witnesses.
2. **Day 5 — filing.** Halask submits the formal indictment to the Aerdi Commercial Court at the seventh bell of morning. By the eleventh bell, the court has docketed it. By dusk, the firm's senior staff are reading copies. **+1 Pressure Clock segment.**
3. **Days 6–10 — preliminary motions.** Sereth's defense files three motions to dismiss. Magister Velren Ostrach (sitting; Halask knows him as honest and dislikes Sereth) denies all three.
4. **Days 11–28 — depositions.** Solen Mereth, Vella Tannin (if available), Hesren Vesh (if available), and one C&V Rel Astra junior partner. **This is when the party is most exposed to retaliation. Run two of the four Pressure Clock segments during this window.**
5. **Day 29 — preliminary hearing.** Ostrach accepts the indictment for trial. Sereth's bail is set at **50,000 gp** (posted within the hour). **The harbor commission suspends candidate selection pending the trial's resolution.**
6. **Days 30–60 — trial preparation.** The party need not stay. Halask carries the trial.
7. **Conviction (off-screen, ninety days after departure).** Sereth convicted on conspiracy-to-defraud and procedural-fraud counts. Sentence: removal from the firm, forfeiture of personal estates, **twenty-year exclusion from any commercial office in Aerdi territory.** Not death. Not imprisonment. Aerdi commercial court does not execute.

What this costs: sixty days plus thirty days of trial. Pressure Clock will reach segment 3 or 4 during the deposition window. Witness loss is likely. About **1,200 gp** in procedural costs.

What this buys: harbor-commission play collapses for at least two years. Sereth permanently removed. Pattern documented in court record. Caelith's standing partially restored (the Civic Reconciliation Closure motion is, by inference, exposed as a mistake; Brane loses standing).

#### Path 2 — Private Removal

The fast path. The party uses the indictment package as **leverage rather than as evidence.** They confront Sereth in his offices, present the consequences of refusal, accept his quiet resignation in exchange for not filing. Lower cost to the party, higher cost to the long-term suppression.

Sample demands:

1. **Resignation from the firm within thirty days, departure from Rel Astra within sixty days.** He accepts without resistance — it is his planned retirement accelerated by ten years.
2. **Withdrawal of all three commissioner candidates.** He accepts Mirelth's withdrawal (he has the documents anyway) and Vesh's (he understands Halask is handling Lyla independently). **He refuses Tenrel's** — Tenrel is not his to withdraw, and pretending she is would expose how thin his actual control is.
3. **The original Mirelth loan documents.** He provides these.
4. **The names of his Hardby correspondents beyond Solen Mereth and Tarsh.** He provides one (a junior C&V Hardby partner he uses for routine document handling) and not the second name the party probably suspects exists.
5. **A written private statement that the firm's harbor-commission positioning was "a personal initiative of the senior partner, not firm policy."** He signs this. It is a lie. The firm wrote the policy. The lie protects the firm and, in exchange, Sereth gets his quiet exit.
6. **The annotation hand.** If asked specifically about the "E." marginalia, Sereth says — and this constitutes **instance 5 of the "E." thread** — verbatim:

> *"The annotations are not from this side of the great desert. I have not met the writer. I do not know whether the writer is a person, an office, or a guild. I have, perhaps once a year, received a note in that hand, and the firm has acted on what was in the note. The notes were always correct. I do not ask further."*

This is the truth. He really does not know. He has, however, just confirmed for the party that **the writer exists, the writer is foreign by some significant margin, and the firm follows the writer's direction without question.**

What he will not concede: his own firm shares, the firm's other operations, any statement that exposes the conspiracy's continuing existence, any cooperation in court should the party ever decide to file the indictment after all.

**How the conversation should run at the table.** Sereth is not a beggar. He has lost a hand he did not expect to lose, but he still has a great deal of game left. He should not appear panicked. He should not appear desperate. He should appear, throughout, exactly what he is: a senior commercial operator handling an unexpected procedural problem at a level appropriate to its difficulty.

**If the party tries to humiliate him, he does not become angry. He becomes less generous.** The party loses concessions for every needless escalation. If the party offers him dignity in exchange for cooperation, he provides more — though he never provides everything.

What Path 2 costs: the conspiracy is paused, not stopped. The firm reorganizes within twelve months under a new senior partner; the harbor-commission play resumes, more carefully, within twenty-four months. Sereth retires comfortably. **Caelith would not approve. Some PCs may not approve.**

What Path 2 buys: speed (the whole confrontation resolves in a single long evening), the "E." admission (genuinely useful, not available on Path 1), Mirelth and Vesh withdrawn (Tenrel still in play but isolated), and **witness preservation** — Solen, Vella, Hesren, and the others are not exposed to post-deposition retaliation.

#### Path 3 — Kinetic Removal

The path of last resort. **Available only when procedural and political paths have visibly closed.** Don't recommend it. Make sure the party has refused or exhausted Paths 1 and 2 before they ask to take it.

Three conditions justify it: Halask is killed or compromised (procedural path closed); Sereth has the package and has destroyed the corroborating depositions; or Sereth refuses both indictment and resignation and has political cover to ride out a public scandal (Ostrach replaced, sudden Aerdi imperial shift).

The encounter at Sereth's offices:

- **Tarlith Vhal Sereth** — CR 5, AC 13, HP 65, **non-combatant; yields at half HP and not within reach of his guards. Never makes a killing blow himself.** Hand Crossbow (+4, 1d6+1) and Concealed Dagger (+4, 1d4+1) as last resorts only. Cunning Action. *Reader of Rooms* — advantage on Insight and Investigation against any creature whose intentions he has read for at least one round.
- **Two Bodyguards (Veteran)** — CR 3 each. Will not retreat unless Sereth orders.
- **Bodyguard Captain (Knight, Defense fighting style)** — CR 3. Prioritizes Sereth's safety over offense.
- **Driver (Bandit Captain)** — CR 2. Back service entrance. Engages if alerted.
- **Two Junior Partners (treat as Acolyte with one *shield* casting)** — present during business hours; will not fight; flee and raise alarm.

**Calibration:** a level-9 party of six wins this. A level-9 party of four struggles and expects at least one PC down. A level-7 party should not attempt it.

Building features: light cover (desks, doors), multiple stairwell choke points, back service stair as the bodyguards' fallback. The basement strongroom is the only defensible position.

Aftermath:

- **Aerdi watch responds within ten minutes.** The party has a narrow window to recover documents (Sereth's body has one strongroom key; the senior junior partner has the second) but cannot stay long. Recommend pre-planned exit route.
- **The firm reorganizes within forty-eight hours** under a successor; the strongroom is emptied and sensitive correspondence shifts to a Rauxes deposit. **The documentary trail goes cold.**
- **The harbor-commission play is paused for about six months**, then resumes with tighter security and possibly a different slate.
- **The party is named in Aerdi imperial bulletins** as wanted for the murder of an Aerdi commercial officer. **Halask cannot represent them; they are advised to leave Rel Astra by sea within twelve hours of the killing.**
- **Caelith will not write again until the party is back in Loftwick.** When he does, he writes measured: *"You did what you decided to do. I will not pretend I would have chosen as you did. I will also not pretend the world is worse for Sereth's removal. The harm is to the procedural ground we needed to stand on. We will rebuild it. Come home."*

What this buys (very little): Sereth dead. A narrow window at the strongroom that may, with a good Investigation roll, surface **two further instances of the "E." annotation hand.** A demonstration that the line the party would not let be crossed was respected.

**This is the worst Phase 4 outcome. Treat it that way.**

### Scene 8 — Caelith's Letters (delivered through the phase)

Three letters reach the party in Rel Astra by separate couriers, two to three days behind the news that prompted them. All on the *Caelith's Continuing Letters (4–6)* page.

- **Letter 4 — within three days of arrival.** Two things. First, **Trina has sent a sealed copy of Veska's notarial opinion** by separate courier to a person she names only as "*my old correspondent in the wood.*" Caelith does not know who. He has not asked. The opinion will, by some path, find at least one set of eyes that no Aerdi or Yeomanry court will ever recognize as having read it. Second, **Brane's Civic Reconciliation Closure motion passed in the Council on the seventh day after the party sailed.** The Vandrell matter is, in Loftwick civic record, declared resolved. Caelith is writing on his own paper. *Anything the party accomplishes in Rel Astra now stands on Aerdi court, not on any letter Caelith can write.*
- **Letter 5 — about a week in.** *"The clerk in my office is named Ostren Pell. I have known this for nineteen days. I have done nothing about it."* Caelith is keeping Pell in place. The lesson he draws for the party: ***"Sometimes the people who know are the ones who appear to be doing nothing."*** Apply that rule to Halask, to the council members, to anyone at the harbor commission who has done a small public courtesy. Also: a man fitting Haskur Vandrell's description was seen in Greyhawk City three weeks ago, in the company of a younger man whose gait was "*not Aerdi and not Yeomanry.*"
- **Letter 6 — toward the end of Phase 4.** *"The conspiracy you have named is not the conspiracy. The conspiracy is positioned to take advantage of the next Aerdy succession."* Whoever drew the plan drew it ten years ago. **The annotation Veska could not place — the party will, in Rel Astra, find a second and possibly a third instance.** Notice the small details. *Do not pursue the writer.* The writer is not in Rel Astra. *"I do not know who the writer is. I know what a hand looks like that has not spent its life under Aerdi or Yeomanry sun, and I have seen exactly one person in twenty years whose hand looked like that, and she would not write on Rel Astran vellum."*

Read Letter 6 at the table. Slowly. **It is the line the players need to remember**, and one of them will say it back to you later: *"The conspiracy you have named is not the conspiracy."*

### Scene 9 — Departure (the ending read-aloud)

Read aloud at the moment the party leaves Rel Astra. Adjust small details based on the path taken. The journal text is route-and-resolution-agnostic; key lines:

> *Rel Astra in your last hour does not change. The harbor's clock chimes the seventh bell of morning and every other clock in the city chimes with it, the way Hardby's never quite did. The Aerdi sun is high already. The breeze off the Sea of Gearnat carries the same lemon-and-dust-and-clean-salt smell that the city carried on your first morning, and will carry on every morning until the seas swallow it.*
>
> *You are leaving. You are leaving with what you came to take, or what you could take, or what was left to be taken after the part you wanted had already been moved.*

The middle paragraph adjusts to outcome: a sealed Aerdi Commercial Court filing under Halask's procedural seal (Path 1), a folded private statement Sereth signed in his working office (Path 2), or a hand-written list of items recovered from the strongroom in the four minutes before the Aerdi watch arrived (Path 3). **The shape is different in each case. The weight is the same.**

Then: *"You also carry a question. The annotation hand. Five observations, none of them sufficient, all of them recurring."*

Closing line — and this is the only line the players need to remember:

> *"The conspiracy you have named is not the conspiracy."*

End the read-aloud with: ***"You ride west."***

**Phase 4 closes the moment the party crosses the city walls outbound.** Phase 5 (Loftwick counterintelligence) begins with their arrival back at the Yeomanry capital.

## Branches & contingencies

**The Pressure Clock has four segments. Show it to the players. When you advance, say which trigger fired and what the consequence is.**

- **Arrival under Official Delegation:** **+2 immediate.** The conspiracy has had three weeks to prepare.
- **Arrival under Commercial Cover:** **+1.** The conspiracy assumes the party is in the city and prepares for it.
- **Arrival under Quiet Pursuit:** +0. The party is unobserved on arrival.
- **Filing an indictment with the Aerdi Commercial Court (Path 1, day 5):** +1.
- **Publicly disqualifying Mirelth (option 2):** +1.
- **Briefing Galenix at the Naelax embassy:** +1 (the embassy leaks within a day).
- **Visiting Sereth's offices openly more than twice without an audience scheduled:** +1.
- **Killing any senior C&V Rel Astra staff member:** +2 immediate.
- **Each full session day with no investigative progress:** +1.

**Segment milestones:**

1. **Polite obstruction.** Records take days instead of hours. Audiences rescheduled. Halask's filings mis-docketed and require re-submission. Costs time. Time advances the clock further.
2. **Witness pressure.** Mirelth leaves Rel Astra "for personal reasons" for his country estate — disqualification documents must be served by Aerdi rural courier (ten days). Lyla's bond doubled, case moved up on the imperial advocate's calendar. One of Halask's old colleagues declines to provide an expected affidavit.
3. **Witness loss.** GM picks based on what hurts most: **Astor's chandlery broken into and partially burned** (records lost; Old Harbor Ward Council route closes); **Lyla's advocate "reassigned" to Rauxes** (Vesh's withdrawal becomes contingent on a new advocate); **Sayan found drowned** (Halask is shaken; the procedural path slows by another week); or — in extremis, only if the party has been visibly reckless — **Halask himself is found dead at the Brass Sextant.** That last one closes Path 1 entirely. Path 2 becomes the only remaining political option.
4. **The seating proceeds.** The harbor commission convenes the candidate vote on the conspiracy's schedule. Vesh and Tenrel seated 3-1. Mirelth held over (Ruth's procedural objection works for the conspiracy's benefit, not the party's). **The campaign's central stakes have been lost; Phase 4's resolution becomes a question of damage control rather than victory.** The party can still indict Sereth, but the seating cannot be undone for at least two years.

Other branches:

- **Sereth refuses Path 2 and the indictment package is solid.** Halask files. The party is on Path 1 whether they wanted to be or not.
- **The party tries to enter Sereth's private study.** Sereth treats them as outlaws from that point forward. Path 2 closes; Path 1 becomes much harder; Path 3 escalates.
- **The party kills a junior C&V partner.** +2 Pressure Clock immediate. Sereth's defense files an additional motion. Magister Ostrach is still honest but is now under public scrutiny; his rulings tighten.
- **The party tries to burglarize the C&V strongroom.** They do not survive in good political standing. Halask will say so before they try; if they ignore him, Path 1 closes the moment they're caught (or the moment the firm reorganizes its document storage in response, which happens within forty-eight hours of any unauthorized entry).
- **Galenix is briefed and the embassy leaks.** Galenix becomes a target. Within seven days, a discreet poisoning attempt is made (the chamberlain catches it; Galenix survives; the four-house coalition tightens his restrictions). The party may feel responsible. Caelith, in his next letter, points out that *Galenix was already a target; the party only changed the timing.*
- **Halask is killed in segment 3.** Path 1 closes. The party can still pursue Path 2 directly. Astor cannot replace Halask procedurally but can provide moral and civic support. Caelith, told of the death by post, will write back grieving but composed: *"He chose this. So did I."*

## Carry to Phase 5

Make a private note of the following — these are seeds that mature in Phase 5 (Loftwick counterintelligence) and beyond:

- **Which Sereth path the party took.** Path 1 leaves Caelith's standing partially restored and Brane's faction weakened. Path 2 leaves Caelith uneasy and the firm reorganizing. Path 3 leaves Caelith measured-but-disappointed and the party flagged in Aerdi commercial circles.
- **Whether Mirelth was disqualified quietly, publicly, or held in reserve.** Public disqualification rebounds in Phase 5 — Brane's faction tries to spin it as Yeomanry overreach.
- **Whether Halask resolved Lyla Vesh's case.** If yes, Halask is a permanent Rel Astra ally and Vesh becomes a quiet posthumous-favor debt. If no, Vesh sits and the conspiracy keeps one seat.
- **What Galenix knows.** Briefed honestly, he is an ally. Unbriefed, he is the same tool the conspiracy intended. Used, he is the party's debtor for years.
- **The "E." observations.** Five on file. The writer is unnamed. *Do not name the writer.* The Drow thread does not surface in Phase 5 either. Phase 5 is Loftwick counterintelligence (Ostren Pell is the leak; Caelith has known for nineteen days when Phase 4 begins). The "E." thread continues into the expansion phases.
- **The line from Letter 6.** *"The conspiracy you have named is not the conspiracy."* If a player quotes it back at you in Phase 5, smile and let it land. That is the campaign's central hinge, and they have it.
- **Trina's sealed copy of Veska's opinion.** Trina sent it to "*my old correspondent in the wood.*" Caelith has not asked. Do not surface this in Phase 5. It matures much later, if at all.
- **Whether Sereth said the "great desert" line.** Path 2 only. If he did, the party has explicit verbal confirmation that the writer is *not from this side of the great desert.* They have not been told what is on the other side. They have not been told there is a "great desert" in literal-cartographic terms; the phrase may be metaphorical. Hold the ambiguity.
- **Haskur Vandrell, last seen in Greyhawk City.** From Letter 5. He is alive, on the move with a younger man whose gait is "not Aerdi and not Yeomanry." Plant this. It matures in the expansion phases — possibly as a Part 3 antagonist, possibly just as the operative who survived this round.

Phase 5 begins with the party's arrival back at the Little Palace in Loftwick, with Caelith waiting in the Refectory and a fresh complication on his desk. Until then: *you ride west.*

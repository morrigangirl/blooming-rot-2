# Phase 5 — The Small Matter — Master GM Walkthrough

*A spoon-fed run guide. Read top to bottom; the order on this page is the order at the table.*

---

## What this phase is for

Phase 5 is the campaign's quiet final act — the counterintelligence aftermath. The party returns from Rel Astra carrying whatever Phase 4 produced (Sereth indicted, retired, or killed). The city they come back to is not the city that welcomed them in Phase 1. Brane's **Civic Reconciliation Closure motion** has passed 4–3 and is in its mandatory fourteen-day review period. Caelith's institutional standing is gone. The brass quill pin he wore in Phase 1 is off his lapel. The phase has four jobs:

1. **Show what changed.** The Audit Hall has rescinded Caelith's clerical support; the Council is nine days from voting his office closed.
2. **Resolve the leak in Caelith's office.** His correspondence clerk **Ostren Pell** has been forwarding sanitized monthly summaries to the conspiracy for two years. Caelith has known for three weeks and has kept Pell in place to trace the chain upward — and because the man who pays Pell is holding Pell's older sister **Sera**. Caelith hands the party the choice of what to do about Pell: five real options, one unmentioned sixth.
3. **Deliver Aldea's reply.** Trina's old correspondent in the wood — half-elven retired paleographer **Aldea Veren** — has analyzed the marginalia samples. Three observations land in the debrief: wax from no surface recipe; a hand court-trained but not court-raised; a writer geographically inaccessible to any sailing port when at least one annotation was made. The "E." thread crystallizes without ever being named.
4. **Get the party committed to a Phase 6 vector.** Four on offer: Greyhawk City (Haskur's trail), the wood correspondent, the Counter-Leak operation (Vesten Quill in Loftwick), and — Path A only — the Rel Astra legal continuation.

Administrative horror, not combat. **DCs cap at 18 investigation, 17 social.** Single optional combat: Vesten Quill (CR 2, +1–3 Thugs). Party tier: **2–3 (levels 8–11).**

The phase is also the only place in the main arc where **Wick Lane surfaces as a recruitment pattern** — Sera's shop is on the same lane where Phase 2's dead bonds clerk Merro Gilst lived above a stationer's. Player-facing surfacing is inference-only; see Carry.

## Scene-by-scene, in order

### Scene 1 — Return to the Little Palace (Day 1, seventh bell)

**Read the Opening read-aloud** (Arrival journal). Key beats: Civic Way thinner than it should be at the seventh bell, Audit Hall doors already shut, West Gate militia unfamiliar and too young. Steward **Elsbet Vael** meets them in the vestibule, clasps the senior PC's hand a beat longer than civic protocol allows, says quietly: *"He is in the office. He has been waiting since noon."* The corridor militia do not speak.

The Civic Office door is barred. Elsbet knocks twice, pauses, knocks once more — a pattern the party does not remember. The bar lifts. **Caelith Dunivar** is at the desk, correspondence cabinet locked behind him. **The brass quill pin is gone from his lapel.** He stands and says:

> *"You did the work. You came back. The small matter is this: we are now inside the part of the conspiracy that survives exposure."*

*"Sit. There has been news."* **No checks.** Let the players notice the pin without naming it.

### Scene 2 — The Framing Speech

Caelith lays four small papers on the desk — one per moving piece — and **reads the Framing Speech aloud**. Verbatim:

> *"Four pieces. I will lay them out the way I would have laid them out for you a year ago, when I had a desk and a pin and a clerical staff to support me. I do not have those now. The desk I have is this one. Bear with me.*
>
> *"The first piece. Brane's Closure motion has passed. The Audit Hall has rescinded my title pending a fourteen-day review period that ends on the morning of the ninth day from today. After that, the Council may declare the Blooming Rot matter resolved. If they do — and Brane has the votes, four to three — my correspondence becomes Council property. Anything I have written you, anything you have written me, is read. Anything you tell me from this evening forward must therefore be told here, in this room, with the door barred. I will not write it down.*
>
> *"The second piece. There is a leak in my office. His name is Ostren Pell. I have known for three weeks. I am keeping him in place because his removal would tell whoever pays him that I know, and because the man who pays him is holding his sister. I would like you to understand that this is not a decision I am proud of. It is the only decision I had to make.*
>
> *"The third piece. Trina's old correspondent in the wood — the woman she would not name to me until two days ago — has answered. The packet is sealed and I have not opened it. I will open it tomorrow, with you in the room.*
>
> *"The fourth piece. Haskur Vandrell has been seen in Greyhawk City. A man whose oath I have known for fifteen years filed an affidavit five days ago: Haskur, in worn travel clothes, at the door of a counting-house called the Brass Crow, in conversation with a younger man whose gait — my contact's word — was 'not Aerdi and not Yeomanry.' The two of them entered the counting-house together. My contact did not follow.*
>
> *"That is the small matter."*

He looks at each of them in turn:

> *"Sereth was not the hand. He was a glove. The hand is older than him, and it is older than this office, and — if Trina's correspondent reads the marginalia the way I think she will — it has been writing on Aerdi paper for at least a decade longer than I have been on the case. It has not stopped. It has rearranged. We are inside the part of the conspiracy that survives exposure, and the only currency we have left is what it does not yet know we know."*

*"Sleep here tonight. The Steward will see to dinner. We will work in the morning."*

**Do not let Caelith answer detailed questions tonight.** Redirect: *"Tomorrow." "In the morning, with the papers in your hands." "The answer is in the packet, which I will not open without you."* He will answer one question — why he is keeping Pell in place: Sera's name, written in the second envelope and nothing else. If asked who Trina's correspondent is: *"A woman who reads hands. Trina has trusted her for many years. I have not met her."*

### Scene 3 — The Debrief in the Strong Room (Day 2, second bell after dawn)

Strong Room — same room they read Haskur's gear in during Phase 1. Three sets of papers on linen squares: the party's Rel Astra evidence, Caelith's files, the still-sealed Trina packet with its woodland-grey wax. Three movements, 30–60 minutes.

**First movement.** The party lays out their evidence in their own order, in their own words. Caelith listens. Takes notes (Handout 1, the Debrief Memorandum). Does not interrupt.

**Second movement — Caelith's six questions** (in sequence; "I don't know" is a valid answer):

1. **Did Sereth fear his superior?** *(Truth: not visibly, but on reflection yes — he behaved like a man whose retirement had already been bought.)*
2. **Did Sereth understand the whole design, or only his piece?** *(A/C unclear; B all but said he did not.)*
3. **Did the Harbor Commission play feel local, imperial, or foreign?** *(Local and imperial in execution, but the timing only makes sense if a foreign actor was driving the calendar. Praise this privately with a single nod if the party reaches it.)*
4. **Did anyone in Rel Astra behave as if they expected you?** *(Yes on A, partially on B/C.)*
5. *(Path A)* **What did the Magister Ostrach hearing reveal that surprised you?** *(Caelith is fishing for whether the defense filed motions Halask anticipated — meaning the defense is being run by someone who has read his prior cases.)*
6. *(Path B)* **When Sereth said "not from this side of the great desert," what was his face doing?** *(Either reading tells Caelith something.)*

**Third movement — synthesis and the packet.** Caelith states it plainly:

> *"Sereth was not the hand. He was a glove. The hand is not in Rel Astra. The hand has not been in Rel Astra in at least a decade and possibly longer. The conspiracy you have named is the part of the hand that touches paper we can read. There is a part of it we do not yet know how to read. Trina sent me a woman who can — possibly — read it. Let us see."*

He breaks the woodland-grey seal, reads silently, then aloud. **Pass Handouts 1 and 2 now.** Aldea's three observations:

- **The wax.** *"This wax was made somewhere I have not seen, by a process I have not read."* Matches none of her Aerdy, Rel Astran, Keoish, or Yeomanry recipes; harder, higher beeswax-to-resin ratio than any surface manufactory she has documented.
- **The hand.** *"This is a hand trained by reading court scripts, not by being taught them. The teacher and the student were not in the same room."* Surface letter forms; wrong stroke order — ascenders begin with a downward push and correct on the upstroke.
- **The geographic impossibility.** The earliest dated marginalia (Hardby branch, Lent 1574 CY) is on paper milled in Rel Astra autumn 1573 and sealed with that wax. *"There is no path by which the writer could have been present in any of those ports during the four-month window when the annotation was placed."*

Closing: *"I have written down what I read. I have not written down what I think. I will not write down what I think. If you wish my speculation, send the party to me in person."*

**What the players know now:** not Aerdy or Yeomanry; court-trained but not court-raised; geographically inaccessible to any sailing port when at least one annotation was placed. **What they do not know:** name, species, current location, organization.

(GM-only — Aldea's private speculation: the writer **is not human** and learned the surface hand from documents that came up to them rather than from a desk on the surface. She will say this only in her workshop, only if asked correctly. Right question: *"Could the writer have learned this hand from someone who has never been to the surface?"* Wrong way — *"Is the writer a drow?"* — she deflects: *"I would not know how to answer that. Ask me what I can read, not what you guess."* The campaign does not name Eclavdra, House Eilserv, the Vault, or the Underdark in Phase 5.)

### Scene 4 — The Five Choices for Pell

Caelith presents the five choices and **does not direct the party**:

**Choice 1 — Expose publicly.** Pell dismissed within a day, censured, stipend and pension gone. Sera permanently safe. *Cons:* the conspiracy switches couriers and recruits a new clerk in a different office within months; party loses tactical use; Caelith is publicly seen to have employed a leak for three weeks. **Caelith:** *"If you choose this, I will not argue. I will help Sera find employment outside the city."*

**Choice 2 — Turn Pell.** Confront privately, offer Sera protection, recruit him as a knowing double agent. Real intelligence flow, operational control over what the conspiracy reads, Sera lives. **Sera must be visibly protected** for the rest of the phase — two PCs within earshot whenever she is on the street, credible second exit through the alley, Trina's courier playing the customer. Handler may attempt a snap-extraction. Pell may flip back. **Caelith:** *"It requires you to actually protect Sera — not promise to. If you cannot commit to that, choose Choice 3 instead."*

**Choice 3 — Feed false information.** Leave him in place; feed him a lie from the False-Packet Menu (next scene). He forwards not knowing; conspiracy reacts within 3–7 days. Sera at baseline risk. Pell remains compromised by design; party morally implicated in using a coerced man. **Caelith:** *"This is the choice I am most likely to make. I am telling you that because I want you to be aware of what I am willing to do, and to choose for yourselves."*

**Choice 4 — Shadow the courier chain.** Do not approach Pell at all. Tail his courier from the dead-drop forward to identify the next layer (Vesten Quill). Pell never knows; channel safe from him. Counter-surveillance risk — clumsy tailing shifts the chain and burns the trail. **Caelith:** *"If you can do this without being seen, this is the best of the four. If you cannot, fall back to Choice 3."*

**Choice 5 — Remove from office.** Reassign him quietly. **Sera remains exposed** — the handler reads the reassignment as Pell flinching and may move against her. Channel closes; no further use. **Worst political outcome unless paired with extraction of Sera, which the party must arrange themselves.** **Caelith:** *"I will do this if you ask. I will tell you it is the worst of the five unless you have a plan for Sera. Do you have a plan for Sera?"*

**The unmentioned sixth — kill Pell.** Caelith does not propose it. Pell does not provoke it. Serious moral and political failure unless extreme circumstances justify. If unjustified: Caelith refuses further cooperation, Letter 7 not delivered, clock **+2 immediately**, Sera dies unless the party extracts her without Caelith, Trina reduces cooperation for the rest of the campaign without condemning them. *The system answers the choice; it does not endorse it.*

### Scene 5 — The False-Packet Menu (if Choice 3, or paired with 2)

Caelith provides Handout 3 and writes the actual packet himself. Five lies, in summary:

| Lie | Reaction (days) | Consequence | Best pair |
|---|---|---|---|
| **1. "Haskur is in Rel Astra."** | 3–4 | Watchers redirect east; Greyhawk lightly observed 6–9 days; Iren Velash safe to approach openly; Vone's irregular ledger at 50 gp. | Vector A |
| **2. "Party pursuing the wood correspondent."** | 4–5 | **Aldea endangered.** Segment 4 fires hard; Aldea vanishes for the phase. Party gains evidence of Stark Mounds conspiracy presence. **Burns Vector B.** | Vector C |
| **3. "No usable evidence from Sereth."** | 5–7 | Conspiracy de-prioritizes the party. Aerdi witnesses easier (Path A); Sereth's estate relaxes (Path B). | Vector D |
| **4. "Party exposing Galenix Naelax."** | 3–5 | Naelax embassy briefly active. Niche. **If the party briefed Galenix in Phase 4, this is a betrayal** — Caelith warns explicitly. | Niche only |
| **5. "Party returning to Hardby."** | 4–6 | Watchers redirect west; Loftwick observation thins for 4–6 days. | Vector C |

Multiple lies compound the Pressure Clock risk.

### Scene 6 — The Courier Chain (Days 3–7, optional, Choices 2/3/4)

Stealth/Investigation/Persuasion crawl over three to five days; PCs split, Sera-protection duty in parallel if Pell was turned. Five locations:

1. **Civic copy room.** **Dead-drop brick: third from the bottom, fourth from the left**, in a wall not recoursed in fifteen years. Investigation DC 13 with knowledge; DC 16 to date last access; Stealth DC 14 day / DC 11 night. Yields the pickup schedule and a faint pipe-smoke signature.
2. **Cooper's lane.** Courier **Trell** (mid-thirties, brown beard, plain dark cloak, slight forward lean, pipe-smoker) crosses twice a week. Stealth DC 13; Investigation DC 14 to ID; Perception DC 15 to notice he checks before crossing. Yields his name and uphill destination.
3. **Civic courier desk.** Run by **Pell Garven** (*no relation* — Pell is a common Loftwick surname). Persuasion DC 15 with credible evidence; Insight DC 13 confirms she is honest and unknowing. Yields Trell's pattern (Tuesday/Friday mornings, seventh-to-eighth bells) and the Quill Street address.
4. **Rented room above the printer's, Quill Street.** Printer **Ven Sallis** sleeps in a back room and does not lock the alley door. Upstairs is rented to *"V. Quill"*. Stealth DC 14; Investigation DC 15 (coded notes Caelith deciphers in 1d4 days, naming **a Hardby-aligned letter agency operating out of a Greyhawk City address**); Sleight of Hand DC 13 for the cabinet. Yields Quill's evening pattern (eleventh-to-first bells).
5. **Hall of Bonds drop.** Sealed lead pipe behind a south-wall buttress; cap disguised as drainage. Investigation DC 16; Sleight of Hand DC 14; Stealth DC 12. **Yields Handout 6 — the courier receipt** (date, page count, single *"V."*).

**Optional combat — Vesten Quill, Quill Street, eleventh-to-first bells.** The *only* Phase 5 combat. Alone if not warned; one Thug if warned; a second Thug from a rooftop on round 3 (whistle on round 1 if he can). **Quill, CR 2 Bandit Captain analog.** AC 15, HP 65; scimitar + dagger or two scimitar; hand crossbow; Parry reaction. **Surrenders at half HP (33);** names the Hardby-aligned letter agency / Greyhawk City address; does not know the layer above; accepts Yeomanry custody and requests the Council.

**Marshal Thale's whistle response** is reinforcement contingency: three militia in three minutes, subdue rather than kill, prisoners to the city barracks. Party attends a brief debrief next morning.

### Scene 7 — Sera protection (Days 2–9, if Pell was turned)

Sera is 41, sharp-eyed, runs a dressmaker's shop on **Wick Lane**, raised Ostren after their parents died, **unaware of any of this**. From Letter 7 Variant C: a second alley exit, two PCs within earshot at all times she is on the street, a credible reason for any unknown visitor, Trina's courier playing the customer. Failure: +1 clock if approached but not taken; +2 if taken. Do not move on Quill before Day 8.

### Scene 8 — The Political Front (Days 2–8, parallel)

Three NPCs to outmaneuver before the Day 9 vote:

- **Councilman Ortwell Brane** — sponsor, *not corrupt*, not E.'s asset. Honest belief the Yeomanry cannot afford the inquiry. *He is right enough.* Cannot be bought, threatened, or shamed. **Can be outmaneuvered.** Will not resign, apologize, or move against Caelith personally.
- **Councilor Edda Halvern** — co-sponsor; widow; former southern-district militia paymaster. Motive is the 8,000 gp Audit Hall foreign-inquiry budget (she wants 5,000 gp for the southern militia, 3,000 gp to general civic). **Does not know her co-sponsorship benefits a foreign conspiracy.** **Movable** by securing 5,000 gp for the southern militia from a non-Audit-Hall source: Trina contributes 3,000 gp if Caelith asks and the party has met her in person; party adds the rest. **The deal must be presented by Caelith or Voss, not by the party directly** — funds must go to the militia paymaster's account with documentation, not to Halvern personally.
- **Councilor Mairra Voss** — sympathetic former trade-clearing notary; voted against the motion 4–3. **Will procedurally sponsor a renewed vote** with three days' notice on a clean documentary case. Strongest evidence: (a) the courier receipt (Handout 6); (b) Quill's confession; (c) Aldea's reply plus the Greyhawk affidavit; (d) Caelith's personal testimony (politically expensive to him).

**Marshal Rennic Thale** — Council-instructed to "be aware" of party movements (loose on Paths A/B, firm on Path C). Will not act without orders, will not refuse one. Approachable directly with a reasonable explanation; quiet support means he briefs his night-watch to look the other way on named alleys and answer a whistle in three minutes. Not for sale, not afraid.

### Scene 9 — The Pressure Clock (Day 1 onward, visible)

Show the players the six-segment clock at the Day 2 debrief. Advance visibly. Name the trigger.

1. **Day 2:** Closure motion enters public record; Pell's pickup signal arrives earlier than usual.
2. **Day 3:** Pell passes a packet (real or false). Handler reacts.
3. **Day 5:** **A false witness statement appears in a Loftwick civic broadside**, claiming the party fabricated the Sereth evidence. Harsher on Path C (murder for personal gain). Caelith can refute privately, not institutionally.
4. **Day 7:** Trina's correspondent endangered or silent. **Hard fire on Lie 2:** Aldea vanishes; the dog is gone, the owl on the roof, a note pinned to the workshop door. **Soft fire otherwise:** a watcher near the Stark Mounds road, no contact.
5. **Day 8:** Haskur's Greyhawk trail begins to cool. **Tellan Verth is reassigned south;** the Brass Crow changes its courier schedule. 48–72 hours to commit to Vector A.
6. **Day 9:** Binding vote. **Either result ends Phase 5.**

Triggers (+1 unless noted): failing to open the debrief within 24 hours; visiting Aldea without Lie 2 (observed only); visiting Aldea after Lie 2 (**+2**); killing Pell unjustifiably (**+2**); engaging Brane publicly without first moving Halvern; failing Sera protection (+1 approached / +2 taken); tipping Quill via clumsy surveillance; moving on Quill before Day 8; publicly contradicting the broadside without Caelith; leaving Loftwick before Day 5; each day with no investigative progress.

### Scene 10 — The Day 9 Vote (climax)

Segment 6 fires at the seventh bell regardless. Two outcomes:

- **Motion fails 3–4** (Halvern moved, Voss sponsoring with the receipt). Caelith retains nominal standing; Brane moves privately for a procedural recess; matter held over 90 days. The brass quill pin may arrive by courier in Phase 6.
- **Motion passes 4–3.** Caelith's office is formally closed; correspondence becomes Council property; he goes silent. The work continues through private channels only.

If segment 6 fires before the party has chosen a Phase 6 vector, **Caelith asks them in Council, in front of the Council, what they intend.** They commit publicly. Phase 6 starts the next morning.

### Scene 11 — Letter 7 and the ending read-aloud

Caelith hands the party **Letter 7** folded and sealed on the morning they leave (or in Council on Day 9). Variants:

- **A — Greyhawk City.** Do not approach the Brass Crow on the first day; visit manuscript dealer **Iren Velash** with the phrase *"the seventh question of the third volume"*; write via the Hardby post, not Domain.
- **B — Wood Correspondent.** The road in is not patrolled but is observed; bring **originals**, not copies.
- **C — Counter-Leak.** Do not move on Quill before the morning of the eighth day; Sera is your responsibility; take Quill quietly.
- **D — Rel Astra Legal Continuation (Path A only).** Halask needs corroborating depositions (Solen Mereth, the Hardby junior partner, Vella Tannin if extracted); Astor will not be visible in person.

*"Read this on the road, not before. The seal is good for a day's ride. After that I would prefer the contents to be in your saddlebag rather than on this desk."*

**Read the Ending Read-Aloud.** Caelith at his desk, four documents in a careful row — the Greyhawk affidavit; Trina's packet refolded with the wax pressed back into shape; Pell's most recent leak opportunity (or, if Quill was taken, the receipt); and — on Path A — the Aerdi commercial court schedule. The brass quill pin is still missing. He looks up:

> *"This is the small matter. We have more doors than hands. Choose your door. Whatever you do not carry through, I will carry as best I can. The work continues either way; the question is which face of it you intend to look at next. I will not write you often in the next phase. What I write will be brief. Use the seal you saw on the workbench, and write me from the post-houses I have named for you. Travel safely. Come home alive."*

He hands them the letter. The four documents stay on the desk. The fifth bell is just beginning to ring.

## NPCs to know in Phase 5

- **Caelith Dunivar.** Same Caelith as Phase 1 — Hand of the Duke, no longer publicly so. The pin is gone until the Day 9 vote decides. Will not direct the party on Pell. Will not write anything he is not willing to have read. **Will not fight.**
- **Trina Alvere.** Largely off-stage. Provided the introduction to Aldea; lends a courier to play the customer at Sera's shop; will contribute 3,000 gp privately to the Halvern side deal if Caelith asks and the party has met her in person.
- **Ostren Pell.** The leak. Early thirties, prematurely receding hair, ink-stained fingertips, brown clerk's coat with the (still-unrecalled) Audit Hall pin. Recruited at 28 by an outside party paying off a 3,000 gp gambling debt his late father left him; locked in by a second envelope containing only Sera's name in the same hand. Tried twice to find a way out: stopped once (a third envelope arrived with Sera's address below her name); tried to confess to Caelith once (lost his nerve at the office door). Knows the dead-drop, the courier signal (a cold-stove rag — grey woolen cloth, not a scrap — hung over Sera's back step before dawn on pickup mornings), and the courier's face. **Does not know Vesten Quill's name and has never seen him.** Calls his contact only *"the man on the lane."* CR 0. *"His first instinct is to confess everything in one continuous breath; his second instinct, three sentences in, is to begin negotiating for Sera. Both are real. Let players decide which they trust."*
- **Sera Pell.** Forty-one, sharp-eyed dressmaker on **Wick Lane**, raised Ostren after their parents died, **unaware of any of this**. The operational stake for any Pell choice that leaves him in place.
- **Vesten Quill.** Handler above Trell. Late thirties, lean, sharp-faced, soft-spoken. Rents above Ven Sallis's printer's on Quill Street under cover of a quiet northern lawyer. CR 2. Knows the next layer (Hardby-aligned letter agency / Greyhawk City address); does **not** know the layer above. **Pell has never heard his name.**
- **Trell.** Courier between Pell and Quill. Tuesday and Friday mornings between the seventh and eighth bells. Never meets Quill directly; the lead pipe behind the Hall of Bonds is the interface.
- **Pell Garven.** *No relation* to Ostren. Honest civic-courier-desk clerk.
- **Ven Sallis.** Honest old printer. Believes V. Quill is a quiet northern lawyer.
- **Mistress Aldea Veren.** Trina's wood correspondent. Half-elven retired paleographer; lives in a stone cottage in the Stark Mounds foothills (day's careful ride west + half day south on an unmarked track). Brindle dog, half-tame fox, two goats, a great horned owl on the workshop roof. Fey-tinge environmental; **never name it, never let Aldea explain it.** **Whatever she says in person, she will not write down.**
- **Councilman Ortwell Brane.** *Not corrupt.* Mid-fifties, bronze wheatsheaf pin. *He is right enough.* Cannot be bought, threatened, or shamed.
- **Councilor Edda Halvern.** Mid-sixties widow, former militia paymaster. Movable through a credible non-Audit-Hall source for the 5,000 gp militia line — through Caelith or Voss, not the party.
- **Councilor Mairra Voss.** Late fifties, former trade-clearing notary. Procedural ally. *"Players who treat her as a pure ally will lose her; players who treat her as a procedural specialist will earn her."*
- **Marshal Rennic Thale.** CR 4 — the only Phase 5 stat block you'll roll for if a fight happens.
- **Elsbet Vael.** Steward, still. The double-knock-pause-knock is hers.
- **Tellan Verth.** Off-stage; retired militia sergeant in Greyhawk's Foreign Quarter whose affidavit reported Haskur at the Brass Crow. Reassigned south on Day 8.
- **Iren Velash.** Off-stage; manuscript dealer in Greyhawk's Garden Quarter. Met only on Vector A.
- **Edrik Vone.** Off-stage; former imperial customs official running a private courier archive near the High Quay. Corruptible — 50 gp regular ledger, 200 gp irregular. Caelith does not approve; he will not stop the party.
- **Tellis Maro.** Off-stage; Cindren-aligned junior advocate planting discrediting rumors against Halask (Vector D).

Stat blocks live in `packs/_source/phase-5-actors/`.

## How Phase 5 ends

The trigger is **the Day 9 Council vote**. The phase closes on the morning the party leaves Loftwick for their chosen vector (Letter 7 in hand, the four documents on Caelith's desk), or — if segment 6 fires before they have committed — in the Council chamber itself, the moment Caelith asks them in front of the Council what they intend and they answer.

Phase 6 begins **on the road to whichever vector they chose**. Letter 7 is sealed for one day's ride. Caelith's correspondence reaches them by separate courier; brief, infrequent.

- **A:** road north to Greyhawk; first scene the Foreign Quarter at evening.
- **B:** road west to the Stark Mounds; first scene the foothills road at dusk on day two.
- **C:** the morning after the vote, in the Civic Office. Guarded by militia (vote held) or empty (vote passed).
- **D (Path A only):** aboard a Hardby coastal galley to Rel Astra, fourteen days after departure.

## Branches & contingencies

- **Party kills Pell unjustifiably.** Caelith refuses further cooperation. Letter 7 not delivered. Clock +2. Sera dies unless the party extracts her alone. Trina reduces cooperation for the rest of the campaign.
- **Party visits Aldea after playing Lie 2.** Segment 4 hard. Aldea gone, note pinned to the workshop door (*"Going to my sister's for a season. The samples you sent are in the press, sealed. Take them. — A."*), samples intact, dog gone, owl on the roof. Investigation DC 14 in the valley reveals reconnaissance tracks to a foothills-road inn called **the Stark's Foot**; one dropped leather tube once containing a burned watch-orders sheet in a Hardby-aligned letter agency hand.
- **Party tips Quill via clumsy surveillance.** Clock +1. Quill leaves the room early; encounter fires with the second Thug present from round 1 and Quill alert.
- **Party moves on Quill before Day 8.** Clock +1. Handler reschedules; the Hall of Bonds receipt may be empty.
- **Party publicly contradicts the Day 5 broadside without Caelith's coordination.** Clock +1.
- **Party engages Brane publicly without first moving Halvern.** Clock +1. Brane closes off.
- **Party departs for Greyhawk before Day 5.** Clock +1. The trail benefits but Loftwick tightens; Vector C becomes much harder.
- **Party bribes or threatens Thale.** He refuses and may report to Brane. He is not for sale and not afraid.
- **A PC tries to lift the quill pin from the desk to give it back.** It is not on the desk. It is in his coat pocket. He is waiting.
- **Path C party tries Vector D.** Closed — Halask has politely distanced himself. May reopen in Phase 6.
- **Sera is approached but not taken.** Clock +1. The party gets one chance to intercept before the snatch.
- **Sera is taken.** Clock +2. 48-hour window before she becomes a Phase 6 problem.
- **Motion passes 4–3.** Caelith goes silent. Phase 6 runs through private channels only. The pin does not come back.
- **Motion fails 3–4.** Matter held over 90 days. The pin may arrive by courier in Phase 6.

## Carry to expansion content (Phases 6+)

Make a private note of:

- **Which vector the party chose.** This is where Phase 6 opens.
- **What the party did about Pell.** Each of the five outcomes shapes Phase 6's Loftwick differently. Exposed: Pell gone, channel closed permanently, Caelith politically damaged. Turned: Pell still feeding the party's strategic input, Sera under continuing protection. Fed: Pell still in place, conspiracy still working off the lies for 5–14 days into Phase 6. Shadowed: Pell never approached, Quill identified, channel cooler but live. Removed: Pell reassigned, Sera at active risk, channel closed under the worst political conditions.
- **Whether Quill was taken alive.** If yes: deposition on file at Thale's barracks, slow intelligence trickle from interrogation. If escaped: conspiracy reroutes through a slower path; the party knows they tried.
- **Which lies were fed.** The conspiracy believes them for 5–14 days into Phase 6.
- **Whether the Closure motion passed.** Phase 6 Loftwick plays differently with Caelith silenced vs. nominally standing.
- **Whether the party met Aldea in person and asked the right narrow question.** If yes, they carry — *in their heads, not on paper* — the GM-only suspicion that the writer is not human and learned from documents that came up to them rather than from any surface desk. Do not over-confirm.
- **Whether Halvern was moved cleanly.** Phase 6 may need to refresh the militia line in 90 days. A clean deal (Trina + party contribution through Caelith or Voss) is durable; a clumsy one re-opens the southern-district question.
- **Whether the party owes Marshal Thale a favor.** Continued discretion in Phase 6 depends on consistent stories.
- **Whether Vector D was pursued (Path A only).** If yes, Halask is on the party's side for Phase 6 Aerdi work; the trial verdict lands at Phase 6 mid-point.
- **Whether the party noticed the Wick Lane parallel.** Phase 5 is the only place in the main arc where the recruitment pattern surfaces explicitly — Pell's two rooms above Sera's dressmaker's shop on Wick Lane, the same lane where Phase 2's dead bonds clerk **Merro Gilst** lived above a stationer's. Pell's *"man on the lane"* alias for his contact mirrors how Gilst was almost certainly recruited four-plus years earlier. **The journal does not give the players a handout that says "same lane,"** and the repo does not back-date Quill to Gilst's recruitment — that connection is reader inference. If your players ask whether it is the same lane, confirm. If they do not ask, hold it as a Phase 6/7 reveal.

The headline open question carried into Phases 6–9 is the identity of **"E."** Phase 5 does not name them; Aldea will not write down what she thinks; the Greyhawk handler **V. Korre** is a pseudonym; the Hardby-aligned letter agency operating out of a Greyhawk City address is an intermediary, not the conspiracy itself. Phase 5 does not name Eclavdra, House Eilserv, the Vault, the Underdark, or confirm the writer is non-human. **Phase 6 may. Phase 7 may not need to.** The campaign continues. The conspiracy continues. The hand has not been named, and Phase 5 did not name it.

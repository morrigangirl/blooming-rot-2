## What this phase is for

Phase 2 is the morning after Phase 1's closing envelope and the two or three days that follow. The party is still under the Little Palace's roof; Caelith now opens the cellar and his own title to them. Daylight re-examination of Haskur's gear turns up three names — **Cindren & Vhal**, **Gilst**, **Moraven** — and the work of the phase is to chase those names while the conspiracy's two arms (a polite deputy with money, a professional thief with smoke pellets) close around the party. The phase has three jobs:

1. Confirm that **Haskur survived by design, not luck** — and show that the operation underwriting him is institution-scale, not a guild operation or a noble cousin's vendetta.
2. Put **Cindren & Vhal, Bonded Factors** of Rel Astra at the center of the conspiracy on paper — surface that the firm's Loftwick reach has killed a clerk (Gilst) and made a grain factor (Moraven) disappear, but not yet die.
3. Force the party to **leave Loftwick under one of three travel approaches** — Authority, Cover, or Distance — before the conspiracy's slow institutional closure (or its fast criminal response) makes the choice for them.

Phase 2 is investigative and political, with one short combat possible in the Strong Room. **DCs cap at 18.** Party tier: **2 (levels 5–10).**

You need to have decided three things before play:

- **Which Trina/Alicia mentor beats you will use, if any.** Ration to one per session.

The best identification anyone offers is *"elven, but peculiar."* The "E." is a letter on a page; that is the whole reveal here. The forged Audit Hall seal recovered from Sarth leads to Ostren Pell in Phase 5 — **do not name Pell** either.

## Scene-by-scene, in order

### Scene 1 — The Morning Evidence Review (Refectory M4, second bell after dawn, day 3)

The party comes down to find the seven items laid out on numbered linen squares on the long oak table. **Trina Alvere** is already there with a cup of pale tea. She is introduced by first name only. **Read the Opening read-aloud** (Evidence journal → *Opening Read-Aloud — The Morning Review*). The beats: gray morning light through leaded windows; bells outside; the woman with the silver leaf at her collar who does not stand; Caelith's two sentences — *"The man you fought was almost certainly Haskur Vandrell. The bad news is that Haskur Vandrell has been dead before."* — and Trina's introduction:

> *"Good morning. I am Trina. I advise Caelith on people. I am sorry about the envelope at breakfast. I expect we will get to it last."*

No checks. Mood and introduction.

### Scene 2 — The Hand of the Duke Speech (immediately after)

Caelith places a brass token on the workbench — a quill above a closed gate — and **reads the Hand-of-the-Duke speech verbatim** (Evidence journal → *Caelith's Expanded Explanation*). Four beats you must hit:

1. **The title.** Caelith is the Hand of the Duke; the Yeomanry maintains no formal record of the title; it is the part of his office that goes outside Yeomanry borders.
2. **The pattern.** Haskur was pulled back from death twice before, in 575 (Onnwal coast) and 580 (Hardby tavern). Each time the line went dead.
3. **The scale.** Underwriting a man twice in nine years costs more than a guild or a noble cousin has. The money is institutional.
4. **The choice, restated, with Trina as witness.** Take the eastern commission and travel one of three ways, or refuse and be paid in full.

The token stays on the workbench through the morning (Passive Perception 14 to notice it is being shown deliberately). Trina says nothing; if Caelith looks at her at the end, she nods once. **Insight DC 14** reads his hesitation about naming a clerk in his own office; he will accept help finding the leaker but will not steer.

### Scene 3 — The Seven Items in Daylight

Walk the party through the seven items in order. **The load-bearing item is #6, the ledger fragment.**

| # | Item | What's new in daylight | Key check + reveal |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Burned Spell Focus** | A scorch mark and a melted beeswax bead. | Arcana DC 13: the focus channeled a contingency *through* it. Arcana DC 16: the wax bears a **three-rayed sun closing into a fist** — not in Haskur's hand. Whoever laid the contingency was not Haskur. *(GM: this is the conspirators' patron school. Do not name it.)* |
| 2 | **False Letter of Passage** | Names "Haskur Voren, Agricultural Assessor, Loftwick Bureau of Crop Estimation." | Investigation DC 12: vellum, wax, chop all real. DC 15: signature matches the **retired** Bureau Chief's hand (retired 581 CY). History DC 14: the Bureau does not field itinerant assessors. *Inside job.* |
| 3 | **Clipped Coin** | The clipped edge bears a tiny countermark: a **tower above three waves**. | Investigation DC 12: private exchange mark. History DC 15: eastern port mark. DC 17: specifically **Cindren & Vhal's internal clearing-mark**. |
| 4 | **Black-Lacquered Scroll Tube** | A smaller tube nested inside the brass map tube. Unbroken red wax seal. | Investigation DC 10: courier-grade vessel, designed to ride inside another tube for deniability. Arcana DC 13: the wax discharges a one-shot *command* — *"Forget"* — Wis save DC 13. Arcana/History DC 17: glyph and lacquer-work are **elven but peculiar**; no Yeomanry sage can place the school. Investigation DC 14 (interior, after opening): wiped clean. Arcana DC 15: heat-reactive-ink residue — something was read, removed, and burned. The tube was a *relay*. *(GM: the work is Drow. **Do not name "Drow" under any roll.** *Legend lore* returns a fragmentary verse about a people who walked downward and were forgotten, which the caster cannot place.)* |
| 5 | **Vellum Strip** | Inside the tube. Four words in clear capitals: **HARBOR BEFORE THRONE**. | History DC 13: phrasing older than current Yeomanry idiom. Religion DC 15: a clause from the **Aerdi Concordat of the Bay** — the harbor outranks the crown until the crown acknowledges the harbor. Investigation DC 14 (as code): a timing instruction — seize the harbor before any new throne can claim its dues. *(GM: do not resolve which throne or harbor in Phase 2.)* |
| 6 | **Ledger Fragment** *(load-bearing)* | Inside the tube. Three lines in **three different hands**: *VANDRELL settled / MORAVEN delayed / GILST transferred.* | Investigation DC 13: three clerks — a status board. DC 14: accounting verbs, not personnel. "VANDRELL settled" matches the date Haskur's escrow was drawn down (**6,000 gp**, per the DM brief; Phase 1's evidence journal still uses the older in-fiction estimation framing of "on the order of ten thousand gold pieces — ship-and-crew scale" that Caelith would offer a curious player at the table — the actual settled figure on C&V's books is 6,000). DC 16: "delayed" = held instrument awaiting countersignature; "transferred" = an account (or person's payroll) moved to a different paymaster. DC 14 on the paper itself: **no sums, no dates, no totals.** Real settlement work always carries figures. **People were the point.** |
| 7 | **Hidden Seed Pouch** | Caelith cuts open the spellbook's lining in front of the party. Sterile durum wheat; a slip reading *"Seed for the dry barony."* | Nature DC 13: durum but blighted at the husk — display seeds. DC 16: sterile durum is used for **false crop claims**. Investigation DC 14: "the dry barony" is a colloquial name for grain-import-dependent regions of the **South Province of the Great Kingdom**. Nature DC 17 / Investigation DC 16 with tools: the seeds are chemically washed — a forgery on the forgery. *Ties Haskur to a false-crop scheme; "Moraven delayed" is now ominous.* |

What the party should leave the Refectory with: three names (**Cindren & Vhal**, **Gilst**, **Moraven**), one phrase (*"Harbor Before Throne"*), one inside-job inference (the false letter of passage), and a conspiracy at least nine years old.

### Scene 4 — The Records Alcove (later that morning, Library M5)

Caelith opens a paneled section of the rear wall onto a small **records alcove** the party did not see in Phase 1 — two walls of bound civic records the public library does not carry. Trina helps cross-reference; her quiet assistance grants Advantage on relevant checks.

| Thread | Check + finding |
|---|---|
| **Cindren & Vhal, the firm** | Investigation DC 12 (Aerdi Commercial Marks): the crane-and-coins seal is **Cindren & Vhal, Bonded Factors**, of Rel Astra (main), Hardby (branch), Loftwick (satellite only). DC 15: the tower-and-waves is their internal clearing-mark. History DC 14 (Charter Roll): the Loftwick agent is **Hesren Vesh** at a Wick Lane notary office — not a bonded branch. |
| **Merro Gilst** | Investigation DC 11 (Treasury Bonds Clerk Register): junior bonds clerk, age 51, fifteen-year posting, last entry stamped **three days before the party returned**, followed by *"posting closed."* DC 14 cross-reference: with no retirement notation and no transfer authorization, that means death. Certifying physician (**Reln Pollow**) is **not** on the Yeomanry coronial rota. Persuasion DC 13 with Caelith: he had not been informed of the death through Audit Hall channels, and asks the party not to pursue Pollow until he has read his licensure record. |
| **Tamsin Moraven** | Investigation DC 12 (Grain Factor Annual Returns): senior grain factor, twelve years in trade, counting house on **Tannerway**. Last filed four months ago. DC 15 cross-reference (returns + watch register): missing-person report filed **eleven days ago**, marked *"open, unescalated"* — left with a bag and cloak to "verify a load" at an unnamed customer farm. History DC 13: her filings included pointed queries against South Province grain shipments — exactly the dry-barony pattern. |
| **Vandrell aliases** *(background)* | Investigation DC 13: three "V---ll" variants in the same diplomatic index in the same hand — **Vendral, Vondrel, Voren** (Voren is the false-passage surname). DC 16: each variant ties to an unrelated incident in the last twelve years. Confirms Haskur as a serial cover-identity. |

The alcove takes most of a morning. If the party works efficiently, all three resolve before the second bell after noon. **Inefficient work or repeated failed rolls advance the Pressure Clock by 1.**

If asked, Trina answers as follows: **Cindren & Vhal** — *"Twice they came to me, and twice I said no, and the second time the man who asked stood a little too long in my doorway after he had been thanked. That is not how a clean firm leaves a house."* **Gilst** — *"I did not know him. I am sorry."* **Moraven** — *"Tamsin paid for half her daughter's tuition out of her own purse. I paid the other half. Tamsin does not vanish on her own initiative. Find her, please."*

### Scene 5 — Investigative Branches (rest of day 3 and day 4)

The party splits time across six destinations. **5a is load-bearing; steer toward it if they leave it cold.**

**5a — Gilst's rented rooms on Wick Lane (load-bearing).** Two rooms above a stationer's, three doors from Caelith's own rooms. Landlord **Doman Reith** (sixties, anxious) lets the party in on Caelith's written request *(for inquiry, not evidence transfer)*. Reith mentions a man with a Watch tabard who spent an hour in the rooms yesterday — wrong colour ribbon at the cuff; livery-roster cross-reference (Investigation DC 12) reveals the tabard was a real one **purchased** from a former courier. *(That was Merev Sarth on a reconnaissance pass; she did not find what the party can.)*

The room has been searched but the searcher missed:

- **The hollowed *Constitutional Commentaries* vol. III** (Investigation DC 13). Inside, a slim leather **receipts book**: forty-one cash entries over four years from "H.V. Hardby" totaling ~1,200 gp. **Three of the forty-one entries are countersigned in a different hand with a single capital initial: "E."** (Investigation DC 14). The countersigned entries are larger and irregular. *(GM: H.V. Hardby is C&V Hardby. The "E." is the actual paymaster. Caelith does not recognize it; Trina does not recognize it; no register names anyone relevant. **Do not name Eclavdra; do not name Drow.**)*
- **The throat-lozenge tin** on the writing desk (Perception DC 14; Investigation DC 12 for the false floor). Under it: an undated, signed, **bearer bank instrument for 200 gp drawn on Cindren & Vhal Hardby** — same chop, same hand as Vandrell's letter of credit. **The smoking-gun document.**
- **The locket** behind a framed sketch (Investigation DC 14). A tiny portrait of an unidentified dark-haired woman, ~30. No name. *(Personal mystery, not resolved this phase.)*
- **The burned note in the cold hearth** (Investigation DC 13). One corner: *"Do not copy eastern marks twice."*
- **A single coin shaving** along the desk seam (Perception DC 15; Investigation DC 12). Gilst was clipping coins for the firm.
- **The fear note** behind the locket portrait (Perception DC 12). Read aloud:

> *"If they ask again, I do not know how to keep saying no.*
> *If they stop asking, I do not know what that will mean.*
> *This is the third winter I have been afraid."*

Also: an **initials list** in the receipts book's back cover (Investigation DC 13) — five sets of initials with thumb-pressed marks, one of them simply *"E."*

Reith asks the party to take nothing, to keep his name out of any Watch conversation, and to reseal the door with their own wax. Honor that.

**If the party brings the bearer instrument back to Caelith, read aloud:**

> *Caelith holds the slip of paper at arm's length, then closer. He sets it on the desk. He looks at Trina. Trina sets down her tea.*
>
> *"That is a Hardby chop," Caelith says, very evenly. "That is the Hardby chop. The man who put that chop on this paper is the same man who put it on Vandrell's escrow. We now have a name for the next ring out. We do not have a face yet, but we have the hand."*
>
> *He does not pick it up again. After a moment he says: "You may have just shortened our work by a season."*

**5b — Reln Pollow** at his Wick Lane office. Soft, anxious, mid-forties. Confirms certifying Gilst's death. Insight DC 14: more nervous than a routine three-day-old certification warrants. Insight DC 17: his nerves are about something he expects to happen *now*. Persuasion DC 18 / Intimidation DC 16: he yields nothing actionable but sweats. If threatened with license-pulling, he asks for **two days** to leave for Greyhawk. **If denied, Pollow is poisoned the next evening.** **Caelith strongly recommends granting the two days.** *(Advances the Pressure Clock by 1.)*

**5c — Tamsin's counting house on Tannerway.** Chief clerk **Doril Veth** opens her last ledger. Investigation DC 12: her last three queries all reference C&V Hardby chops. DC 15: her own copies of two queries are **missing** — only carbon impressions remain. With Trina's help (or Investigation DC 16 against the carbons), the worst pattern surfaces: insurance and indemnity claims on three South Province shipments were **submitted before the corresponding losses occurred** — by eleven days in one case, three weeks in another. *Caelith: "That is not theft. That is being paid to know what tomorrow looks like."*

**5d — Vellin Moraven's home.** Tamsin's husband, mild, forty, a schoolmaster. Aged a year in eleven days. Hands the party, without prompting, the slip Tamsin left him: *"If I am not back by Sevenday, take Ailen out of Hardby."* Insight DC 12: gently suggest he send for Ailen tonight. Trina offers to send a letter to Ailen by her own courier — a poet leaving for Hardby that night. **Take her up on it.**

**5e — Sergeant Halir Anwic at the watch house.** Honest, overworked, embarrassed about his thin Moraven report. Will reopen at Caelith's request, not before. *(A **public** Watch escalation instead advances the clock by 1.)*

**5f — The C&V Wick Lane satellite (optional).** **Hesren Vesh** (early thirties, dark-green firm livery, courteous-to-stiffness) receives the party. He confirms the firm's marks from his published *Charter Roll of Marks* but will not discuss any client account without written authorization from Rel Astra. Insight DC 14 (if shown the Vandrell endorsement): his eyes settle on it for two breaths too long. Persuasion DC 18 / Intimidation DC 17: he allows it *"reads like Hardby work, not Loftwick work,"* but names no partner. If pressed harder he excuses himself to send a sealed courier message to Rel Astra (Perception DC 14 notices the courier leaving). *(Hesren is not a conspirator. **Visiting this office advances the clock by 1** and triggers C&V Hardby to begin preparing Sarth's contract.)*

### Scene 6 — Arthen Moll's Bribe Visit (afternoon of day 3 or morning of day 4)

**Trigger:** the party has identified **at least two** of the three names but has not yet committed to a travel approach.

Moll arrives by appointment. His card: *"A brief courtesy call. Half an hour, no more, with the gratitude of the Yeomanry."* Caelith asks Elsbet to receive him in the Library (M5), not the more ceremonial Reception Parlor. Trina, if in the building, slips in and takes the chair at the writing desk without speaking.

**Read Moll's pitch verbatim** (Interruptions journal → *Moll's pitch*). The substance: the party has done enough; a **1,200 gp honorarium per PC** by Yeomanry draft (not Council coin); the matter will be carried by the institutions whose business it is. He explicitly will not block their inquiry — but he will file a formal note that their continued visible inquiry is making Yeomanry eastern trade more expensive, and his Council allies will start asking Caelith uncomfortable questions in private session.

**Insight checks during the pitch:**

- DC 12: Moll is sincere; he wants them to take the money and stop.
- DC 15: the "eastern banks watch" line is not his own framing — he is repeating someone else's argument.
- DC 18: Moll has had this same conversation with three other parties this season on three different matters. He is the wall the soft-pressure faction puts in front of inquiries before they get too loud.

**Branches:**

- **Accept.** Drafts arrive at sundown. The Pressure Clock does not advance; the campaign continues quietly without the party as active investigators. Phase 3 may resume with them as informants.
- **Decline.** The Pressure Clock advances by 1. Within 24 hours a formal note is filed (Caelith burns one favor); through Hesren Vesh's earlier message, C&V Hardby confirms the party is uncontainable. **Sarth's raid is triggered the following night.**
- **Defer.** Moll leaves the drafts and returns next morning. **Pressure Clock advances by 1 regardless.** Sarth is triggered the night the decision is made.

Trina afterward, if asked: *"He believes what he says. That is the worst kind of obstacle. You cannot persuade him; you can only outwork him."*

### Scene 7 — Merev Sarth's Night Raid on the Strong Room

**Trigger:** the night following the party's decline-or-defer of Moll.

Sarth is on a **retrieval contract, not a kill contract.** Paid extra not to leave bodies. Fallback is to ignite the evidence. Her plan: enter via the courtyard service alley (M8 south gate) at the third bell after midnight in a Yeomanry courier tabard with a forged Audit Hall inspection writ; down the spiral stair (M12 → C1) to the cellar; pick the **Strong Room (C5)** double lock (Sleight of Hand DC 18 against each — wax cast of the second from a recent inspection by Hesren Vesh's clerk); exit through the **Old Service Tunnel (C7)**.

**What she does not know:** the chest contains **decoys** (originals went to Trina's townhouse the moment Vesh's courier left Wick Lane); the Service Tunnel has been watched for three days, with **Brell Kostan** (MM Warrior Veteran) at the far end; Caelith has placed an **alarm rune** inside the Strong Room door (Arcana DC 14 to recognize) that chimes softly in U1 and U2.

**Resolution branches.** *Intercept en route* — Stealth opposed by Passive Perception. Sarth's priority is exit, not victory; smoke pellet, Cunning Action Disengage, runs for the tunnel. *Party asleep, alarm chimes* — wakes Caelith and any PC in U2 or U1 (Wis save DC 8). Caelith does not draw a weapon. He says: *"You will leave that wallet, and you will go through the tunnel as you intended, and the corporal at the other end will explain what happens next."* Sarth drops the wallet and runs for the tunnel. *Sarth clean to the tunnel* — Brell Kostan disarms her; the party hears at breakfast.

**Combat (if it comes to it).** Sarth: CR 4, AC 15, HP 65, DEX 17, Sneak Attack +14 (4d6), Cunning Action, Uncanny Dodge, Evasion. Tight 10×12 ft chamber, single door. If reduced to 30 HP or below she yields; if offered surrender, takes it.

**Captured alive.** Two days of silence; on the third in lawful custody she yields: the job was **three drafts of 200 gp each** on different banking houses (laundering layer); the Hardby intermediary is named **"Tarsh"** (thin man, fifties, smoker's voice — she thinks "Tarsh" isn't his real name either); she will not name her broker under any circumstance. Caelith offers her a Yeomanry passport plus 100 gp for one testimony (Persuasion DC 14 — she accepts).

**Killed.** Recovered: wallet, oilcloth, smoke pellets, glass oil vial, forged writ, tabard. **The forged Audit Hall seal on the writ** matches a stamp Caelith has been quietly looking for in his own office for nine months. *(GM-only: leads to Ostren Pell in Phase 5. **Do not name Pell.**)*

**Outcome:** clock advances by 1 regardless. The party leaves the night certain that the political pressure (Moll) and the criminal pressure (Sarth) are coordinated.

### Scene 8 — Caelith's Conclusion (morning of day 3 of investigation, after Sarth resolves)

Refectory. Evidence still on the table. **Read Caelith's Conclusion verbatim** (Departure journal → *Caelith's Conclusion*). The beats: the countermark beside the clipped coin; *"Cindren & Vhal. Bonded factors. Respectable enough for clean rooms. Useful enough for dirty ones."*; the eastern coast pinned between the linen squares; the senior partners in Rel Astra's Old Harbor Ward who *"answer to no one in this kingdom or the next, in any way the law would let us reach them"*; the close —

> *"I do not know whether Haskur Vandrell is there.*
>
> *But someone there was ready for him.*
>
> *And if we have learned this much by morning… then by nightfall they will know we learned it."*

This pivots the phase from *"what happened?"* to *"how soon do we leave?"* **If the Pressure Clock has not advanced past segment 2, advance it by 1 as the speech ends.**

### Scene 9 — The Three Travel Approaches (Old Council Chamber U4)

Caelith asks the party to meet him in U4 — the disused upper-floor room with the ring of nine carved chairs. Elsbet has opened it; the hearth is just warming. Trina sits in the second-oldest chair (by founding-district name). Three small leather folios on the round oak table.

| Approach | Speed | What it buys | What it costs | Pressure Clock |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **1. Official Delegation** | 8–10 days to Hardby, 8–10 more to Rel Astra | Legal standing; C&V cannot refuse to receive accredited inquirers; diplomatic cover for any rescued witness. | The conspiracy knows when the party will arrive. Witnesses vanish; documents are sanitized. Caelith burns **four favors** filing credentials. | **+2 immediate**, **+1 per travel week**. |
| **2. Commercial Cover** | 6–7 days to Hardby, 4–5 more | Plausible access (C&V receives them as a routine business call). Trina's letter to **Castrian Vell**, poet in Hardby. Veska Maelan (Phase 1's forward hook) as backup. | Discipline — must play the role consistently. No diplomatic cover if it breaks (up to 30 days held on suspicion of false identity). | **0 at departure.** +1 if cover seriously broken (GM call). +1 if the party visits Castrian Vell openly more than twice in Hardby. |
| **3. Quiet Pursuit** | 4–5 days to Hardby, 3 more | Invisibility — the conspiracy doesn't know they're moving until they arrive. Optionality on arrival. | No cover. If caught with false papers east of Hardby, Caelith cannot reach them. C&V will not receive them without an introduction — find a side door. | **0 at departure.** +2 if recognized in Hardby and unable to leave quickly. |

**Caelith's framing**, when the party asks him to help them choose:

> *"I can give you authority, cover, or distance. I cannot give you all three."*

If pressed for a recommendation: *"If your party can keep its mouth shut for two weeks, the second path is the path I would take. If your party cannot, the first path will keep you alive longer than the third, even if it makes the work slower."* Trina, if asked, says nothing about paths 1 or 3. Of path 2: *"Castrian's house is mine in everything but address. He will know you are mine. Do not embarrass him."*

**Fourth-approach proposals.** Magical travel — Caelith has no *teleportation circle* tuned to Hardby or Rel Astra, and is wary of Aerdi harbor wards reading magical arrivals; the party may have their own resource. Sea passage from Saltmarsh — adds two weeks but bypasses Hardby (C&V have no sea-leg there); allowed. Splitting the party — Caelith advises against: *"You are stronger than my agents because you are together."*

### Scene 10 — Departure (Ending Read-Aloud)

**Read the Ending read-aloud verbatim** (Departure journal → *Ending Read-Aloud — Departing East*). The beats: the Civic Way at its quietest, lamps along the Audit Hall still burning under a milk-coloured sky; the footmen raising lanterns once and lowering them (the old Yeomanry sign for safe travel); Caelith at the gate offering a brass Hand-token to the lead rider — *"If you find the man who underwrote him, write to me at this house, with this on the seal. Three days from now I will be in this office or its successor; the token will reach me either way"*; Trina at his shoulder, silent, lifting a hand briefly; the Wool Road; Hardby six days east pressed hard, Rel Astra eleven by the maps but closer in Caelith's quiet calculation.

**Alongside the brass token, Caelith presses a small folded paper into the lead rider's hand.** *(New in v1.3.0; see `packs/_source/phase-3-journals/journal-phase-3-mossen-place.json`.)* In his careful handwriting: **17 Cooper's Tale, Hardby merchant quarter. Ask Mistress Halver at the corner grocer for the key. Tell her the cellarer's apprentice came down from Loftwick to mind the house. She will not ask further.** The deed is held under the name **"Mistress Eleyn Mossen, retired, of Hardby"** — a cover identity Caelith has maintained for nearly a decade through a Loftwick solicitor. Caelith does not say more at the gate; the paper does the rest. The party reads it on the road. *(GM: this is the Phase 3 safe house. Full layout, two hidden caches, and Caelith's sealed letter to whoever finds the floorboard live in the Mossen Place journal.)*

**Phase 2 ends here.** Phase 3 picks up at the gates of Hardby.

## The Pressure Clock (transverse to all scenes)

Phase 2's pacing device — a **six-segment visible clock**, players see it, target end-of-phase **3–4**. The full trigger table, segment-by-segment fires, target curve, telegraphs, and overrun/underrun recovery moves are in the DM brief journal's **Pacing the Pressure Clock** page (canonical mechanic in the Departure journal). The walkthrough above already names the in-scene advances at the moments they fire.

Two staging notes the walkthrough needs to keep visible:

- **Moll's visit (Scene 6) and Sarth's raid (Scene 7) are NOT clock segments.** They are scheduled scenes triggered by the party's choices; they overlap with the clock but do not depend on it.
- **Decide your segment-6 closing event before play** (Tamsin confirmed dead / Caelith recalled / Vellin taken — pick the one that hurts most given your party's investments). This is the only pre-game prep the walkthrough requires beyond reading the journal.

## How Phase 2 ends

The trigger is **the party riding out of the Lesser Gate onto the Wool Road** under whichever travel approach they chose. The Ending read-aloud fires at the gate. Phase 3 begins at the gates of **Hardby**.

A clean Phase 2 ends with the party carrying: the three identifications (firm, dead clerk, missing factor); the bearer instrument from Gilst's tin (linking C&V Hardby's chop to Gilst's payroll); the "E." in Gilst's books (never to be named in this phase); the *Harbor Before Throne* strip and the ledger fragment; the forged Audit Hall seal from Sarth (a Phase 5 thread); Caelith's Hand-token; Trina's letter to Castrian Vell (if cover); Veska Maelan's introduction from Phase 1 (still good).

## Branches & contingencies

The DM brief journal's **Common GM Pitfalls** page covers the eight standard failure modes with recoveries (naming "E.", confirming "Drow"/"Underdark", letting Pollow die unnecessarily, running Sarth as a kill encounter, splitting the party too far, skipping Trina & Alicia moments, the party riding straight to Rel Astra, and the party accepting Moll's bribe). Use that page when the run drifts toward one of those failure modes. The branches below are the ones specific to this walkthrough's scene-by-scene flow:

- **The party never visits Gilst's rooms.** Steer toward it — the rooms are load-bearing. If you cannot, Caelith mentions in Scene 8 that he visited himself and presents the bearer instrument and fear note as if found by his own contact. They lose the texture but keep the evidence.
- **The party identifies fewer than three names.** Moll's trigger (Scene 6) requires two; the bribe visit can still fire. If the party only has one name by the end of day 4, advance the clock aggressively for inactivity and have Caelith summarize the third thread in Scene 8 — they don't get the rolls, but they get the names.
- **The party tries to leave Loftwick before identifying the three names.** Caelith will not stop them; he asks them to carry the bearer instrument and the *Harbor Before Throne* strip at minimum, and to write to him from the road.
- **A PC tries to extract Caelith's suspected leak.** He will not name a clerk he cannot prove. The Audit Hall seal recovered from Sarth eventually leads to **Ostren Pell** in Phase 5; **do not name Pell in Phase 2.**
- **A PC presses Trina on her patron.** *"I share a teacher with you. I have not finished my lessons. When I have finished, I will tell you her name."* Insight DC 18 reads that she is hiding more than embarrassment, but **do not give the party Tasha's name in Phase 2** even on a successful Insight.
- **The clock reaches segment 6 before the party has chosen a travel approach.** Force the choice in Scene 9. Whichever closing-event you picked fires; they either ride out within 48 hours or sit in a Loftwick where Caelith cannot help them.

## Carry to Phase 3

Make a private note:

- **The Mossen Place folded paper.** The v1.3.0 Phase 3 journal `journal-phase-3-mossen-place.json` adds a Hardby safe house Caelith hands over via a small folded paper at the Phase 2 farewell gate (17 Cooper's Tale, ask Mistress Halver at the corner grocer for the key; cover identity *"Mistress Eleyn Mossen, retired, of Hardby."*). The paper is given alongside the brass Hand-token (Scene 10). Phase 3 opens with the party reading it on the road.
- **Which travel approach the party took.** Sets Phase 3's opening at Hardby — credentialed delegation, wool-merchant party calling on Castrian Vell, or three nameless riders looking for a side door.
- **The Pressure Clock's final segment count.** Phase 3 inherits it. Clock 3 has room; clock 5 is racing the broadsheets there.
- **Which closing-event you picked for segment 6**, if it fired. Tamsin alive-but-delayed is the default; if you committed her to dead, Phase 3's Tamsin thread becomes a recovery, not a rescue.
- **Whether Sarth was captured, killed, or escaped.** Captured gives "Tarsh." Killed gives the forged Audit Hall seal (Phase 5).
- **Whether the party gave Pollow his two days.** Alive in Greyhawk is a Phase 4+ witness.
- **Whether the party accepted Trina's quiet favor.** If unspent, she offers it again on their next Loftwick return — but only once.
- **Whether Ailen Moraven is being sent home.** If the letter went, she will be at Vellin's when they next visit Loftwick. If not, she is still in Hardby and is the next obvious hostage.
- **The "E." countersignature is in the players' notes, unnamed.** Phase 3 does not name it. Phase 4 begins to surface the school; Phase 5+ surfaces the patron. **Do not slip and name Eclavdra or Drow early.**
- **The phrase *Harbor Before Throne* is in the players' notes, unresolved.** Hold for the long arc.
- **The forged Audit Hall seal recovered from Sarth.** Caelith is reading it quietly. Leads to a clerk in his own office (Ostren Pell). **Phase 2 and Phase 3 do not name Pell.** If you used the Phase 1 Burning Page complication, it is the same thread.
- **Whether the party has begun to trust Trina.** Phase 3 weighs heavier on her network if they do.

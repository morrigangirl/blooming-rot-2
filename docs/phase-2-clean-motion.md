# Phase 2 — *The Dead Man's Receipts* — Clean-Motion GM Prep

**A ground-up rework of the Phase 2 flow.** Status: draft for review (not yet folded into the Foundry journals).

This document re-engineers *how Phase 2 moves* — the order of discovery, the way each scene hands the party to the next, the failsafes that keep a stalled group moving, and a new in-city chase. It **preserves every canon fact** already built into the Phase 2 journals (the seven evidence items, the three names, the NPC knowledge matrix, the Pressure Clock, the three travel approaches, and every "do not name" guardrail). What changes is the **spine and the propulsion**, not the lore.

> **How to use this doc.** Run Phase 2 from *this* file. When it points to detail — a full NPC line, a statblock, a read-aloud — it names the existing journal page to pull from (e.g. *Investigation → Merro Gilst*). Think of this as the conductor's score; the existing journals are the parts.

---

## 0. Why the rework — the one problem to fix

The Phase 2 material is strong: tight evidence chains, a genuinely excellent *Who Knows What* matrix, a well-built Pressure Clock. The flaw is **motion**. In the current build:

- **Clues *identify* but don't *direct*.** Nearly every clue tells the party *what something is* ("this seal is Cindren & Vhal"). Almost none tells them *where to go next*. The investigation therefore can't pull itself forward — propulsion comes entirely from Caelith pointing and the clock pushing. That reads as a rail, not a hunt.
- **The structure is three parallel info-dumps** (Thread A / B / C) plus two interruptions. There's no place where one discovery *changes* another, and no spatial logic the players can hold in their heads.
- **Nothing in the city moves.** Every in-city beat is a static search or a seated conversation. The phase never gets off its chair.

The rework fixes all three with one idea:

> **Every scene must answer the question *"where do we go next, and why?"* — in the fiction, on the table, without the GM having to say it.**

That turns Phase 2 from *a list of places Caelith sends you* into *a city that keeps pointing east on its own*.

## 2. The five rules that make it move

These are the design rules the rework runs on. They're also your at-the-table instincts when players go somewhere unplanned.

**1. Clues point forward, not just inward.** Every clue carries a *direction* as well as an *identity*. When you reveal what a thing is, in the same breath reveal *where the thing it implies can be found*. (The clue tables in §4 give you the "→ next" for each one. Use them.)

**2. The three-clue rule — no single roll gates a must-know.** Every fact the party *must* leave Phase 2 with is supported by **at least three independent sources**, so a missed check or a skipped location never stalls the phase. §3 lists the sources for each must-know. If two are missed, the third still lands.

**3. Geography over abstraction.** The middle of Phase 2 is **two streets**, not three lettered threads. Players navigate *Wick Lane* and *Tannerway* — places they can picture and return to — not "Thread B." Everything dense lives on Wick Lane; everything human lives on Tannerway.

**4. Convergence is the engine.** You are not building suspense about *whether* the party goes east — Phase 1 already aimed them there. You're making the city *earn* it. **Six independent vectors** (see the map) all point to Hardby. The party assembles the arrow themselves. That feeling — *"every thread we pull runs the same way"* — is the clean motion.

**5. Pressure is a metronome, not a hammer.** The Pressure Clock paces the phase and, crucially, **re-energizes a stalled table**: every segment is a *new in-fiction event* that hands the party something to react to. Used right, the clock is itself a rail (see §7).

## 3. What the party must learn (the knowledge ledger)

Three tiers. **Must-know** gates entry to Phase 3 — and each is guaranteed by the three-clue rule. **Should-know** sharpens the hunt and makes the choices land. **May-collect** is texture and campaign seed: gather it, do *not* resolve it.

### MUST leave Phase 2 knowing (the gate to Phase 3)

Each must-know is phrased as the table-facing truth, followed by its **independent sources** (any one suffices; three exist so it can't be missed).

### SHOULD know (sharpens the hunt)

- **Gilst was murdered, procedurally** — coronial system *maneuvered around* (contract physician, 12-hour cremation, "posting closed" with no transfer). The Yeomanry wasn't corrupted; it was *used*.
- **Tamsin was investigating false-crop receipts** on the same grain trade Haskur's seed pouch points at — and the **claims were filed *before* the losses** (prediction → profit → erasure).
- **C&V's structure**: Rel Astra (senior partners, the deep work), Hardby (the operational hand), Loftwick (one satellite clerk who doesn't know what his seniors do).
- **The firm can reach inside the Little Palace** (Sarth) *and* inside the Council (Moll's faction) — and a watcher has been on Wick Lane all along.

### MAY collect — do **not** resolve (campaign seeds; see §8 guardrails)

- **"E."** — the initial above C&V. Collected once (Gilst's countersignatures + the initials list). *Never identified.*
- **The "elven, but peculiar" tube** — deep-craft texture. *Never named as Drow.*
- **HARBOR BEFORE THRONE** — a timing instruction against an unnamed succession. *Phase 4.*
- **The leak in Caelith's office** — implied by the inside forgery, the inventory tampering, and the forged Audit Hall seal on Sarth's writ. *Caelith will not name a clerk he can't prove.*
- **"The man at the Brass Crow"** — only if Pollow dies with a note (Greyhawk / Phase 6 seed).
- **The Hardby barmaid (580)** — a loose thread from Caelith's speech.

## 5. The motion, beat by beat

Each beat below gives you only what the rework changes: the **setup**, the **forward-pointing clue(s) out**, and the **handoff**. For full read-aloud, DCs, and NPC lines, pull the named journal page.

### ACT 1 — THE TABLE *(≈ half a session)*

The job of Act 1 is to convert **objects into addresses**. By the end, the party should be reaching for their cloaks because they know two streets to walk to — not because Caelith told them to.

#### B1 · Morning Evidence Review — *Refectory (M4)*
*Pull: Evidence Review → Opening Read-Aloud; Caelith's Expanded Explanation; The Daylight Re-Examination.*

Run it as written through Caelith's Hand-of-the-Duke reveal and the seven items. **The one change is the exit.** Don't let the morning end on "interesting objects." End it on **three names and a place to take them.** As the party turns over the ledger fragment (#6 — *VANDRELL settled / MORAVEN delayed / GILST transferred*), Caelith makes the motion explicit:

> *"Three names, three hands, no sums. That is not a banker's ledger — it is a roster. I keep the rolls that turn names into men: who they were, where they posted, where they died. They are downstairs, behind the library wall. Let us go and make these three into people."*

- **Forward clue out:** the ledger fragment is no longer just "people were the point." It is a *to-do list with an address* — the Records Alcove.
- **Texture to flag, not chase:** the tube (#4, "elven but peculiar"), the strip (#5, HARBOR BEFORE THRONE), and the focus's sigil (#1) are explicitly set aside — Caelith says he has *"seen that hand twice in nine years and never with a name; we do not chase what we cannot yet hold."* (Tells the table: collect, don't pursue. See §8.)
- **Handoff:** straight to B2. Trina comes down with them.

> **If the table is large or slow,** cut the item-by-item and let each PC *claim* an item to "read to the room." Same evidence, half the table-time, and every player gets one forward clue in their own mouth.

#### B2 · The Records Alcove — *Library / Records Room (M5), hidden panel*
*Pull: Investigation → The Records Alcove.*

This is the **pivot of the whole phase** and the rework leans on it hardest. Run the three identifications as written — but the deliverable is no longer "you now know three names." It's **two streets and a firm**, said out loud, in this shape:

| Name resolves to… | …and the address that comes with it |
|---|---|
| **Cindren & Vhal** (crane-and-coins + tower-over-waves) | a satellite office on **Wick Lane**; main houses in **Hardby** and **Rel Astra** |
| **Merro Gilst**, dead bonds clerk | lodged **above the stationer on Wick Lane**; certified by Pollow, *also on Wick Lane* |
| **Tamsin Moraven**, missing grain factor | counting house on **Tannerway**; husband on Tannerway; an open Watch file |

- **Forward clue out (the engine):** "Wick Lane has three reasons to visit it; Tannerway has three. Where do you want to start?" The party now has **two hubs** and chooses their own order. *That choice is the motion.*
- **East already whispering (plant, don't push):** the alcove also yields that Moraven was *corresponding with a Hardby weighmaster*, the firm's seniors are *in Rel Astra*, and the chop on the credit is *Hardby work*. Three quiet east-pointers, laid down before the party has even left the building.
- **Handoff:** to Act 2. Caelith does **not** escort them into the city — *"My face is known on Wick Lane; yours is not yet. Go as the strangers you are."* (This matters: it hands the legwork — and the motion — to the players.)

> **Three-clue safety:** if the party blows the alcove rolls, the addresses are *not* gated behind them. Trina supplies any name the dice withhold (she knew Moraven personally; she recognizes the firm's "odor"), and Caelith's rolls (*Bonds Clerk Register*, *Charter Roll*) backstop the rest. Nobody leaves this room without both streets.

### ACT 2 — THE CITY *(1–2 sessions, player-driven)*

Two hubs. Let the party split a *day* between them (morning on one, afternoon on the other) rather than splitting the *party* (see §7 on the split trap). Everything here is designed so that **whatever they pull on runs east.**

#### HUB 1 · WICK LANE — *the dense hub (and the kinetic one)*

Three doors and a street, all within sight of each other: the green-liveried C&V satellite, the stationer's with Gilst's sealed rooms above, Pollow's office two doors down — and, dryly, Caelith's own rented rooms three doors along (he notes the coincidence and does not like it). **This is where the city stops sitting still.**

**▸ The C&V satellite — Hesren Vesh.** *Pull: Investigation → Cindren & Vhal Firm Dossier → Visiting the Loftwick satellite.* Run as written. The two things that matter for motion:
- Show him the endorsement → his eyes hold on it two beats too long (*Insight DC 14*); pressed (*Persuasion 18 / Intimidation 17*) he gives the forward clue in four words: **"reads like Hardby work."** *(M3, east-pointer.)*
- He sends a courier the moment they leave. In the rework this is **not** a "watch but don't intercept" tease — it's one of the two triggers that arms **the chase** (§6). Tipping Vesh puts a runner on the street. *(+1 Pressure Clock.)*

**▸ Gilst's rooms — Doman Reith.** *Pull: Investigation → Gilst's Rented Room.* The seven hidden items are unchanged; what's sharpened is which ones *direct*:
- **B. The throat-lozenge tin → the smoking gun.** A bearer instrument on the C&V **Hardby** chop, *same hand as Vandrell's escrow.* This is the single most forward-pointing object in the phase. Caelith, shown it: *"We now have the hand. The hand is in Hardby."* *(M3.)*
- **A/F. The "E." countersignatures and initials list →** collect, don't resolve (§8).
- **Reith's "watch-tabard man" →** ties to Sarth's recon *and* tells the party **they are not the first or only ones working this room** — which primes them to notice they're being watched (the chase trigger). The wrong cuff-ribbon (*Investigation DC 12*) proves the tabard was *bought*, not Watch-issued.

**▸ Pollow's office — Reln Pollow.** *Pull: Investigation → Confronting Reln Pollow.* Unchanged. The rework only asks you to treat **"grant him two days"** as a *rail with a reward*: spare him and Caelith holds him through Hardby contacts (a live witness for later); let him die and he leaves **one** trembling line — *"the man at the Brass Crow"* — a clean Greyhawk/Phase 6 seed. Either way the scene points forward.

**▸ ★ THE CHASE — the posted watcher.** Working this lane — leaning on Vesh, or coming out of Gilst's sealed rooms — trips a lookout who has been logging everyone who comes asking. The party moves on them; they run. **Full set-piece in §6.** Its payload (guaranteed regardless of whether they catch anyone) is a dropped fold that points to **a Loftwick drop-point** *and* **"east on the Sevenday cart."** This is the beat that makes Hub 1 *move*, and it's the single biggest addition of the rework.

> **Wick Lane convergence:** by the time the party leaves this street they should be holding the Hardby chop (Gilst), the words "Hardby work" (Vesh), and "east on the Sevenday cart" (the chase). Three east-pointers from one street.

#### HUB 2 · TANNERWAY — *the human hub*

Where Wick Lane is paper and pursuit, Tannerway is people. This hub supplies the *stakes* that convert the party's curiosity into urgency (M5).

**▸ Tamsin's counting house — Doril Veth.** *Pull: Investigation → Tamsin Moraven.* Her last ledger yields three **C&V Hardby** queries (*Investigation DC 12*); two original carbons have been *removed and replaced with impressions* (*DC 15*) — **someone got here first**, which tells the party the firm reaches into Loftwick at will. With Trina assisting, the **claims-before-loss** pattern surfaces (*DC 14*): the conspiracy wasn't stealing, it was *arranging tomorrow.* *(East-pointer + stakes.)*

**▸ Vellin Moraven's home — Vellin.** *Pull: same page.* The Sevenday note — *"If I am not back by Sevenday, take Ailen out of Hardby"* — and the fact that **Ailen is in Hardby right now.** This is the emotional hinge of M5: the party isn't chasing a ledger, they're chasing a family with a clock on it. Trina, if present, writes to Ailen that night (the letter pays off in Phase 3). *(East-pointer: Hardby, with a face on it.)*

**▸ Sgt Halir Anwic — the watch house.** *Pull: same page.* Reopens the file on Caelith's word; becomes a Watch ally; if the clock later removes him, he gifts his notes. Keeps the Moraven thread warm without gating anything.

> **The "delayed" reading.** Hold Caelith's line: *"The verb is not 'settled.' She is somewhere they have not yet decided to close her account. They will, eventually."* Tamsin **alive but contained** is what makes the travel-approach choice in Act 3 *hurt* — speed might save her; visibility might get her "settled."

#### WOVEN · Trina & Alicia
*Pull: Trina Alvere → Mentor Notes — Alicia.* One beat per session, no more. The recognition at the table; the moth scarf after Moll or after Gilst's death lands; the clerks'-poems beat in the Library. This is the **soft counterweight** to the political cold — ration it so it keeps its weight. It carries no plot motion by design; it carries *morale*, which a horror-of-administration phase needs.

#### The convergence (say it with the map, not with Caelith)

When the party pools Act 2, lay the vectors side by side and let *them* draw the line:

```
   Gilst's bank instrument ── Hardby chop
   Hesren Vesh ────────────── "reads like Hardby work"
   The chase fold ─────────── "east on the Sevenday cart"
   Tamsin's queries ───────── C&V Hardby
   Ailen Moraven ──────────── in Hardby, unprotected
   Veska Maelan (Caelith's) ─ in Hardby, can read the chops
                                   ▼
                              H A R D B Y
```

Six independent vectors, one destination. **You never have to tell them where to go.** You just have to let them notice they already know.

### ACT 3 — THE SQUEEZE & THE ROAD *(≈1 session)*

Act 3 turns curiosity into urgency (the last yard of M4 and M5) and puts the party on the road in a way that *shapes Phase 3.*

#### B-Moll · Arthen Moll's pressure visit — *Library (M5)*
*Pull: Interruptions → Arthen Moll.* Unchanged and excellent. Trigger it once the party has **two of three names** and hasn't chosen travel. The bribe is real (1,200 gp each); the threat is institutional, not violent; he *believes himself* (Insight 12/15/18 peels the borrowed line). Declining: **+1 clock and it arms Sarth.** Moll is the political face of M4.

> **Motion note:** Moll is the wall the soft-pressure faction puts up *before things get loud.* The party's own visible legwork in Act 2 is what summons him — which means the chase (loud, public, in the street) can be the thing that brings him a day early. Let that causality show.

#### B-Sarth · Merev Sarth's retrieval night — *Cellar / Strong Room (C5)*
*Pull: Interruptions → Merev Sarth.* Unchanged: retrieve-or-burn, **not** a kill contract; the chest holds decoys (originals at Trina's); the alarm rune; Corporal **Brell Kostan** waiting at the tunnel's far end; she yields at 30 HP. Capture (or her later testimony) yields the name **"Tarsh"** — the Hardby intermediary. The criminal face of M4, and another Hardby pointer.

> **Rework tie-in (optional, strong):** the posted watcher from §6 was feeding the *same chain* that dispatched Sarth. If the party staked out or traced the chase's **Loftwick drop-point**, let that legwork pay off here — they anticipate the retrieval, or recognize the courier's mark on Sarth's forged writ. It makes the two squeezes feel like one hand, which they are.

#### B-Road · Three Approaches, the Clock, and Departure — *Old Council Chamber (U4) → the Gate*
*Pull: Departure → Caelith's Conclusion; Three Travel Approaches; The Pressure Clock; Ending Read-Aloud.* Unchanged. Caelith's line is the whole choice: **"I can give you authority, cover, or distance. I cannot give you all three."** Each approach sets a *different Phase 3* (see §9). This is the gate — run your M1–M5 check (§3) before the folios hit the table.

> **The clock at the gate.** Aim to arrive here at **segment 3–4** — pressure landing without having prevented the hunt. §7 tells you how to recover an over- or under-run clock.

## 6. ★ The Chase — *"The Sparrow's Run"*

> The new in-city set-piece. An investigation on Wick Lane flushes a lookout the party can chase but **cannot catch** — by design — and the chase pays them a clue *whether or not* they ever lay a hand on her. It is the kinetic heart of Phase 2 and the cleanest single source of forward motion in the phase.

### 6.1 Design intent (read this first)

A "you can't catch him" chase fails at most tables for two reasons: it feels like a railroad, and it feels like a punishment. This one avoids both with three rules:

1. **The clue is decoupled from the capture.** The watcher sheds the clue *partway through the route* — to clear an obstacle — so the party recovers it **on a win, on a loss, or on a draw.** Motion is guaranteed. The capture is a *bonus*, not the objective.
2. **The escape is earned, not decreed.** The watcher is a former message-runner escaping across *her own ground* (a culvert she fits and an armored adult doesn't; a skiff she's pre-positioned). You can say this out loud. She isn't faster because the GM says so; she's faster *here* because this is literally her job.
3. **Catching her yields little more than the fold.** She's a terrified local hire who knows only a drop-point and a stranger's coppers. So even a brilliant capture doesn't break the design — the fold is still the payload, and now the party has a scared kid and a moral choice (see 6.6). The GM is *never* left stuck.

### 6.2 The watcher — "Sparrow"

> **Anla "Sparrow" Coyle**, ~16. A wiry, wary former courier-leg, out of work since the small message-house she ran for folded. A fortnight ago a man she never got a clear look at left coppers and a chalk-stub at a dead-letter niche with a job: *watch the stationer's door on Wick Lane, mark who goes up to the dead clerk's rooms and who calls on the green-coats, leave a tally each Sevenday, keep nothing.* She does not know who pays. She does not know what it's for. She knows the lanes of Loftwick better than the Watch does, because she used to outrun the Watch for a living.

She is deliberately a **minor, sympathetic NPC** — this echoes Phase 1's working-class-downstream-of-the-conspiracy note (the Cook's Niece) and gives the chase a conscience. *If your table wants a harder target, see the variant in 6.7.*

**Quick stats** *(build as a Commoner with a courier's edge; full actor can be added on integration):*
`AC 12 · HP 9 · Speed 35 ft.` · DEX 15 (+2), proficient **Acrobatics +4, Stealth +4, streetwise (Survival) +2.** **Nimble Bolt:** Bonus Action Dash or Disengage (the engine of her escape). Carries no weapon she'll use — only a fleece-hook and the oilcloth fold.

### 6.3 Triggering the chase (over-determined on purpose)

The chase should fire **reliably** — it's a spine beat, not a lucky break. Three independent triggers, any of which starts it (three-clue rule applied to a *scene*):

- **They clock the tail.** While on Wick Lane: passive Perception **14**, or an active Perception/Insight **DC 13**, notices a loiterer with a clean sightline to the stationer's door who shifts whenever the party shifts.
- **They flush her on the way out.** If no one spots her, the moment the party leaves Gilst's rooms *carrying anything*, she peels off to report — and a PC may catch the movement (**DC 12**) as she goes.
- **Reith hands her to them.** Hard rail: the landlord mentions, unprompted, *"there's a young one's been hung about the corner since the watch-coat man came. Coyle's girl, I think. Hungry-looking."*

The instant the party **moves to approach or intercept**, Sparrow bolts. Chase begins.

> **If the party never engages** (ignores her, lets her watch): she tails them home, and *her* report is what tips the firm — fold that into the timing of Moll/Sarth and advance the clock as for an uneventful day. She resurfaces on Wick Lane later, giving a second shot. No dead end.

### 6.4 Running the pursuit — a four-reach lead clock

Run it as a **chase clock**, not a single roll. Track an abstract **Lead** (start: **2**). Each round, every pursuing PC makes **one** check against the current reach's obstacle; **net successes reduce the Lead, net failures raise it.** Sparrow reaches her bolt-hole when the route runs out (Reach 4). Catching her requires Lead **0** *before* she clears Reach 4 — which her home-ground advantage makes very hard on purpose.

Use the **Loftwick — City** scene (token vision ON; drop a light set of street walls along the route; place four reach-markers). Keep it to **3–4 rounds.**

| Reach | The ground | Obstacle (pick the PC's approach) | DC | What happens |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **1** | Wick Lane spilling into the **Cloth Market** | **Crowd.** Athletics (shove), Acrobatics (over a stall), Persuasion/Intimidation ("*Make way!*"), or Insight/Investigation (read her line, cut the corner) | 13 | She uses the press; Nimble Bolt keeps her ahead. |
| **2** | The **market wall & the Loft Steps** down toward the water | **A low wall + worn steps.** Athletics or Acrobatics to keep pace | 15 | **★ THE FOLD DROPS HERE.** To vault one-handed she slings off her oilcloth shoulder-fold (or it's jolted loose); it skids onto the steps. A PC may grab it (costs that PC's action this round) or mark the spot and retrieve after. |
| **3** | The **Tanners' cut**, narrow and reeking | **A barrow of fleeces** she upends behind her. Acrobatics/Dex to vault, Athletics to bull through, or Survival/streetwise to know the cut | 15 | She banks her lead here. Failed pursuers lose a round untangling. |
| **4** | The **Loft culvert / a waiting flat-skiff** | **Her bolt-hole.** She slips a culvert grate too small for an armored adult, or drops into a pre-poled skiff into a drain-arch | — | **Designed escape.** Unless the party has *already* driven Lead to 0 **and** has a real way to follow (a flying/Small PC, a readied grapple, *hold person/monster*, water-walk), she is gone into the under-streets. |

**Fairness at the table:** show the Lead tracker. Announce that Reach 4 is her ground. Never let one failed roll end the chase — it's a clock, it absorbs misses. If a PC spends a *spell or a real resource* to cut a reach (e.g. *misty step* past the wall, Trina's *hold monster* if she's nearby and earned), reward it: drop the Lead by 2 or skip a reach. The chase should feel like the party *did* everything right and still met a professional on her home street.

### 6.5 The dropped fold (the guaranteed payload)

A flat oilcloth packet, a courier's everything: a chalk-stub, a notched tally-stick, a half-written report-slip, and a folded instruction-card. Read it in layers, like the daylight evidence:

- **Investigation DC 10 — what it is.** Surveillance. Someone's been *logging who visits the stationer's door and the green-coats' office.* The half-written slip carries the party's **own** descriptions — and a prior line: *"watch-coat man — 1 hr — took nothing."* (That's Sarth's recon. The party realizes the firm has been running this lane for days.)
- **Investigation DC 13 — the instruction-card → a *Loftwick* thread.** She leaves her tallies at **a drop in the city** — name a concrete spot you can stake out: *the dead-letter niche behind the loose ashlar at the Coopers' Arms on the Tannerway corner.* *"Marks lifted each Sevenday. Keep nothing."* → an **immediate Phase-2 lead**: watch the drop, trace the courier, brush the chain that also sent Sarth.
- **History/Investigation DC 16 — the routing mark → the *Hardby* thread.** The card bears a small clearing-glyph kin to the **tower-over-waves** (evidence #3 — C&V's internal mark) and the line *"east on the Sevenday cart."* → the reports go to **Hardby.** The watcher is the Loftwick end of a courier chain whose far end is the firm's Hardby hand.
- **Insight / from Reith / if cornered — who she is.** A local hungry kid paid by a stranger via the drop. *She knows nothing above the drop.* (This is why capture yields little — and why the fold, not the girl, is the prize.)

**Two forward pointers, guaranteed:**
1. **Inside Loftwick →** a drop-point to stake out (keeps Act 2 moving under the party's own steam; optionally feeds B-Sarth).
2. **East →** "east on the Sevenday cart" + a C&V-kin routing mark → **Hardby.** *(M5.)*

> **Canon discipline:** the fold names a *route and a firm-mark*, never **"E.,"** never the tube's craft, never a throne or a harbor. It points at the **road**, not the **mastermind.** (See §8.)

### 6.6 Outcomes (every branch lands clean)

| Outcome | How it happens | What the party leaves with |
|---|---|---|
| **A — Clue + escape** *(intended)* | She clears Reach 4 into the under-streets | The fold (both pointers). A fair loss with a real prize, and the firm doesn't yet know its watcher is blown. |
| **B — Clue + corner** | Party drives Lead to 0 *and* spent a resource to follow at Reach 4 | The fold **plus** a terrified kid who confirms only *"a man left coppers at the drop — I never saw his face."* Now a **moral beat** (see below) and the firm's lane-watch is blown — bring Sarth/Moll a touch earlier. |
| **C — Lose both** | Party fails the chase or breaks off | **Failsafe:** the fold dropped at Reach 2 regardless. A fleece-seller returns it that afternoon (*"a runner dropped this — looked official"*), or Caelith's quiet contact does. Motion preserved. |
| **D — Never engaged** | Party ignores the watcher | Her report tips the firm → folds into Moll/Sarth timing; she resurfaces for a second attempt. No dead end. |

**The moral beat (Outcome B).** A cornered Sparrow is not a prisoner worth keeping — she's a hungry sixteen-year-old who watched a door for coppers. What the party *does* with her is the scene's quiet test. **Trina** will take her in without being asked (*"I have a back room and a use for someone who knows the lanes"*); **Caelith** will quietly relocate and pay her, the same way he handles Reith. Roughing her up costs the party nothing mechanically and something tonally — and a watching Trina will remember it. This is the conscience the chase exists to provide.

### 6.7 Variant — the professional (if your table wants teeth)

Swap Sparrow for a **Hardby "leg"** — the adult courier the firm actually runs the chain through (the runner Vesh dispatches). Same four-reach structure, but:
- The escape is a **pre-positioned skiff**, not a kid's culvert; he's armed (smoke pellet, a knife he'll use to *make distance*, not to kill).
- Cornered, he gives a **description that matches "Tarsh's" network** and the Hardby end of the drop — a stronger Phase 3 seed — but **no name** under any roll (he's paid for silence, like Sarth).
- **Trade-off:** you gain menace and lose the conscience beat. Don't run both targets; pick the one your table needs.

### 6.8 Foundry staging notes (for the integration pass)

- **Scene:** *Loftwick — City* (Phase 1 Scenes). Per its own map notes, switch **token vision ON** and add a small set of **street walls** along Wick Lane → Cloth Market → Loft Steps → Tanners' cut → culvert. Place four labeled **reach markers** and one **fold drop** note at Reach 2.
- **Actor:** add *"Sparrow" (Anla Coyle)* to **Phase 2 — Actors** using the 6.2 block (Commoner chassis + Acrobatics/Stealth proficiency + the *Nimble Bolt* feature). Hostile-neutral disposition, mystery-man token.
- **Handout:** the **fold** as a player-facing image/journal entry (chalk-stub, tally, report-slip with the party's own descriptions, the routing card). Mirrors the existing Phase 2 evidence handouts.
- **Optional macro:** a simple Lead-tracker counter card, in the spirit of the Pressure Clock notecard.

## 7. Getting the group back on course (the rails)

One place for every "they've fallen off" move. The philosophy: **the party should never be able to break Phase 2 — only slow it.** Rails come in four kinds.

### 7.1 The stall ladder — when nothing is happening

Escalate gently to firmly. Don't skip rungs; each is an in-fiction event, not a GM lecture.

1. **Soft (a thread tugs).** Trina mentions, in passing, that **Ailen is unprotected in Hardby.** The human stake re-aims a drifting party at action. *(Costs nothing; use first.)*
2. **Clock (the conspiracy moves).** Advance the Pressure Clock one segment for the wasted day and **read its telegraph** (§7.3). Each segment hands the party a fresh thing to react to — *the clock is your primary anti-stall tool, because every tick is a new scene.*
3. **Firm (an ally pushes).** Caelith pins the brass token on the map and says the quiet part: *"At Loftwick speed we lose Tamsin, and we lose Ailen. I would prefer not to."* Trina, harder: *"The thing you are looking for is not in Loftwick. I will push where Caelith will not."*
4. **Hard (collapse a thread to force motion).** Fire a **witness-loss** segment (Pollow dies / Anwic is reassigned / Gilst's room is cleaned). Losing a thread they were dawdling on re-prices urgency instantly.

### 7.2 Per-skip recoveries — when they miss a place or a beat

| If the party… | It's fine — here's the recovery |
|---|---|
| **Skips Gilst's rooms entirely** | The Hardby chop (M3) is also carried by Vesh's "Hardby work" line and the chase fold's routing mark. The smoking gun can resurface later: Caelith's contact recovers the bank instrument from the room before it's cleaned and forwards it. |
| **Skips Tannerway / Tamsin** | Trina carries the stakes: she raises Ailen and the Sevenday note unprompted. Sgt Anwic can bring the Moraven file to Caelith. M5 still lands. |
| **Never triggers the chase** | The watcher tails them home and tips the firm (folds into Moll/Sarth). The Hardby pointer is redundant with five other vectors. Nothing is gated behind the fold. |
| **Catches the watcher instantly** (Outcome B) | The fold is the payload; you lose nothing. Run the moral beat; bring the squeezes a little early. |
| **Accepts Moll's bribe** | Run a downtime session. Three weeks later Caelith arrives: Tamsin surfaced dead, Vellin and Ailen are gone, Pollow is dead, Caelith is being recalled. The conspiracy used the time the party didn't. Most tables re-engage hard. *(Pull: Pitfalls → §8.)* |
| **Tries to ride straight to Rel Astra, skipping Hardby** | Legitimate — it's Approach 3. Tell them the cost *in Caelith's voice* first: no Tamsin window, no "Tarsh," no Veska Maelan, no Castrian Vell; Phase 3 gets much harder. Then give them the riders. |
| **Tries to confront a senior partner / the firm's seniors now** | They're in Rel Astra (Phase 4) behind a bodyguard chain. The Loftwick satellite (Vesh) genuinely *cannot* open client accounts — that wall is real and in-fiction, not GM fiat. |
| **Splits the party across both hubs** | Offer the hubs as *options for the day*, not "split now." If they insist, call a **midday working lunch** in the Refectory to re-pool (the +1 clock for the lost half-day is the cost). Same coverage, one table. |

### 7.3 The clock as a rail — telegraph table

Track it openly (a 6-segment card at the table edge). Announce the trigger when you advance. Each milestone is a *gift of momentum*:

| Seg | In-fiction telegraph (the morning of) | What it re-aims |
|---|---|---|
| 1 — Inventory tampering | Caelith: an item in the chest moved *one inch* overnight. *"Someone was looking."* | Re-energizes; proves the cellar isn't safe. |
| 2 — Anonymous warning | Trina lays the note on the table: *"The neighbor is mistaken."* | The conspiracy knows their hosts. Personal. |
| 3 — Public rumor | A broadsheet at midday; the cook is laughing; by tea-time the party is recognizable. | Visibility now has a price (sets up Moll). |
| 4 — Witness loss | Cut the thread that hurts most given what they've invested in. | Converts dawdling into urgency. |
| 5 — Council hearing | Elsbet hands Caelith the Closure card. He reads it standing. *"The motion is filed."* | 48-hour fuse — pushes them to the gate. |
| 6 — Closing event | GM-only; closes the strongest still-open thread. | Phase 3 begins under worse conditions. |

**Over-run (hit 5 with threads still open):** have Caelith *burn a favor* to buy 24 hours (*"I have paid for one more morning. Use it."*), or cut straight to the Three Approaches — the gate *is* the pressure release.
**Under-run (still at 1 going into the last session):** trigger Moll early with a clerk and a quiet threat; have Trina recommend departure; have Caelith pin the map.

### 7.4 The three-clue guarantee (the deepest rail)

This is the rail that means you rarely need the others. **Every must-know (M1–M5) has 3+ independent sources** (§3). Track them like a checklist: as long as the party has hit *any one* source per row by the gate, the phase is whole. If a row is short at the gate, §3's note tells you which late line supplies it. You will almost never reach the gate with a gap — that's the point of building it this way.

## 8. Canon guardrails (the never-name list)

The rework changes flow, **not** secrets. Hold these exactly as the existing journals do:

- **"E." is never identified.** Not a name, not a species, not a faction. The party collects the initial (Gilst's countersignatures; the initials list) and leaves with an open question. *(Eclavdra of House Eilserv — never spoken at the table, this phase or for a long time.)*
- **The tube is "elven, but peculiar" — never "Drow," never "Underdark."** Under any roll, the best identification is the peculiarity itself. *legend lore* returns a fragmentary verse about a people who "walked downward and were forgotten," unplaceable. The word Underdark does not appear in player-facing text through Phase 9.
- **HARBOR BEFORE THRONE is a slogan of intent, not a resolved plan.** No throne, no harbor, no succession named. *(Phase 4.)*
- **The leak in Caelith's office is implied, never pinned.** The inside forgery, the inventory tampering, and the forged Audit Hall seal on Sarth's writ all point inward — but **Caelith will not name a clerk he cannot prove**, and neither do you.
- **The chase fold points at the *road*, not the *mastermind*.** A route and a firm-mark — Hardby, the Sevenday cart, a C&V-kin glyph. Never let it carry "E.," the tube's craft, or a throne.
- **Trina's patron is never named** — not even to Alicia. Insight beyond the wall returns *"I share a teacher; I have not finished my lessons."*

> If you slip on any of these: don't panic-retcon. *"That's what's on the page; there's no other answer in this room."* The campaign survives one slip. (Pull: *DM Brief → Common GM Pitfalls* for the full recoveries.)

## 9. Hand-off to Phase 3 (what the gate sets)

The travel approach the party picks at B-Road is the **single most consequential choice in Phase 2** — it sets the clock Phase 3 runs on. Don't editorialize it as right/wrong; price it and let them choose.

| Approach | Time the firm has had | Tamsin in Phase 3 | Veska Maelan | "Tarsh" |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Official Delegation** (authority) | ~2 weeks | **Confirmed dead** (body 3 days before arrival) | Fled; an apprentice passes her notes | Moved upcountry; paper trail only |
| **Commercial Cover** (cover) *— recommended* | ~1 week | **Alive, moved** to a coastal estate 2 days east | In Hardby, cautious; helps in private | In Hardby; confronted in person |
| **Quiet Pursuit** (distance) | ~3 days | **Alive in Hardby**, a warehouse on Black Cog Lane | In Hardby, fully cooperative | In Hardby, unprepared — easiest |

**The chase clue's payoff in Hardby.** The fold's *"east on the Sevenday cart"* and its drop-network are the Loftwick end of a chain whose Hardby end the party re-encounters in Phase 3 — the same courier infrastructure that runs through **Tarsh's** circles and the C&V Hardby branch. On integration, you can quietly make the Phase 3 drop the *recognizable far end* of the Phase 2 fold: a small, earned *"we've seen this mark before."* (Keep it a recognition, not a shortcut — it shouldn't skip any Phase 3 content.)

**What Phase 2 must have delivered to Phase 3 (the gate, restated):** M1–M5 (§3). The party leaves *knowing the road, carrying the Hardby hand (the chop), owing a missing woman a rescue, and pursued* — which is exactly the running start Phase 3 expects.

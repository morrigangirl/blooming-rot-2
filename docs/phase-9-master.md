# Phase 9 — Below the Clean Paper — Master GM Walkthrough

*A spoon-fed run guide. Read top to bottom; the order on this page is the order at the table.*

---

## What this phase is for

Phase 9 is the campaign's first descent into the under-road. The party crossed the threshold beneath Tarnsmere at the end of Phase 8 and now arrives at **Witness Station Seven** — what the route's own paperwork calls the **First Under-Road Waystation**. The phase has four jobs:

1. Show the players that **the surface conspiracy's paperwork has a basement**, and that the basement is older than the conspiracy. Every surface name they've gathered has been fed into a procedural machine that has been running for centuries.
2. Convert **Haskur Vandrell from villain into cargo**. His route filing reads *"recoverable but unreliable."* He is not the operation's center. He never was.
3. Make the party experience **route language** as the only currency that matters below — tally marks, witness tokens, escort authority, category declarations. The Black Ledger Gate at the end of the phase checks what category they've made themselves into, not who they are.
4. Hand them the **Lower Route Fork** — three corridors marked Recovery, Escort, Supply — and let them pick which way Phase 10 opens. Or retreat to surface with what they have, which is also a real exit.

Phase 9 is a compact site-trespass interlude. **Three clocks run** (Alert, Record Destruction, Breath and Stone). **DCs cap around 18.** Party tier: **2–3 (levels 8–11).** One to two sessions of tense site play.

**Player-facing language constraint, locked.** Do not use the word *Underdark*. Do not name Eclavdra, House Eilserv, or the Vault. Do not confirm "E." as non-human. The route uses its own vocabulary — *below, under-road, deep road, lower route, descent, witnessed road* — and so should you.

## Location-by-location, in order

Phase 9 is a single-site interlude, not a scene sequence. After L1, the chambers are reachable in any order; follow the party. The order below is roughly the order play will take.

### L1 — Final Surface Landing (the eighth landing)

**Read the route-neutral Opening read-aloud** ("The stair widens almost imperceptibly... You count your landings. Seven so far below the threshold. The eighth is wider still..."). Then layer in branch-specific text for Openings B/C/D/E/F if applicable.

Three beats to land: the masonry shift from Aerdy civic stone to older black-seamed work; smoke that hangs instead of rises; and **the first tally row** — six vertical cuts in three pairs, each crossed by a small horizontal, with an empty token socket below. No password. *A system.*

Above the archway: **Handout 1 — "Accounts above. Witness below."** Same phrase as the Phase 8 Permission Door, re-cut more deeply within the last month. Investigation DC 13 finds a cleaner boot scuff, a grey-and-bone thread (if Kethren passed), or Garden Quarter boot polish (if Haskur did). Survival DC 14 estimates 2–5 people moved through in the last fortnight.

If anyone took Caelith's note literally and speaks their names aloud here, **note it.** The route records names whether the PC offers them or not.

### L2 — Account Hall (and L3 — Witness Registry Wall)

The site's largest chamber. 50 by 35 feet. Three lamps at half-brightness. Seven counter-bays of dark dressed stone — token slots, tally columns above, no chairs, no inkwells, no paper. *Nothing soft anywhere in this room.*

The seven bays: surface arrivals; witness intake; **recovery intake (where Haskur was processed)**; cargo passage; courier transit (Kethren's mark fits); escort declaration; supply receipt (the Blooming Rot crates were logged here).

**Investigation DC 14** at Bay 3 finds the H.V.A. transfer line: *"Recovered cargo. Escort: K. Witness pending. Recoverable but unreliable. Surface name attached: Andren."* This is the moment the floor tilts. Haskur is filed here as cargo with a courier.

**Insight DC 15** lands the design horror: *the slots are not for keys; they are for declarations. What you put in the slot is what you are.*

The long left-hand wall (L3, physically part of the hall) is the **Witness Registry Wall**. Investigation DC 15 or a wax impression of the H.V.A. column yields **Handout 2 — Witness Tally Fragment.** Includes Andren's column with "conditional unreliable" outcome; two halfling-cousin entries circled in chalk (*Cam's payoff*); and at the column's head a **shallow arc with three notches** — the higher-authority mark, unfamiliar. *Do not name it.* The H.V.A. column is scraped clean at Record Destruction 4 — if clocks are climbing, prioritise this room.

The Correction encounter fires here at Alert 4. Counters give half-cover; lamp brackets at the corners can be shattered for spilled-oil fire; south-wall side ducts (3 ft wide, DC 13 Dex as a bonus action) admit Medium creatures to the cistern slit.

### L4 — Toll-Token Cage

A small alcove off the Account Hall. Iron-grilled cabinet of working tokens — descent, witness, escort, recovery, blanks, plus one damaged lower-route token (Smith's/Tinker's Tools DC 15 to repair). At Record Destruction 3, cleaners have stripped most witness tokens. **This is the toolbox for the gate methods.** Whatever the party plans to declare at L13, the matching token comes from here or from the Warden Post box.

### L5 — Old Route Office

Stone desk, wax tablet stand. **Handout 3 — Conversion Note** sits there as two side-by-side artifacts: V. Korre's signed surface directive (*"Andren cleared for recovery; escort assigned (K.); deliver below..."*) and the route clerk's compressed shorthand summary with annotations Korre did not write (*"Andren unreliable. Escort carries second page."*).

The juxtaposition is the point. Same content, two languages, one obligation. **Korre's signature is real, written, and signed.** If the party ever puts this in front of Caelith or Iren, it is the cleanest single proof in the campaign that surface authorities feed the route.

### L6 — Sleeping Alcoves

Eleven carved bunks, none occupied. One alcove has a **panic prayer** scratched in tiny Saint Cuthbert script — *"not witnessed... not witnessed... not witnessed..."* — by an earlier captive, not Merrit. Religion DC 13 places the prayer. **Use this lightly.** It tells the party there are more Merrits than the one they will meet.

### L7 — Supply Cache

Stacked stone crates. Most are routine route supplies; a handful are wax-sealed, seed-and-wax-marked, holding **Blooming Rot crop and tissue samples**. One bears a stone disc on grey twine — **Handout 4 — Recovery Crate Tag**:

- *"Sample code: HRD-87-3. Recovery. Damage confirmed: yes (early stage). **Claim precedes loss: yes. Surface panic acceptable: yes.**"*
- *"Bonded claim reference: CV-487/H-220. Surface processor: Cindren & Vhal (Hardby branch). Route authorisation: [Kethren's mark + deeper-authority mark]."*

**This is the document that proves Blooming Rot was planned recovery, not reactive.** "Claim precedes loss" is fraud in surface courts. "Surface panic acceptable" tells the party the conspiracy *measured* the public's fear. Investigation DC 15 cross-references CV-487/H-220 to Doman receipts from Phase 2. At Record Destruction 5 the crate has been moved through the gate — Handout 4 unrecoverable; the party assembles the recovery logic from circumstantial cues.

### L8 — Burn Furnace

A stone brazier in a wall niche; a clerk's basket of surface-paper slips waiting to be fed. At Record Destruction 2, the **"Andren"** slip is in the queue — roughly ten in-fiction minutes until burning. Investigation DC 14 extracts it: **Handout 5 — Furnace Scrap.** Side A: *Andren*. Side B: ***"Blooming was proof. Rot was proof. Recovery was the question."***

**Thematic centerpiece of the phase.** Hold the beat. The line was written by a route clerk who knew it would never be read by an outsider — they wrote it anyway. Insight DC 16 catches the editorial gloss. **The hand is different from every other route hand in this station.** That clerk is not currently in residence. Phase 10 ally seed.

Securing the Burn Furnace (subduing the cleaner, smothering the brazier, lifting the basket out of reach) pauses Record Destruction advances — a major clock intervention.

### L9 — Sealed Witness Cell

Iron grate, stone bench, wax-sealed water slot in the door. **Merrit Osk** is held here if you're using her — Human, mid-thirties, Greyhawk Free Quarter, three weeks in custody after an "interview" with *a man in grey-and-bone clothing whose face I do not remember*. (Face-erasure is real, not protection. Religion DC 17 / Arcana DC 16 notes this kind of selective amnesia is rare.)

She knows: surface names are burned or converted; Kethren-type couriers outrank surface handlers; **"recovery" means a planned retrieval after controlled failure** (she is the only NPC at the station who will say this in surface words); the next gate leads to a larger route, not a settlement.

Persuasion DC 13 plus paper and pencil yields **Handout 7 — The Unclaimed Witness Statement**: *"They asked my name above. Below they asked what I proved... My mother is in the Free Quarter. She lives at fourteen Cooper's Lane."* Let it land.

With her consent, Merrit can be declared the substitute witness at the gate (Method A becomes much cleaner). Declaring her *without* consent is offering a living person to the gate as cargo — the party should feel it. Brought to surface, she is one of the strongest assets they can produce from Phase 9 — a first-person account of route procedure that has actually reached open air.

### L10 — Cistern Slit

Old water channel from the original mining cut; the route adapted it for sound-control and emergency egress. Two reasons to know it:

- **Method G at the gate (Bypass).** The slit runs past the gate's foundation; about 80 ft east-southeast crawl. Athletics DC 14 to drop in, two air-pocket sections (DC 14 / Con DC 13), tampered east-end grate (Investigation DC 13). Wet equipment degrades paperwork unless wrapped in waxed cloth from the Supply Cache (Survival DC 13).
- **Hazard B (Cistern Pulse).** At Breath and Stone 3+, pressure surges — DC 14 Dex or be knocked prone and pushed 10 feet downstream.

### L11 — Under-Road Marker Gallery

Corridor connecting the Account Hall to the Warden Post and the gate. Both walls bear route marks — categories, transfer kinds, branch indicators, the deeper-authority mark in several places. Where the party first sees the **three branch motifs** that will reappear at the Fork: recovery (hook with three dots), escort (Kethren's mark + seven cuts), supply (square-and-X + seed-and-wax). At Alert 3 some marks are draped with cloth; uncovering them advances Record Destruction +1.

### L12 — Warden Post

Savax's office. Slit window overlooks the Account Hall — he can see and signal from cover. Investigation DC 14 on the desk finds **Handout 6 — Warden Instruction Strip.** Six standing instructions; two matter most:

- **Item 5.** *"If trespassers descend, correct category before removal. Lethal force is authorised only after correction is offered and refused. Surface intruders to be classified as: surface witness, corrected trespasser, recovered asset, or cargo. The classification is yours."* **The route's default is to classify, not to kill** — this is why Method E at the gate works.
- **Item 6.** *"Defer all questions of higher authority to channel beyond Black Ledger Gate. The route's deeper figures will respond to formally filed queries; do not pursue informally."* **The deeper authority operates through formal channels only.** The party cannot reach "E." by knocking. They have to file paperwork.

Also here: the **route bell** and signal cord. Disable the bell (Thieves' Tools DC 15) or the cord (Sleight of Hand DC 15) to prevent one Alert advance.

### L13 — Black Ledger Gate

The lower exit. The campaign's second great threshold, asking **what are you below?**

A darker stone gate set flush, no handle on the party's side. Three token grooves at chest height; a witness tally cut at the lower-left (sized for Handout 2); three marker slots across the upper face. The scraped lintel inscription:

> *Unwitnessed cargo waits. Witnessed passage continues.*

That is **Handout 9** and the gate's policy statement. The gate does not check identity. It checks **route categories**. The party declares one.

| Method | Requires | Alert | Records | Breath/Stone | Phase 10 carry |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| A — Witness | Tally Fragment + 2 tokens + spoken declaration | 0 | 0 | 0 | Cleanly, expected arrival |
| B — Escort (Kethren) | Kethren alive, present, cooperating | 0 | 0 | 0 | Next station expects party; Kethren's leverage grows |
| C — Warden (Savax) | Savax cooperating or bargained | 0 | 0 | 0 | Party classified under whatever Savax declared |
| D — Forged Tally | Cast/repaired token + wax impression + plausible declaration | 0–1 | 0–1 | 0 | Route audit catches forgery in 1–2 days |
| E — False Category | Plausible category + token + Persuasion/Deception 15–18 | 0–1 | 0 | 0 | Runs Phase 10 under self-assigned category |
| F — Force | Athletics 24 / mason work / *Knock* | +2 | 0 | +2 | Backlash 4d8 thunder; Tally Sentinel wakes; active pursuit |
| G — Bypass (Cistern) | Located the slit; waxed cloth for paperwork | 0–1 | 0 | 0–1 | Most invisible; route oblivious |

**The gate does not dead-end the adventure.** Mild failures cost nothing immediately; serious failures sound a signal and lock one method; catastrophic failures fire a half-strength Backlash (2d8 thunder). The party will get through. The question is what they pay. If the table stalls: re-read the inscription; cue an untried category; offer Method G if they haven't found the cistern; give an Insight or History advantage roll on a phrase they overheard earlier.

**Method F wakes the Tally Sentinel** — a mute four-foot stone construct standing dormant in the antechamber niche, the deeper-authority mark on its tablet face. *It does not attack first.* If a PC drops to 0 HP within 5 feet, it presses its tablet to that PC's forehead as a reaction. The PC takes no damage and is **marked**. The mark persists indefinitely and is detectable by Detect Magic. **Note which PCs get witnessed.** Phase 10 may make it matter.

### L14 — Lower Route Fork

The chamber beyond the gate. Three corridors lead away. Each lintel bears one of the branch motifs (**Handout 8 — Lower Route Marks**, sketched or wax-impressed before committing): left **recovery** (Haskur's route), centre **escort** (Kethren's route), right **supply** (Blooming Rot logistics).

The party can also **turn back here.** Outcome G (Retreat with Proof) is a fully supported ending; do not punish it.

**Read the Closing Read-Aloud** the moment the party either steps into a corridor or steps back toward the gate. ("The gate has closed behind you... they did not build a road beneath the world. They built a way to decide who was allowed to matter below it.") Let the closing line land alone. Do not speak. Let a player speak first.

## NPCs to know in Phase 9

- **Route Warden Savax.** New. The local procedural administrator. CR 5 (Bandit Captain base, adjusted; HP 75, AC 16). **Not the mastermind. Not a villain.** Career clerk who believes unauthorised passage is contamination. Pale, gloved, ageless in a way that resists surface markers. **Do not confirm his species in player-facing text.** Eyes that avoid open torch flame. Silence around the feet. Removes one glove when uncertain, then puts it back. He will speak before fighting and offers dialogue mid-combat. He values records above his life and continuity above revenge. Persuadable, deceivable, bargainable. Useful lines: *"Names are for surfaces." "If you are not witnessed, you are cargo." "I am the warden here. I am not the authority. I have been instructed."*
- **Merrit Osk — The Unclaimed Witness.** New. Human Greyhawk-Free-Quarter clerk, three weeks held. Commoner with **Clerk's Eye** (reads route shorthand at DC 13 instead of 15). The phase's emotional anchor and a real mechanical asset.
- **Silent Courier.** New, generic. CR 3 (Scout Captain base; HP 66, AC 14, climb 30, Disengage as bonus action, **Route-Mask** for darkvision and difficult-terrain ignore). Carries a sealed dispatch — capture alive is a major intelligence prize. Fights only to clear an escape; jumps into the cistern slit if cornered.
- **Tally Sentinel.** New construct. CR 4 (Animated Armor variant; HP 33, AC 18, blindsight 60, immune poison/psychic). Dormant in the gate antechamber niche; wakes on Method F or Alert 5. Witnessing reaction described above.
- **Route-Cleaners.** Re-used Phase 7/8 Spy archetype, CR 1. Three to five present depending on Alert. **They prioritise destroying evidence over killing.** A cleaner will accept death to feed one more item to the fire.
- **Under-Road Scout.** Re-used Phase 7 Silent Scout statblock, CR 1/2. Watches, signals, flees.
- **Haskur Vandrell** (conditional). State A: diminished from his Phase 6 statblock — CR 4, HP 50, AC 13, his most aggressive spells unavailable. He has been *altered.* State B: moved on; encounterable in Phase 10 down the Recovery Route. State C: filed as "failed recovery; substituted witness."
- **Kethren Ilvath** (conditional). State A: most useful, most expensive companion. CR 4, HP 65, AC 16, Cunning Action, Route-Mask, Evasive. **He fears recall** — he visibly recoils at the word. Method B is cleanest with his cooperation. State B: glimpse only. State C: his mark serves as surface evidence.
- **V. Korre** (conditional). State A: out of jurisdiction; valuable as a translator, not as combat. Her papers are formally void below. She helps in exchange for one promise — *get her back to the surface alive.*

Statblocks for new NPCs in `packs/_source/phase-9-actors/`. Returning NPCs use prior-phase entries.

## How Phase 9 ends

The trigger is **the party's commitment at the Lower Route Fork** — into a corridor, or back toward the gate. Read the Closing Read-Aloud at that moment. Hand out any remaining earned handouts. Let the table sit with the artifacts. Then choose the appropriate **Outcome Package (A–H)** from the Endings journal and read its summary aloud. End the session.

Outcomes stack. Most parties exit as some combination of A or B (clean opening with or without alarm), one of C/D/E (chosen branch), and possibly H (prisoner complication).

| Outcome | Trigger | Phase 10 opening |
|---|---|---|
| A — Quiet Witness | Method A/B/E, Alert 0–2, Records 0–2 | Cleanly; route oblivious. |
| B — Evidence Rescued, Alarm Raised | Clean opening but Alert 3–4 | Evidence in hand; pursuit prepared at next station. |
| C — Haskur Trail Followed | Recovery Route chosen | Phase 10 follows Haskur's processing. |
| D — Kethren Route Followed | Escort Route chosen | Phase 10 reveals route culture. |
| E — Supply Route Followed | Supply Route chosen | Phase 10 reveals operational mechanics. |
| F — Forced Gate | Method F | Under pursuit; Tally Sentinel may have witnessed PCs. |
| G — Retreat with Proof | Party declines to cross | Surface return; political consequences phase. |
| H — Prisoner Complication | Major NPC alive in custody crossing the gate | Phase 10 must manage the prisoner alongside other goals. |

**Outcome G is fully supported.** Caelith's warning applies: *"Proof has weight only if someone survives to use it."* The descent can be re-entered later, with allies — though the route may have moved on by then.

## Branches & contingencies

- **The party never opens the gate.** Outcome G. Caelith's note explicitly preserves the option. Phase 10 begins on surface.
- **The party forces the gate.** Run the Backlash hazard. Wake the Tally Sentinel. **Note which PCs get witnessed** — the mark is permanent.
- **The party "wins" the Correction by talking.** Savax accepts a category declaration, even falsely, and processes them (Method C). Phase 10's next waystation receives them under whatever category Savax recorded.
- **The party kills Savax.** Method C is unavailable; Record Destruction advances faster; the route audits more aggressively.
- **The party leaves Merrit behind.** Note it. In Phase 10, refer back: *the panic prayer in the Sleeping Alcoves was by another person like Merrit.*
- **The party brings Merrit out alive.** Major Phase 10 asset. Her statement is one of the only first-person accounts of route procedure to reach surface.
- **The party brings Haskur, Kethren, Korre, or Savax across the gate.** Outcome H. The route will want the prisoner back; surface authorities will want them too; the party is between competing claims.
- **The party captures a Silent Courier alive.** The sealed dispatch in their writing case names the courier's destination and the category of business. Investigation DC 14 / History DC 16. Major Phase 10 intelligence.
- **A PC speaks their names aloud at the eighth landing** (per Caelith's note). Note it. The route records them either way.
- **A PC is witnessed by the Tally Sentinel at 0 HP.** Permanent. Route personnel recognise them on sight; route gates respond differently to them; allied surface clerics or arcanists can read the mark.
- **The party damages the waystation significantly** (Breath and Stone 5+). Cascading collapses. The party's retreat route may be impassable; they may be forced to commit to the under-road when they would have preferred to surface. **Cascading failures** (Alert 6 + Breath and Stone 6 simultaneously) give 15–30 in-fiction minutes to commit deeper or evacuate under fire.
- **The party tries to communicate with Caelith from below.** They cannot. The route does not carry surface messages. First contact happens only on return or through Outcome G.

## Carry forward

Phase 9 is the latest shipped (v1.1.0; portrait/token polish in v1.1.1). There is no Phase 10 yet, but the source files explicitly seed it. Make a private note of:

- **Gate method used.** A/B/C/E (clean) → expected arrival at next station. D (forged) → route auditing irregularity. F (forced) → active pursuit. G (bypass) → route oblivious.
- **Final clock values.** Alert determines next-station hostility; Record Destruction determines evidence brought out; Breath and Stone determines whether the waystation is intact behind them.
- **Which corridor was chosen at the Fork**, or whether the party retreated (Outcome G).
- **Companion NPC status.** Haskur, Kethren, Korre, Merrit, Savax, captured Silent Courier — alive, dead, with the party, or with the route.
- **Witnessed PCs.** Anyone the Tally Sentinel marked carries it forward indefinitely.
- **The higher-authority mark.** Players have seen the shallow arc with three notches at the H.V.A. column's head, on the Recovery Crate Tag, in the Marker Gallery. **Do not name "E." or House Eilserv.** Players know the mark, not the figure.
- **The clerk with a conscience.** The hand on the Furnace Scrap's Side B is different from every other route hand the party saw. That clerk is not in residence. Phase 10 ally seed.
- **Caelith's standing on the Yeomanry Council** (contested by Phase 9 per Phase 5 events). Phase 9 evidence brought back via Outcome G may restore him or end his career; the Council's read makes it political.
- **Iren and Aldea on the surface.** The Conversion Note turns Korre's signature from rumor into evidence. Iren can hold captured route personnel as "persons of investigative interest" without disclosing the under-road. Quiet suppression is possible; public prosecution is not.
- **The Guild of Miners and Jewelers.** Institutionally denies the under-road. Vetch may privately confirm. Phase 10–12 storyline.
- **Cindren & Vhal residuals.** CV-487/H-220 connects bonded claims to deliberate crop damage. C&V has several weeks of grace before they realise their surface paperwork is exposed.
- **The deeper authority is not encountered.** Not named, not seen, not heard. The party leaves knowing it exists, knowing its mark, knowing it operates above Korre, Sereth, and the C&V residuals — and not knowing who it is. *There is no boss fight. There is paperwork.*
- **Per-PC light touches.** Kitty's chthonic recognition is louder below than above; Cam confirms two more halfling cousins from the registry wall; Gianni completes the geographic-erasure hypothesis; Selvara's dream-direction registers the three corridors as three qualities of silence; Alicia's pact-mark goes from cold to *reading* (something here has noticed her, not her silent patron); Elle may receive a brief letter from her abbess slipped in with route-cleaner papers; for PCs without a tracked thread, use the closing-fork beats in the Endings journal.

The party leaves Phase 9 with the conviction the campaign has been building toward: **respectable surface institutions have been feeding people and information into a system they pretend does not exist.** They walked it. They are carrying it. They have a category now too.

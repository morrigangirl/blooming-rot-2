# Phase 6 — The Brass Crow — Master GM Walkthrough

*A spoon-fed run guide. Read top to bottom; the order on this page is the order at the table.*

---

## What this phase is for

Phase 6 is the campaign's Greyhawk City investigative phase, and the first of the expansion phases past the five-phase main arc. The party has followed the conspiracy's information chain into the Free City, and has finally reached the living trail of **Haskur Vandrell** — the man they killed in the wilderness before Phase 1, who was pulled back through a contingency. Phase 6 closes that mystery and replaces it with a bigger one. Four jobs:

1. **Confirm Haskur is alive** in Greyhawk under the alias **H. V. Andren**. End the question of whether he survived. Begin the question of who pays for him.
2. **Identify V. Korre** — Veyra Korre — as the Greyhawk handler. She is the surface operator, not the mastermind. She is the kind of person the conspiracy is willing to lose.
3. **Surface the younger man — Kethren Ilvath — without naming what he is.** The table should feel he is wrong for the surface without being told the answer. *Do not say drow. Do not say Underdark.*
4. **Plant "the Seventh Stair."** A route marker, not a metaphor. Iren Velash, the Garden Quarter manuscript dealer, reads it as a *descent term* — older than the city, not on any port chart. This is the seed of Phase 7.

Phase 6 is node-based. Six locations; any three converge on the climax. **Combat is possible but not the default.** The climactic encounter is optional. **DCs cap at 18 (investigation) and 17 (social).** Party tier: **2 / early 3 (levels 8–11).**

The phase climaxes at the **eleventh-bell transfer in the Old Stonecistern**, a disused civic cistern beneath a derelict bathhouse. *This is not the Underdark. It is Greyhawk infrastructure. It feels like a rehearsal for descent without being one.*

Decide these before play:

- **Which Phase 5 vector the party took.** A (Greyhawk Direct), B (through Aldea Veren's wood cottage), C (Counter-Leak through Quill), or D (Rel Astra legal continuation through Halask). Openings differ; the main body is identical.
- **The full carry-forward state** — Sereth's path (A indicted / B privately removed / C kinetically removed), Closure motion (passed / held over / defeated), Pell's fate, Quill's outcome, and which of Lies 1–5 was fed up. - **What you will *not* say.** Phase 6 must not deliver: the full conspiracy, the identity of "E.", the names *Eclavdra*, *Eilserv*, *Vault*, *Underdark*, *Demonweb*, *Lolth*; confirmation that the "E." writer is non-human; or the final defeat of Haskur. *Phase 6 is the door, not the room.*

## Scene-by-scene, in order

Phase 6 is node-based, not strictly linear, but it has a fixed shape: vector opening, first-day choice tree, three or more nodes (in any order), then the cistern climax.

### Scene 1 — Arrival in Greyhawk

The party crests the West Gate bridge above the Selintan, late afternoon, regardless of vector. Read the **Arrival read-aloud** from the journal. Closing beats are doctrine for the phase: *the city is too large to care whether you are right; money is the only language anyone uses without an accent; you are being noticed, but not yet by name.*

Then play the **vector opening** matching the party's Phase 5 choice. Each is a single short scene.

- **Variant A — Greyhawk Direct.** Cleanest. Three days north of the Yeomanry; the West Gate takes a coin per PC.
- **Variant B — Wood Correspondent.** Stop at Aldea Veren's slate-roofed cottage. *Two states*: Aldea present (she gives three professional observations — the wax recipe-family, the trained-but-unused hand, and *"one of the geographic phrasings is wrong by a long mile. The author writes as if their first geography was below ground and they are guessing at distances above it"*) or Aldea vanished (only if Lie 2 was used — the cottage is empty, a corner of paper in the hearth reads *"…ne return passage … via Greyhawk … Kestrel & Reed …"*). Convergence target either way: Iren Velash.
- **Variant C — Counter-Leak.** Open in Loftwick after the Council vote. *Two states*: Quill captured (he names **Kestrel & Reed, Foreign Letters, Hawkstone Way** as his paymaster; was to report there for re-tasking) or Quill escaped (the party has only the receipt and a partial route, naming **Edrik Vone** on the High Quay or K&R).
- **Variant D — Rel Astra Legal Continuation.** A short procedural hearing. Halask hands across a deposition naming a witness-pressure packet whose courier chit lists Greyhawk as origin. He says privately: *"The packet came through Greyhawk. I cannot follow it from here. You can."*

Within two hours of arrival, an indifferent runner delivers **Caelith's Road Note** (Handout 1, variant matching the vector). Unsigned but the hand is unmistakable. Key line in every variant:

> *You will recognize the address by the sign. Watch from across the street. Note who arrives. Note what they wait for. Do not enter on the first day. Do not give names. The wood reply was correct. The boy is not a subordinate. There is a third hand we have not seen.*

**If the party walks into the Brass Crow on day one, advance the Haskur Trail Clock by 1 the same evening.** Caelith warned them.

### Scene 2 — First Day: the choice tree

Four reasonable starting points. **Order does not matter.** Any two primary nodes reveal V. Korre; any three reveal the eleventh-bell transfer.

1. **Tellan Verth** at the Hand of Coals — fast witness; free (no day cost, no Trail Clock).
2. **Watch the Brass Crow** from the cobbler's upper room across Tinker's Way (2 sp/day) — Caelith's recommended first move; free unless the party is seen (Stealth/Deception DC 13).
3. **Iren Velash** in the Garden Quarter — slowest start, deepest payoff. One day.
4. **Edrik Vone** on the High Quay — most expensive start. One day.

A fifth path — **Kestrel & Reed** on Hawkstone Way — is available immediately if the party arrived via Vector C with Quill captured; otherwise it surfaces from Verth, Brass Crow surveillance, or Vone's ledger.

Cheap rooms 5 sp/night; decent 1 gp/night; food 5 sp/PC/day. If the Heat Clock becomes visible, services start costing more and rooms get rented out from under the party.

### Scene 3 — Node 1: Tellan Verth at the Hand of Coals

Sixty, lame, brass-headed cane. Drinks at the Hand of Coals (Foreign Quarter) every evening from the second bell after dusk until the watch passes at the first bell after midnight. He sits at the back-left bench beside the chimney. **He has been waiting.**

When the party sits opposite him he says quietly, *"Are you the ones?"* If they signal Caelith — even obliquely — he closes his eyes and says: **"I have been waiting for you."** Without the signal: Persuasion DC 13 to open him, Insight DC 14 to read his shame.

He gives the account in pieces. Each detail beyond the first three needs a follow-up question, not a re-roll: the man was Haskur Vandrell; entered the Brass Crow at the third bell after noon; the desk clerk repeated the alias **H. V. Andren**; the younger man was already inside, came in from the back alley; grey-and-bone clothing, unfamiliar cut; his gait was wrong — small constant corrections, *"like he was used to walking somewhere narrower"*; he watched the door lintel and the street's reflection in a glassed window, not the people; Haskur listened more than he spoke; Verth could not follow (his knee — he apologises twice). Verth hands the party a folded amended statement (Handout 2), unsigned.

**The complication.** A thin man at the bar is listening — **Tassel**, the Brass Crow runner. Passive Perception 14 notices him; Insight 13 reads his attention. Uncaught, he reports to the desk clerk by the next bell and **the Trail Clock advances by 1**. Shadowed (Stealth DC 13), he runs a circuit ending at **Kestrel & Reed on Hawkstone Way** — yielding that address without entering a primary node openly.

**If Verth is unprotected and the Trail Clock reaches 4+, he is killed in his rooms above the Hand of Coals on the night of the eleventh bell.** The only authorial death in the phase. Protection: Caelith's seal, a Foreign Quarter advocate's letter, or a one-week paid bodyguard.

### Scene 4 — Node 2: The Brass Crow Counting-House

Foreign Quarter, Tinker's Way, two streets north of the main square. Three storeys, brass-bound doors, a brass crow above the lintel. *Caelith's instruction is to watch, not approach, on the first day.*

Four approaches; each has a cost:

| Approach | Method | Trail | Heat |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Social** | Walk in as merchants. Persuasion DC 13 (current week registry), DC 16 (previous month). Aerel Mossen appears if questions get specific; DC 17 or leverage opens him. | +1 | 0 |
| **Surveillance** | From the cobbler's upper room. Observe arrivals, departures, lintel checks, the runner's circuit. | 0 (unless seen) | 0 |
| **Burglary** | Back alley door DC 16; third-floor window from cobbler's roof DC 15 + DC 14 latch; cellar drain from the cistern side (reverse access, late only). | +1 clean / +2 messy | +1 if Watch summoned |
| **Official** | Yeomanry papers. Aerel cooperates publicly and warns Korre within the hour. | +2 | +1 |

Clues from the registry and strong drawer (Handout 3 = registry page):

- **H. V. Andren** in the current registry, third entry of last week. Beside it, a **foreign-correspondent channel side mark** — a vertical stroke crossed at three-quarter height. Appears beside no other client.
- A **payment note** in the strong drawer ties Andren's account to **Kestrel & Reed**.
- A **time notation** on the back: *"11th bell, rain door."*
- A separate slip in the proprietor's hand: *"second page not burned."* Aerel was paid extra to file it without filing it.

Staff: **Wenna Roost** (23, desk clerk, helpful if treated kindly; straightens the inkwell when afraid; *protect her or she vanishes from Greyhawk by phase end*). **Aerel Mossen** (50s, proprietor, cooperative with leverage; warns Korre if Yeomanry papers appear). **Tassel** on circuit.

The third-floor guest room is **Haskur's**. His Aerdy travel coat in the wardrobe has a list of names sewn into the lining (one is Korre, one is "K. I.", two are unknown — one a Greyhawk forger, one a **Hardby notary**, the Phase 7 hook).

### Scene 5 — Node 3: Velash Manuscripts (the keystone reading)

Garden Quarter, sign is a black book on dark green. Shutters half-closed. **The only Phase 6 location that is genuinely safe for the party.**

**The pass-phrase is "the seventh question of the third volume."** Caelith taught Iren this in 568 CY. Said inside her shop with an honest face: closed shutters, locked door, a kettle. Without it she will still read materials (25 gp per item, no negotiation), but won't share her own observations.

**If the party brings multiple items at once, she produces a triangulated reading more useful than the sum of its parts. This is the keystone of the phase. Reward this.** Written analysis (Handout 5) the next day. Closing line: *"Do not bring this analysis back to my shop. Read it and burn it."* She means it — bringing it back is +1 Heat the next day.

What she reads: **Korre's hand** — *"She wrote in this hand because someone showed her how. She did not learn it from a master"* — imitates Aerdy commercial-court shorthand badly. **"E." marginalia** — *"This is a hand I cannot place in any tradition I know."* **Wax flake** — matches Aldea's "unknown recipe-family," third such she has read. **Aldea's reply / Haskur's false papers** — incremental; identifies a Greyhawk forger (reachable through Vone).

**The keystone — "Seventh Stair."** She goes to the locked paper press, returns with a thin volume, dust on the gilt:

> *"Of the route-marks set in old workings, those that descended past use were marked by their step-count: the First, the Third, the Seventh. The Seventh, where it appears, is reserved for the deeper sort, that connect to passages no longer surveyed by guild or crown. It is unwise to enter the Seventh Stair without a guide who has been there before. The author of this glossary did not return from his."*

She closes the book, looks up, says:

> *"This is not a sea-route term. It is a descent term. I do not know what is below your Seventh Stair, but the route is older than the city. You will not find it on a port chart."*

**She will not say *Underdark*.** She will not name the deep organisation. She has not read these things in ink and does not guess under her own roof.

Iren's price isn't money — she wants copies of everything the party finds. If they promise her copies, she halves her rate.

### Scene 6 — Node 4: Edrik Vone's Private Archive

High Quay, second floor, near the customs house. Forties, fastidious, former imperial customs official dismissed for selling certified cargo lists. *Spy* (CR 1). **Tell:** lays his pen perpendicular to the paper when lying.

Vone listens, sets a price, **does not negotiate in the same conversation**. Access:

| Ledger | Base | Lie 1 used | Path C used | Yeomanry shown |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Regular | 50 gp | –25 | +50 | +100 or refusal |
| Irregular | 200 gp | –50 | +100 | +100 or refusal |

Other leverage: blackmail of a customs colleague (–50%); promise of protection (substitutes for half irregular); frightening evidence (–25%, risk: Vone flees by morning).

**Regular ledger** clues: three packets per month on consistent timing to rotating Foreign Quarter rooms, recipient initial cycling *V. K. / V. Korre / K—*, all from **Kestrel & Reed**.

**Irregular ledger** clues (Handout 4):

- **V. Korre** in hand-altered form (*Korre* → *V. K.* → *K—*). Annotation: *"repeat custom, monthly address."*
- Korre's **current monthly address**: a rented room above a bakery on **Tannerway**, two streets east of the Brass Crow.
- A packet marked **H.V.A.** redirected to the Brass Crow last week; annotation: *"diverted by hand, see K&R counterpart entry."*
- A pending pickup scheduled **eleventh bell two nights from now** at *"old bath, Tinker's Way side."* Cancelled if Trail Clock past 4.

Vone refuses to discuss what these clients matter for, who Korre's principal is, "H.V.A.", or the Seventh Stair.

### Scene 7 — Node 5: Kestrel & Reed, Foreign Letters

Hawkstone Way, six streets west of the Brass Crow. Run by senior clerk **Doras Kemmel** (40s, Hardby-born, wife and three children in the Garden Quarter). **Framing: K&R is an *intermediary*, not the conspiracy itself.** Treat as turnable, not adversarial.

Approaches:

- **Bribe Doras.** 50 gp for a packet's metadata; 200 gp for a one-time look at the lockbox wall. Privately, away from the office.
- **Forge authorisation.** DC 16, or DC 14 if Iren has read Korre's hand.
- **Break in.** Rear cart court (DC 14 gate, DC 15 back door) or roofline (DC 16 climb, DC 13 ceiling hatch). The lockbox wall: each box DC 17, *one at a time*, and a small bell rings if a box is touched without the desk clerk's knowledge.
- **Shadow the pickup.** Yields Korre's apartment address without ever entering K&R.
- **Quill's deposition.** If captured in Phase 5, gives legal standing to inspect K&R records. **The cleanest entry.**
- **Yeomanry authority.** Immediate Heat Clock advancement; warns the network. Korre changes address that night.

Lockbox wall clues:

- Quill's Loftwick packets came through here. Doras knows Quill's name.
- V. Korre received **summaries of Caelith's office** — legible enough to identify Pell as the source.
- One packet from box 17 is **missing**, picked up this week by *"a young man in grey-and-bone clothing"* (Doras volunteers this unprompted).
- One packet remains in box 17, sealed and **heat-reactive** (Handout 6 — ninety seconds over a candle reveals *"11th bell, rain door. Second page not burned. No sail. No road."*).

**Doras turning.** Offer formal protection (Caelith's seal, an Aerdy advocate's witness slip, or Halask if Vector D), give him 24 hours, and **he turns**. K&R closes temporarily; the party gets Korre's three-month correspondence record and a turnable witness. *He needs to know his family will be safe. The protection has to be real.*

### Scene 8 — Node 6: V. Korre's Monthly Address

Rented room above a bakery on Tannerway, two streets east of the Brass Crow. Side stair from the alley, locked door at the top (DC 14). The bakery side door is paid-off-unlocked from Korre's side; roofline runs three buildings to the cistern's hidden entrance; a 14-inch service drain under the privy is dirty and dangerous.

**State depends on the Trail Clock when the party arrives:**

- **0–2 — present and packing.** Korre with two agents (Mell and Saern), packing one travelling case. Time to flee but not to clean. *Best-case capture.*
- **3–4 — absent but recently there.** Hearth warm, kettle empty, a burned letter strip in the brazier. One agent on the stair warns Korre, who is two streets ahead.
- **5 — bait.** Korre on the roof; two agents inner room; one in the bakery side room. *Worst case.*

Clues regardless of state:

- **Burned letter strips** in the brazier. Legible fragment: *"… clean hands drown first …"*
- **One clean ledger line**: *"A." (Andren)* to be transferred to *"K." (Kethren)* at the eleventh bell, location *"OBT"* — Old Bath, Tinker's.
- **One wax flake** on the desk — Aldea's "unknown recipe-family."
- **Route notation:** a slip with **"VII Stair"**, struck through then rewritten. Strike-through is Korre's hand; rewrite (smaller, more careful) is Kethren's.
- **A charcoal sketch** on a folded paper: seven steps cut under a black hill. Matches the cistern wall mark.
- **A second sealed packet** in the desk drawer, heat-reactive: *"No sail. No road. Andren to be moved before any clerk's question reaches the Watch. Korre to close. K. to follow with the second page."*

**Catching Korre at the apartment (Trail 0–2).** She will not start a fight. Sample lines:

> *"I am being paid to move correspondence. Nothing else."*
> *"I do not know who pays. I receive sealed instructions. The wax is always the same kind. The kind of person who can afford that kind of wax does not write their name."*
> *"The boy is not Andren's man. The boy is the route. I do not know what is at the other end."*
> *"Take me into protection and I will write what I know. Threaten me and I burn the rest."*

Insight DC 14 reads her tell (she is closer to taking the offer than her face suggests). Persuasion DC 16 + concrete protection secures her. **The second-cleanest capture in Phase 6** (after Doras turning).

### Scene 9 — The Eleventh Bell Transfer (climax, Old Stonecistern)

With three primary nodes assembled, the location is clear: **Old Stonecistern**, beneath a derelict bathhouse at the south end of Tinker's Way, three streets past the Brass Crow. Its service drains connect to the Brass Crow's back alley. *Not the Underdark. Greyhawk infrastructure. A rehearsal for descent without being one.*

**Read aloud — approach to the bathhouse:**

> *The bathhouse stands on the south end of Tinker's Way where the street starts to slope toward the river. It has been derelict for a generation. Slate has come off the roof in patches; the front portico has lost two columns and is held up by a leaning wooden brace; the door has been planked shut and is now half-rotten.*
>
> *The alley around the side is narrower than it looks on the surveyors' map. Down a flight of slick stone steps below the portico's broken corner, a low iron door is set into the cellar wall. Above the door, almost flush with the lintel, someone has scratched a small mark in the soot: seven steps cut under a black hill. The mark is fresh.*

**Read aloud — entering:**

> *The iron door yields. Beyond it the air changes: stone-cool, mineral-damp, the faint sweetness of standing water. A short passage, three uneven steps, and then the cistern itself opens out below you.*
>
> *It is larger than expected. The dry platform — old bath tiles, cracked, stained — slopes down to a wet lower channel of green-black water. Light reaches the room only from a ceiling grate to the street far above, where the streetlamp flickers like bad coin on the surface of the water. There is a brazier set in the brick floor at the far end of the platform, lit, the iron sides streaked with old soot.*
>
> *You are not the first ones here this evening.*

**Who arrives at the eleventh bell:** Haskur (carrying the sealed packet — the Ashen Itinerary's pre-burning state); Kethren from the cistern's other side, alone (to brief Haskur, take the "second page," lead Haskur out through the deeper drain); Mell and Saern (to burn what does not travel); and **V. Korre only if both clocks are 0–2** — otherwise she sends a proxy.

**Conspiracy priorities, in order:** burn the packet; move Haskur out through the deeper drain; let Kethren escape with the second page; erase the route mark. **They do not want to fight.** Their goal is to escape clean.

**Approach options:** shadow and observe (Stealth DC 14); ambush (preset the room — surprise round, likely capture); social interruption (one PC reveals themselves, offers Korre a deal in front of the agents); legal seizure (advocate or Watch — conspiracy attempts to flee, packet may burn); magical surveillance (*clairvoyance, scrying*); direct fight (GM's last preference, not first).

If combat is not triggered, the party may shadow Kethren toward the sluice gate and intercept Haskur at the cistern's south side as he's moved toward a courier wagon. **The cleanest capture.**

**Keyed cistern map** (footprint ~70 ft E–W × 40 ft N–S, dry platform south, wet channel north):

1. Rain door (cellar entry, three uneven steps in)
2. Broken bathhouse stair on south wall (DC 12 climb, DC 14 silent descent)
3. Dry platform (raised 18", old tiles, broken columns)
4. Wet channel (knee- to chest-deep, difficult terrain)
5. **Sluice gate** (east end) — iron lattice; Kethren has the key; *the deeper drain*
6. Pipe bridge (24" wide; DC 11 at speed, DC 8 walking)
7. Packet-burning brazier (platform's far end)
8. Maintenance crawl (south wall, west end; DC 12 Athletics)
9. Locked service door to alley (west end of platform; DC 15)
10. Deep water pocket (8 ft, one round to surface)
11. Ceiling grate (only natural light)
12. **Route mark scratched into old stone** (south wall, beside the broken stair)
13. Sealed escape channel (west end — *not tonight's way out*)

**Combat triggers:** PC identified entering with line of sight; PC tries to take the packet without setup; party announces themselves loudly; Kethren sees a PC he recognises.

**Encounter (level 8 baseline; scale up per the Scaling page):**

- **Haskur** — modified Mage (CR 6). HP 64, AC 13. Escape/control magic: *misty step ×2, suggestion, counterspell, fly, dimension door* (one cast left). Pre-cast *mage armor*. **Will not blast.** Uses *dimension door* to escape only below half HP.
- **Kethren** — Deep-Route Courier (CR 5). HP 58, AC 16. Poison needle (DC 13 CON or poisoned 1 min), short blade, *Vanish* 3/day, Defensive Step reaction. Priorities: get the second page from Haskur; burn the packet; escape through the sluice gate. *Describe him as comfortable in dim conditions; do not confirm origin.*
- **Mell** (Spy, CR 1) and **Saern** (Thug, CR 1). *Saern moves directly to the brazier and starts burning the packet within two rounds.*
- **V. Korre** — Spy Master analog (CR 4–5), only if both clocks 0–2. HP 52, AC 14, Cunning Action, Parry reaction. **Does not stand and fight.** *If she is caught with the packet on her person, the party has won the phase.*

**Morale.** Haskur flees if bloodied or commanded. Kethren uses Vanish + sluice gate if below half HP; cannot be caught at the gate without a PC blocking it before round 2. Saern dies for the brazier; Mell does not. Korre flees before dying. **Reward the party for closing exits.**

**Victory conditions** (any one): capture Haskur; capture Kethren; capture Korre; recover the Itinerary intact; recover the route mark (rubbing or sketch); prevent the packet from burning; track the deeper drain even to the next sealed grate.

**Failure conditions and what survives.** Even total tactical failure leaves the route mark on the cistern wall, a wax flake on the platform, a scorched fragment of the Itinerary with *"no sail, no road"* still legible, and Iren's later reading if the party brings her any of these.

**Chase rules.** Each round: fleeing NPC and pursuing PC each roll Athletics or Acrobatics (PC's choice; NPC's better stat). Higher result moves 30 ft toward goal. Hazards apply each round (wet channel DC 11 or prone; pipe bridge DC 11 at speed; sluice gate DC 16 pick or DC 17 force; maintenance crawl DC 12 + weapons larger than shortsword unusable; service door DC 15). **Block one exit — a PC at the sluice gate, the alley door, or the broken stair — and the chase resolves with at least one capture.**

## NPCs to know in Phase 6

Twelve named figures; stat blocks in `packs/_source/phase-6-actors/`. (No separate Phase 6 actor file for Haskur — his Phase 1 block is upgraded inline in the cistern document.)

**Central:**

- **Tellan Verth** — 60, retired Yeomanry sergeant. *Veteran* CR 3. Will not fight unless cornered.
- **Iren Velash** — early 50s, half-Suel manuscript dealer. *Mage Apprentice* CR 2 + Sage. Knows Caelith's pass-phrase. **Stops blinking when genuinely interested.** Will not say *Underdark*.
- **Edrik Vone** — 40s, dismissed customs official. *Spy* CR 1. Bribable, blackmailable, persuadable, or frightenable; one is sufficient, stacking is rude. Tell: pen perpendicular when lying.
- **Veyra Korre** — late 30s/early 40s. *Spy* + Bandit Captain features, effective CR 4–5. **Surface operator, not mastermind.** Tell: touches left wrist with right thumb when about to lie (Insight DC 16). Birth name is not the campaign's payoff.
- **Kethren Ilvath** — young adult, grey-and-bone clothing. Scout/monk-assassin analog, CR 5. **Do not confirm his species in player-facing text.** More afraid of *recall* than death: *"Send me back and I am dead."*
- **Haskur Vandrell** (alias H. V. Andren) — late 40s, modified *Mage* CR 6. Contingency safety **spent**. If captured (Handout 8): *"I was not rescued. I was collected. Korre moves paper. The boy moves doors. Whoever pays them is not in the city."*

**Scaffolding:** **Aerel Mossen** (50s, Brass Crow proprietor, *Spy* CR 1, fee-driven). **Wenna Roost** (23, desk clerk, *Commoner* CR 0 — *protect her or lose her*). **Tassel** (19, runner, *Scout* CR 1/2 — surrenders if cornered without violence). **Doras Kemmel** (40s, K&R senior clerk, *Spy* CR 1 — turns with formal protection). **Mell** and **Saern** (Korre's agents, Spy/Thug, CR 1/2–1; *Saern dies for the brazier*). **Captain Jarn Heshet** (Foreign Quarter Watch, *Veteran* CR 3, appears at Heat 3+; not corrupt, not impressed).

**Carry-forward references** (offstage): Caelith via Road Notes only; Aldea only via Vector B; Halask only via Vector D; Quill only if captured.

## How Phase 6 ends

Four canonical endings:

- **A — Haskur Captured.** Cleanest. Party has Haskur alive, his testimony (Handout 8), confirmation of *collected, not rescued*, the Seventh Stair as a route, and Korre/Kethren as subordinates. Kethren almost certainly escaped through the sluice gate with the second page. Captured publicly: +1 Heat, Greyhawk court takes formal custody, *party loses interrogation access*. Captured privately and removed from Greyhawk: no Heat change, Caelith handles legal cover.
- **B — Haskur Killed.** Party has body, papers, possibly the Itinerary, and the coat-lining list. No testimony. Visibly: +1 Heat, formal Aerdy court interest, newspaper one-liner within two days. In the cistern with no surface witness: no Heat change. Kethren or Korre becomes the next thread.
- **C — Haskur Escapes, Evidence Recovered.** The default successful ending. Itinerary (intact or partially burned), route mark, Iren's reading, one of Korre/Kethren as continuing thread. **The party will see Haskur again. When and where is a Phase 7 question.**
- **D — Haskur Escapes, Evidence Mostly Burned.** Fail-forward. Saern made it to the brazier; the packet is mostly ash. What survives: a scorched fragment with *"no sail, no road"* legible, the route mark, one wax flake, Iren's later reading. Four pieces of independent evidence and one named handler. **Do not apologise.**

**Closing read-aloud (route-neutral).** Open with *"ash and water"* (B, C, D) or *"water and the prisoner's breath"* (A):

> *You climb back out into Tinker's Way at the third bell after midnight. The street is empty. Above you, somewhere in the second row of windows, a baby is crying briefly and is comforted by a tired voice. Two streets over, a watchman's whistle answers a watchman's whistle.*
>
> *Greyhawk continues. It does not look down.*
>
> *You have ash on your hands — or wet paper, or a prisoner's slow breath, or all three. You have a phrase you have read three times this week and one you have just read for the first time.*
>
> *No sail. No road.*
>
> *You have a sketch of seven steps cut under a black hill, and a manuscript dealer in the Garden Quarter who has stopped blinking when she reads.*
>
> *Whatever the next door is, it is not political and it is not mercantile. You will not find it in a port chart. You will not find it by asking which Aerdy noble paid for what.*
>
> *You will find it by looking down.*

**Caelith's final note** (the next morning, indifferent runner, unsigned, the hand unmistakable):

> *The city was the desk. You have found the drawer.*

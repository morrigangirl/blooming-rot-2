# Phase 7 — The Seventh Question — Master GM Walkthrough

*A spoon-fed run guide. Read top to bottom; the order on this page is the order at the table.*

---

## What this phase is for

Phase 7 is the phase where the party **turns a phrase into a place**. They left the Old Stonecistern (Phase 6) carrying a handful of language — *No sail. No road. Seventh Stair. Andren transferred.* — and a sketched route mark of seven descending cuts beneath a black hill. By the end of Phase 7 they will have walked northeast from Greyhawk into the southern Cairn Hills, found a ruined Aerdy tollhouse called **Tarnsmere Toll**, and stood at a chest-high cairn whose east face holds the same seven cuts they saw scratched into a Greyhawk sluice wall a hundred miles south. The phase has three jobs:

1. Confirm that **the Seventh Stair is not a port term or a metaphor** — it is a class of *permitted descent* in obsolete Aerdy mining law. "No sail. No road." is literal: the conspiracy's next leg bypasses surface travel.
2. Walk the party through a five-category triangulation — **Name, Function, Permission, Material, Place** — across five interpretive sources (Iren, Aldea, Vone, the Guild and Vetch, and whichever Phase 6 prisoner survived) so no single NPC carries the phase. The conspiracy is compartmentalised by design; the players solve it by cross-referencing.
3. Get the party to **the Seven-Cut Cairn** by the close of the phase — *only* to the cairn. The iron-bound stairwell door behind the ruined tollhouse is the threshold of Phase 8.

Phase 7 is investigative and travel-pressed. **One essentially mandatory combat** (the prisoner extraction, if a prisoner is held). Tier 2 / early Tier 3. **Party tier: levels 8–11.**

**Player-facing language constraint.** Do not use *Underdark*, *Eclavdra*, *House Eilserv*, *Vault*, *Demonweb*, or *Lolth*. Use *below*, *deep road*, *descent*, *underway*, *old stair*, *permitted descent*, *sealed route*. (GM-only: "E." is **Eclavdra of House Eilserv**, drow, Erelhei-Cinlu. Phase 7 must not even confirm "E." is non-human.)

## Before you run it

Read these in this order:

- `journal-phase-7-arrival.json` — GM brief, route-neutral opening, and the **seven branching openings (A–G)**. You only run one.
- `journal-phase-7-triangulation.json` — the five-category system. Memorise the source-to-category table.
- `journal-phase-7-nodes.json` — the seven interpretive nodes (Iren, Aldea, Vone, Guild + Vetch, Prisoner, False Stair, Field Reconnaissance).
- `journal-phase-7-clocks.json` — the two pressure clocks (**Route-Cleansing**, 6 segments; **Prisoner / Heat**, 6 segments).
- `journal-phase-7-npcs.json` — full NPC dossiers, especially **Dalmor Vetch** (new).
- `journal-phase-7-cairn.json` — the final location, the discovery read-aloud, outcome packages, the Phase 8 hand-off.
- `journal-phase-7-handouts.json` — eight player-facing handouts. Pre-print them.

Decide five things before play:

- **Which Phase 6 ending applies (Openings A–G).** C (default), G (hardest), D (fail-forward).
- **Route-Cleansing Clock start.** D starts at 1; G starts at 2; otherwise 0.
- **Prisoner/Heat Clock start.** Haskur killed visibly +1; open violence at the cistern +1.
- **Which prisoner (if any).** Haskur, Kethren, Korre, Doras Kemmel, or none. None solves the phase alone.
- **Whether Caelith can act publicly.** If Closure passed in Phase 5, unsigned courier notes only. Otherwise he can act, at +1 to the Route-Cleansing Clock per public action.

## Scene-by-scene, in order

### Scene 1 — The Morning After (Greyhawk City, day 1)

**Read the Route-Neutral Opening.** Key beats: a baker shouting at her apprentice, barge-hands at the High Quay, a yawning toll-clerk at the West Gate. Greyhawk's indifference is its weather. End on the pivot line — *"It is about where obedience goes when it leaves the surface"* — and let the table sit with it.

Then run the **branching opening** that matches Phase 6's outcome:

- **A — Haskur Captured.** Frightened, bitter, in a borrowed back room. Tier 1 under light pressure (Insight DC 12, Persuasion DC 13). Tier 2 needs harder pressure or a survival offer; Tier 3 needs a promise of protective custody. **If threatened without survival, he hands them the False Stair map deliberately** (Scene 6).
- **B — Haskur Killed.** Inventory his effects in a borrowed cellar. **Hand the players Handout 1B.** Significant: brass-button slip *"V. K. — 8th of Patchett,"* two pewter chits stamped *Tarnsmere Toll — route account*, a pouch of iron-grey-with-green stone dust (for Aldea), a half-burned slip *"… no sail. no road. VII. cairn under the …"*, and a hollow brass crow containing a cipher slip **T · S · C** (resolves later as *Tarnsmere Spur Cairn*; don't say so). Heat Clock +1.
- **C — Haskur Escaped, Evidence Recovered (default).** Iren has had the Ashen Itinerary on her reading table since the third bell. *"Sit. Do not lean on the table. I have been reading since the third bell."*
- **D — Haskur Escaped, Evidence Mostly Burned.** Scraps only. *"We have less than we should. We have more than nothing. Stop apologising and tell me what you saw."* Route-Cleansing Clock starts at 1; field searches now require 4 of 5 categories.
- **E — Kethren Captured.** Often combined with another opening. Answers literally and narrowly. Will not name "E." Will not monologue. *"Where is it?" "Where the surface roads end at the marker." "What does the marker look like?" "Seven cuts. Beneath a hill that is not a hill." "Where is the hill?" "Northeast."* By the third humane day: *"Do not return me. Whatever you do, do not return me."*
- **F — V. Korre Captured.** Negotiating, not confessing. Opens with three questions of her own — *am I charged in a Greyhawk court; held on Yeomanry authority; who signs for my disappearance?* If the party can answer two of three, she tells them what she knows.
- **G — Everyone Escaped.** No prisoner. Korre's apartment is being scrubbed. The Brass Crow's shutters are closed under a sign reading *"Under reorganisation."* Route-Cleansing Clock starts at 2. **Make this opening feel hungry.**

**No checks here.** The opening sets the season; Scene 2 begins the investigation.

### Scene 2 — Iren Velash and the Glossary (load-bearing)

Almost regardless of opening, the first interpretive stop is **Velash Manuscripts & Bindings, Garden Quarter**. Iren has finally stopped pretending she does not care.

**Read aloud:**

> *Iren has cleared the reading table. She has pinned down the Ashen Itinerary with four river-stones and a single thin band of black silk along the burnt edge. The brass reading lamp is at low flame; the shutters are half-drawn. She does not look up when you come in. "Sit," she says. "Do not lean on the table. I have been reading since the third bell."*
>
> *After a long moment: "This is not a sea-route term. It is not a road term. It is a* descent *term."*

Iren establishes four things — speak them as conclusions, not checks:

- **VII** is not a sequence in a ledger. It is a *route-class marker* — the seventh class of permitted descents in old Aerdy administrative practice.
- **"Stair"** in old route glossaries means *artificial* descents — cut steps, switchback shafts, hauling platforms.
- **"Seventh Question"** and **"Seventh Stair"** are connected through an old Aerdy surveyor's index where descents were catalogued by step-count and surveyor's question-number.
- **"No sail. No road."** is literal. *Class VII transit condition.*

Pushed for more: Investigation DC 13 reveals the "VII" was added in different ink pressure — *later, by a different hand*. History DC 15 surfaces the **Aerdy Permissive Survey** (dissolved 542 CY). Arcana DC 15 confirms heat-reactive but not magical-dictation.

Bringing Iren more material deepens her readings. **Vone's ledger is the keystone**: she recognises the obsolete VII account-class prefix from a glossary on her highest shelf.

When she is satisfied, **hand the players Handout 1 — Iren Velash's Glossary Note**. *"Read this and burn it. I will not say so again."*

**Iren delivers Name, Function, partial Place.** She cannot directly supply Permission or full Place. *Risk-tracked: at Clock 4–6 her shop is targeted; her glossary is lost unless she has copied it to a safer location.*

### Scene 3 — Aldea Veren (Material Memory)

Trina's correspondent in the wood. She reads matter the way Iren reads paper. In person or by dead-drop courier (an old beech stump near the stream). On her work-table: glass dish, brass tongs, candles, black silk, iron-gall pen. *She is waiting for materials, not words.*

By courier: a folded packet returns within three days as **Handout 2 — Aldea Veren's Material Reply**.

What Aldea reads:

- **The wax.** Recipe-family unfamiliar. Closest match: *old mine-seal wax* from some Cairn Hills sealed claims. Darkening agent for *cool, lightless storage*; non-standard resin last seen on a 200-year-old sealed survey.
- **The stone dust.** Worked stone, not river-stone. Iron-grey with a thread of green — consistent with *the southern Cairn Hills and the Stark Mounds. The Cairn Hills are the closer match.*
- **The route mark.** Older than Yeomanry, modern Aerdy, or Suel. The "hill" is convention, not topography — a surveyor's mark for a *sealed entrance*.
- **The burned paper.** Treated with an old mining-charter fixative. *Not commercial stationery.*

She will not name the deeper faction, species, or destination. Pressed: *"You are looking for a road because the paper taught you to. Stop obeying the paper."*

**Aldea delivers Material, Function, partial Place.**

**Optional complication.** If Lie 2 was active in Phase 6, the conspiracy is copying her packets (envelope-seam ink-mark). If the party does nothing, Route-Cleansing Clock +1.

### Scene 4 — Vone and the Route-Exception Ledger

Vone is more expensive, more nervous. The sheet of paper on his desk is no longer perpendicular; **Insight DC 13** spots the tell. He has begun moving the irregular ledger out of the building.

Phase 7 prices: regular ledger 75 gp, irregular 300 gp, **route-exception copies (new) 500 gp or a credible promise of protective custody**. Modifiers: Lie 1 active −30%; Path C carryover +50%.

If Vone has vanished (Clock 3+): find him via Pesh his assistant at a Garden Quarter cousin's (50 gp + DC 14 Persuasion + protection), or trace him to the unsanctioned cellar on **Bracken Lane** (one day's searching).

**Hand the players Handout 3 — Vone's Route-Exception Ledger.** The phrase **"STAIR"** appears *only* in route-exception entries. **VII** is the account prefix on Korre's traffic. **Tarnsmere Toll** is the destination. **NE SPUR — route exception** identifies the direction. Vone's own internal note: *T · S · C → Tarnsmere Spur Cairn → Guild old map sheet 47 → CONFIRM with surveyor (Vetch?).* **Vone was about to do exactly what the party is doing.**

Useful Vone line: *"A courier ledger says where things went. A route-exception says where the clerk was told not to ask."*

**Vone delivers Permission, Place, Name.**

### Scene 5 — The Guild and Dalmor Vetch

Greyhawk Guild of Miners and Jewelers, Bargemen's Street. **Not conspiracy-controlled** — but someone has been quietly removing certain old records.

Approach: Persuasion DC 14 (public hall + one claim file); bribery 15–30 gp (back-archive, half a day); Stealth DC 16 (full access, Heat risk); Scholar DC 15 Investigation + History (identifies the obsolete VII account class *before* raising it with anyone); Legal writ (full access in a day, Heat +1).

**Hand the players Handout 4 — Guild Mining Claim Extract** (sheet 47, Tarnsmere Spur, Claim no. VII / 0341, 487 CY). Crucial readings: the **claimant's name has been physically scraped**; the **Aerdy Permissive Survey seal was re-waxed in 549 CY, seven years after the Survey was dissolved**; **Garrold Eft** is the missing Yeomanry surveyor Caelith referenced (went out 542 CY, did not return); the recent margin note is signed *Underclerk Y.P.* — **Yorth Pell**, being paid to swap records (cracks at Persuasion 16 / Intimidation 18).

**Then Dalmor Vetch.** Reachable via Tellan Verth (Hand of Coals, Phase 6) as a free intro, or 20 gp through the Guild.

> *Vetch sets two heavy mugs of small beer on the table without asking. "You're after stairs," he says. "Not roads. Roads, you'd ask the Guild. You came to me." He taps the table with his maimed hand. "A road is what merchants call it when they can charge toll. Miners call it a stair when the earth charges first."*

What Vetch knows: the **Tarnsmere Spur** ends at ruined **Tarnsmere Toll**, then a footpath, then a cairn line, then a cairn with seven descending cuts beneath a black hill symbol. **The seven cuts are not a count — they are a permission mark.** The black hill is *a way down that has been closed by authority*. When someone says "road," Vetch taps his cane once. *It is the most reliable opinion he has ever had.*

For **20 gp and a promise not to mention him to the Guild**, Vetch draws **Handout 5 — Dalmor Vetch's Sketched Map**: GHC → Old Eastern Trade Route → Tarnsmere Spur branch at ~14 mi → dry ford → split ash tree → climb → ruined tollhouse → cairn with seven cuts. Bottom in different ink: *"If the seventh cut is gone, you are too late, or you are at the wrong cairn."*

**Guild + Vetch deliver Place, Function, Material confirmation.** By end of this scene the party should have 4–5 of 5 categories.

### Scene 6 — The False Stair (optional)

Run Node 6 if the Route-Cleansing Clock reaches 3, if the party has been threatening prisoners without offering survival, or if Opening G is in play. The party gets **Handout 6 — False Stair Map** — single oak instead of split ash, six cuts instead of seven, parchment too clean.

Sources: a "helpful" stranger; planted documents in Vone's archive; coerced statement from Yorth Pell; **Haskur deliberately, if threatened without survival**.

Catching the forgery is easy: Investigation DC 12 against Vetch's sketch (six vs. seven cuts); DC 13 (parchment is this season); Insight DC 13 on the stranger.

If the party walks in unaware, small ambush: **2 Route-Cleanser Agents** (CR 1), **1 Hired Greyhawk Tough** (CR ½), optional alchemical-smoke or collapsing-timber trap (DC 14 Dex, 4d6 bludgeoning + prone). The enemy is *not trying to kill the party* — they want to waste a day. They disengage if any PC is captured or killed.

Even total tactical failure leaves the party with proof that "six cuts" is the planted answer — by exclusion, the real marker has seven. **Counts as a partial-Place category.** *Do not make the False Stair a punishment. Make it a bruise with a receipt.*

### Scene 7 — Prisoner Extraction (mandatory if a prisoner is held)

Triggers at **Prisoner/Heat Clock segment 4**. Automatic if a prisoner is held more than three days.

Composition: **1 Silent Scout** (CR ½), **1 Record-Burner** (CR 2 arcane operative, not a religious figure), **2 Hired Greyhawk Toughs** (CR ½ each). Third bell after midnight. Their goal is **the prisoner**, not the PCs. Passive Perception 14 catches the intrusion early; otherwise the alarm goes up only when the prisoner is touched.

If the party fails, the prisoner is gone by morning, Route-Cleansing Clock +1, investigation continues from documents and surviving witnesses.

### Scene 8 — Field Reconnaissance (Cairn Hills, days 4–5)

Once the party has 3+ categories (4+ under Opening D), they ride. Northeast on the **Old Eastern Trade Route**, then off onto the **Tarnsmere Spur**. Two days each way. Day 1 ends at the **Three Wickets** roadside inn; from the second bell, the road's character changes — older surface, deeper ruts, no inn for the rest.

On day 2, give all three field clues:

1. **Old cart ruts that vanish into grass** at a point where a stone marker has been pushed flat.
2. **A waymark with the seventh mark scraped almost flat** — six cuts sharp, the seventh worn within the last week.
3. **A dead or abandoned pack animal** with harness wrong for a merchant route — *fittings for carrying through narrow places*.

One optional encounter: (A) charcoal burners with a sketch of "strangers with too-clean boots"; (B) an old man riding south who denies the spur exists (Insight DC 13: afraid; DC 15: paid); (C) a disguised route-cleanser scoring out the seventh cut on a marker; (D) a silent watcher at dusk, gone by first light (*Kethren if escaped, otherwise a route-cleanser proxy*).

### Scene 9 — Arrival at the Seven-Cut Cairn (closing)

Late on day 2 of reconnaissance, the party crests a low rise at sunset.

**Read aloud:**

> *The mining spur ends here. Two hundred yards of grass has grown up since anyone walked this road regularly. Below you, in a shallow fold of the hills, the ground levels out. There is a ruined tollhouse, its roof long fallen in, only the doorway-lintel still standing. Twenty yards beyond it, the stone foundation of what was once a survey station, the slate and timber long since grown over with grass. And beyond that, on a small rise where the path used to climb, a cairn.*
>
> *The cairn is roughly chest-high. The stones are local; the mortar is old. The wind is in the grass. There are crows somewhere high above you, riding the air.*
>
> *On the east face of the cairn, cut into the largest single block, in the old soot of a dozen winters:* seven descending lines, beneath a hill that is not a hill.
>
> *The seventh cut is the sharpest of the seven. The blackening on the hill-symbol holds the last of the sunlight.*
>
> *There has not been a Greyhawk bell since you turned onto the mining spur this morning. The silence is not silence. It is administered.*

Surface features: the **ruined tollhouse** with *a stepped iron-bound door at the back of the inner room, sealed with old wax and rusted bands* (Phase 8's threshold); the **collapsed survey station** with a surveyor's iron stake still driven into a corner, its chain ending in nothing; the **half-buried Aerdy mining-road sign**; the **shallow quarry scars**; the **cairn** itself; and behind the tollhouse, **the dry well that is actually the top of a sealed stairwell**. *Phase 8 begins by opening this. Phase 7 does not.*

| Check | DC | Reveals |
|---|---|---|
| Investigation (cairn) | 12 | The seventh cut was made *last* — later than the other six, by years if not decades. |
| History / Mining lore | 13 | The "hill" is a sealed-entrance convention. The cuts are a permission count. |
| Survival | 13 | Three sets of recent boot prints. One set returned, on a different night, with tools. |
| Perception (tollhouse) | 13 | The rear inner wall has the stepped iron-bound door — wax-sealed, rusted bands. |
| Arcana (residual magic) | 14 | A faint ancient non-elemental enchantment on the seven cuts. *It feels like a contract.* |
| Investigation (wax) | 14 | A fragment in a crevice matches Aldea's unknown recipe-family. **This is the place.** |

**Hand Handout 8 — Final Waymark Description.** *"You have seen this symbol before. It is the same shape that was scratched, in fresh soot, into the wall of the Old Stonecistern."* If they take a rubbing, it matches the Phase 6 sketch exactly. **Hand that confirmation across the table; it is the moment the campaign clicks into place.**

**Captured NPC reactions.** Haskur: no recognition. Kethren: looks at the seventh cut for a long time; says nothing; Insight DC 14 reveals fear that is *not surface-fear*. Korre: shakes her head once — *"This was a route-account on paper. I never asked what the account was for."*

**If Clock is at 5,** the seventh cut has been scraped almost flat within the last 48 hours; wax-match, sealed stairwell, and Vetch's map still confirm the site. **If Clock is at 6,** the tollhouse is occupied — two Route-Cleanser Agents and one Hired Tough re-stoning the stairwell entrance.

Camp upslope. No fire — wind is too constant. Cold food. Watch set. Read the **Ending Read-Aloud**:

> *Above you, the Cairn Hills wind moves through the grass like a finger drawn across paper. Greyhawk City is twenty miles south and full of bells you will not hear tonight. Someone — one of you, or the prisoner, or only the wind — speaks the phrase the cistern gave you, three nights ago and a hundred miles away:* "No sail. No road." *The road ends at the cairn. The stair begins where the maps learned to lie.*

**Pause between the second-to-last line and the last.**

If Caelith can still write — Closure was held over or defeated, or his last courier slipped past the Phase 5 leak — **hand Handout 7 — Caelith's Courier Addendum.** Unsigned. *"You are no longer following a man. You are following the permissions around him. Roads are watched from the sides. Descents are watched from below. Be ready. Be slow."*

## NPCs to know in Phase 7

- **Iren Velash.** Manuscript dealer, Garden Quarter. Interpretive anchor; honest in the irritating way of exact people. *Risk-tracked at Clock 4–6.*
- **Aldea Veren.** Trina's correspondent in the wood. Reads matter — wax, stone, mineral, ink.
- **Edrik Vone.** Courier archivist, High Quay. More expensive, more frightened. Vanishes as the clock climbs; traceable via Pesh his assistant.
- **Caelith Dunivar.** Hand of the Duke. Mostly by courier. If Closure passed, unsigned letters only. Knows about missing surveyor **Garrold Eft** and two closed descent claims in 542 CY.
- **Dalmor Vetch (NEW).** Retired Greyhawk mining surveyor. Two fingers missing on his right hand. Hates clean maps. Stat: `actor-dalmor-vetch.json` (CR 0; will not fight unless cornered).
- **Yorth Pell.** Guild underclerk. *No relation to the Loftwick Pell.* Paid to swap records by an unfamiliar client. Cracks at Persuasion 16 / Intimidation 18. `actor-yorth-pell-guild-underclerk.json` (CR 0).
- **Haskur Vandrell (if captured).** Frightened, calculating. **Hands the False Stair map deliberately if threatened without survival offered.** Asks once whether the party intends to take him when they descend. *He does not want to.*
- **Kethren Ilvath (if captured).** Young, grey-and-bone. Answers literally and narrowly. **By the third day: *"Do not return me."*** GM-only: if held more than a week without protection, simply *gone* from his locked room. *Do not explain.*
- **Veyra Korre (if captured).** Negotiating, not confessing. Loyal only to her trade discipline. Will testify about structures, not people.
- **Doras Kemmel (if turned in Phase 6).** Sworn witness from Kestrel & Reed. Testifies the packets moved through a mining account.
- **Captain Heshet, Greyhawk City Watch.** Polite, jurisdictional, increasingly insistent at Prisoner Clock segments 1, 3, 5.

Combat stat blocks (`packs/_source/phase-7-actors/`): Silent Scout (CR ½), Record-Burner (CR 2), Route-Cleanser Agent (CR 1), Hired Greyhawk Tough (CR ½).

## How Phase 7 ends

The phase closes on the **Ending Read-Aloud at the camp upslope from the ruined tollhouse**, after the party has examined the cairn, identified the sealed iron-bound stairwell door, and made camp without a fire.

The party has the threshold. They have not opened it. They carry rubbings of the route mark, wax fragments from the cairn site, Vetch's map, surviving captured NPCs and their information, Iren's glossary, Aldea's material reply, Vone's ledger excerpt, and (where possible) Caelith's addendum. They are at the foot of an iron-bound door in the back wall of a roofless tollhouse on a hill no current map names.

**Suggested closing GM line:**

> *"You have the threshold. You have not opened it. When you are ready — when you have rested, and gathered, and brought what you need — we will pick up at the iron-bound door behind the ruined tollhouse, with the wax intact and the seven cuts above you on the cairn. That is Phase 8."*

## Branches & contingencies

- **All 5 categories, low clock (Outcome A).** Clock 3 or below. Threshold intact but sealed. Standard Phase 8 setup.
- **All 5 categories, high clock (Outcome B).** Clock 4–5. Waymark partially defaced; stairwell trapped or partially re-sealed.
- **Partial triangulation (Outcome C).** 3 of 5. Right region; short field-search at the start of Phase 8 to locate the exact tollhouse.
- **False Stair first (Outcome D).** Route-cleansers reach the cairn first; the site is being actively scrubbed when the party arrives.
- **Captured NPC helps (Outcome E).** Party reaches the site faster. Prisoner becomes a liability or bargaining chip.
- **Captured NPC escapes (Outcome F).** Clock +1. The conspiracy gains fresh intelligence.
- **Stuck at 2 categories.** Give a passive prompt: a courier re-copies a previous handout with one new detail highlighted; an NPC remembers a phrase; the False Stair appears as planted misdirection whose wrongness reveals what is hidden. **The phase must not dead-end.**
- **Party threatens Haskur without offering survival.** He hands them the False Stair map. Use it.
- **Party tries to get the phase from one captured NPC.** Their answers degrade into "I don't know." *Each was told only what they needed to know.*
- **Party tries to descend the stairwell at end of Phase 7.** The iron-bound door will not open to force without ritual or key. *That is Phase 8.*
- **Kethren held more than a week without protection.** He is simply gone. Door still locked. *Do not explain.*
- **Party kills or displays a captured agent publicly.** Clock +2.
- **Yeomanry authority brought loudly into Guild halls.** Clock +1; informal channels close for a week.

## Carry to Phase 8

Make a private note — these mature in Phase 8 ("The Stair Beneath Tarnsmere") and Phase 9 ("Below the Clean Paper"):

- **The wax recipe-family.** Aldea has now seen it on three independent samples; Iren cross-read it; the cairn carries it in a crevice. *In Phase 8 the iron-bound door is sealed with this same wax.* The wax is what permits.
- **The recall threat (Kethren).** Phase 8 may begin to explain how a man vanishes from a locked room with the door still locked. *Phase 7 must not.*
- **Garrold Eft.** The Yeomanry surveyor who went out for the 542 CY survey of the Tarnsmere Spur and never returned. Surveyor General's note unavailable.
- **The contract enchantment.** The cairn's seven cuts carry an ancient, non-elemental enchantment the party cannot read. *Phase 8's threshold reads it.*
- **Outcome A–F.** The state of the cairn (intact / defaced / actively defended) is Phase 8's opening tableau.
- **Surviving captured NPCs and their terms.** Haskur wants a quiet Aerdy town; Kethren wants not to be returned; Korre wants Aerdy under managed disappearance; Doras wants his family safe.
- **Iren's glossary relocated?** If yes, by-courier consultation continues. If no, the conspiracy has her latest readings.
- **Aldea's packet interception.** If unaddressed, the conspiracy has a copy of her material analysis and will be at the cairn ahead of the party.
- **Caelith's status.** Whether the addendum reached the camp, and whether he can act publicly from here.
- **The seven cuts and the black hill remain unnamed in player-facing text.** The party may have *guessed* they look at a sealed entrance. The word *Underdark* belongs to Phase 9 at the earliest. Do not slip and name it now.

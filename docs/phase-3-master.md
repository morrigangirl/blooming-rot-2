# Phase 3 — The Hardby Investigation — Master GM Walkthrough

*A spoon-fed run guide. Read top to bottom; the order on this page is the order at the table.*

---

## What this phase is for

Phase 3 is the middle link in the Loftwick → Hardby → Rel Astra chain. The party arrives in **Hardby** — a Free City of merchants and mercenaries on the Wild Coast, formally governed by an old female-led council (the **Gynarchy**) whose authority has been quietly slipping for two decades — having chosen one of three travel approaches at the end of Phase 2. The conspiracy is operationally based here. The phase has three jobs:

1. **Name the broker.** "Tarsh" — the Hardby intermediary who hired Merev Sarth in Phase 2 — is identified by his real name (**Anver Resh**) and confronted, or, on the slowest approach, named on paper.
2. **Resolve Tamsin Moraven.** Alive in Hardby, alive at an estate two days east, or confirmed dead with body and last letter recovered. The travel approach picks which.
3. **Produce the document that puts a senior partner of Cindren & Vhal Rel Astra inside the conspiracy in his own hand** — specifically **Tarlith Vhal Sereth**, the recluse senior partner. **Veska Maelan's notarial opinion is the bridge to Phase 4 and the single critical handout.**

Phase 3 also clarifies what *"HARBOR BEFORE THRONE"* operationally means: the conspiracy is racing the Aerdy succession to seat candidates on Rel Astra's harbor commission before a new Overking is named. The party does *not* yet learn which candidates, or which Aerdy claimant the conspirators support. That is Phase 4.

Phase 3 is denser than 1 and 2 combined and includes one real combat sequence (Tamsin recovery) and possibly a second (Pressure Clock segment-5 squad attack). **DCs cap at 17 except the Solen turn (DC 22).** Party tier: **2 (levels 5–10).**

- `packs/_source/phase-3-journals/journal-phase-3-hardby-npcs.json` — Veska, Castrian, Hesren, Ailen, Tarsh/Resh, Zoria Weis.
- `packs/_source/phase-3-journals/journal-phase-3-investigation.json` — Veska reveal, three Tamsin recoveries, the Coopered Wreck confrontation, the Harbor-Before-Throne reveal at the Gynarchy registry.
- `packs/_source/phase-3-journals/journal-phase-3-cv-branch.json` — C&V Hardby branch, Solen Mereth, three paths to the documents (burglary, sympathetic clerk, subpoena).
- `packs/_source/phase-3-journals/journal-phase-3-departure.json` — three routes to Rel Astra, the Phase 3 Pressure Clock, the ending read-aloud.

You need to have decided four things before play:

- **Which travel approach the party chose in Phase 2.** Official Delegation, Commercial Cover, or Quiet Pursuit. This is the load-bearing variable.
- **Whether Phase 2's Pressure Clock fired segment 5.** If yes, Hesren Vesh has been recalled to Hardby and is recoverable. If no, he is still in Loftwick.
- **Whether you (or Trina) sent a letter to Ailen Moraven during the Phase 2 visit to Vellin.** If yes, Ailen is warned and packing for Loftwick. If no, she is alone and unaware.
- **Where the Pressure Clock face is visible.** Phase 3's clock is its own — six segments, faster than Loftwick's. Put it on the table.

## Scene-by-scene, in order

### Scene 1 — Arrival in Hardby

Read the arrival page that matches the party's approach.

- **Official Delegation.** Approach the West Gate in formal column behind the Yeomanry councilman (default **Ortwell Brane** — Phase 1's closure-faction figure, politically maneuvered into this duty and privately furious). A Hardby herald in oxblood-and-grey livery meets them at three hundred yards, defers audiences to "the day after tomorrow." Inside the gate, two strangers in unfamiliar livery take note of every face and *write*. Above the inner arch, an old Aerdi tower-and-three-waves clearing-mark — *the same mark as the silver coin in their pack.* Lodged at the Hall of Salt and Tide on Whalemarket Quay.
- **Commercial Cover.** Enter the South Gate at sundown as a small wool-trade delegation; the guard waves them through. Make for the address Trina gave them — a stone-fronted townhouse off Rope Walk, brass plate reading **VELL**. Castrian opens the door, looks them over, and says: *"Mistress Alvere wrote to me about you. Come in. The wine is good and the kettle is just on. I have been hoping you would arrive before the firm noticed."* Lodged at Castrian's.
- **Quiet Pursuit.** Arrive at the South Gate in early morning haze on fast horses, false papers, light saddlebags. The guard takes the papers, asks no questions, hands them back. They have one address (Veska's notary office on Whalemarket Quay) and one phrase (*"A tower above three waves."*). It is the third day after Hesren's recall message left Loftwick. Lodging the party's choice; default the **Blackwell Inn** or the **Green Magpie**.

**No checks here.** What the players should take away: Hardby is not Loftwick. Faster, looser, mercantile, and the Gynarchy is real but tired.

### Scene 2 — The Mossen Place (the party's Hardby base)

A quiet domestic beat before the investigative ones. The party finds its base of operations, gets the lay of the merchant quarter, and reads the single most important character letter Caelith sends them in this phase. Run this as soon as the party has a free morning — typically the second day in Hardby on QP/CC, the same afternoon as arrival on OD (the Hall of Salt and Tide lodging is for the formal mornings; the Mossen Place is where the party actually works from).

**The handover.** At the Phase 2 farewell, alongside the brass Hand-of-the-Duke token, Caelith handed the lead PC a small folded paper in his careful handwriting:

> *17 Cooper's Tale, Hardby merchant quarter. Ask Mistress Halver at the corner grocer for the key. Tell her the cellarer's apprentice came down from Loftwick to mind the house. She will not ask further.*

**The cover identity.** The deed is held in the name of *Mistress Eleyn Mossen, retired, of Hardby* — a fictitious widow Caelith has maintained for nearly a decade through a discreet Loftwick solicitor (**Mistress Aldaen Veth**), who pays Hardby rates and tithes by courier each quarter. The cover is robust against Hardby Watch and against C&V's local branch (which does not handle non-bonded private property). It is *not* robust against sustained loud occupation; two or three weeks is the comfortable window.

**Mistress Halver.** Sixties, widowed, runs the corner grocer at the south end of Cooper's Tale. She knows the house has belonged to an absentee elderly widow she has never met. She hands over the key when the party speaks the right phrase. Pleasant, incurious about Mistress Mossen — and **the single best gossip source for Hardby's merchant quarter.** Treat her kindly. (Gossip leads below.)

**The first day at the house — the dusting scene.** A small downtime beat. The party arrives at a quiet musty three-storey townhome on a quiet side street, uncovers furniture from white linen dust-sheets, lights the parlor, dining-room, and kitchen fires, opens windows. A morning's work for the full party.

- **The forgotten dark wool cloak on the foyer coat-rail.** No name in the collar. Caelith may know whose it was; he does not say. A PC who pockets it and produces it later may get a quiet reaction from Caelith in a future scene.
- **The wiped spot on the kitchen worktable** — a small clean area where someone wiped without bothering with the rest. **Insight DC 13** on Mistress Halver: she hasn't been inside in three months except to top the cistern. Whose work was the wiped spot, then? GM's choice; hold the thread.
- **Cistern and pantry.** Cold water works. Pantry stocked with non-perishables under Caelith's standing order. The party will need to shop the merchant-quarter market for produce, milk, meat.

**Cache 1 — The library compartment (second-floor library, M2).** **Investigation DC 13** on the library floor beside the writing desk on the east outer wall finds a slightly raised floorboard. **Sleight of Hand DC 10** or **Thieves' Tools DC 12** lifts it. Underneath, an iron-bound lockbox (**Thieves' Tools DC 14**, or the key is hidden under the wash-stand basin in the second-floor bath M4 — **Perception DC 14**). Contents:

- A small jar of good lamp oil (six refills).
- A quire of blank paper and three sticks of sealing wax in red, black, and dark blue.
- Four hand-drawn Hardby street-maps: the merchant quarter, the docks, the C&V branch's block, and the Gynarchy Registry quarter.
- **A sealed letter in red wax with Caelith's chop, addressed simply** *"To whoever finds this."* This is a handout. Read aloud verbatim — it is a key character moment.

> *Friends —*
>
> *If you are reading this, you have found the floorboard. I assumed you would. The compartment is small and you are not stupid.*
>
> *Three things, briefly.*
>
> *First — this house is yours for as long as you need it. I will not ask what you do here. I would prefer it if you did not write to me from it under your own names; the four maps in the lockbox are routes that have, at various times, worked.*
>
> *Second — the Hardby branch of Cindren & Vhal is at 4 Brass Lane, four streets east of Cooper's Tale. The branch is open from second bell after dawn until fifth bell after noon. The bonded clerk on duty most days is a thin man named **Jaron Pell**; he is paid to be civil to walk-ins and to send a runner to Rel Astra at the first sign of meaningful inquiry. Veska Maelan at the trade-clearing notary's office on Long Quay will be more useful to you than the firm itself.*
>
> *Third — if any of you is hurt in a way that the Hardby Watch should not see, take them to **Brother Wenra Holst** at the small infirmary on Cobbler's Walk (three streets south of here). He is a half-orc cleric of Pelor who treats discreetly for a donation. I have left twenty gold with him against your account; it is enough for two serious mendings or one extended convalescence.*
>
> *I have not left a weapon in the house. I have left a place to put a weapon down.*
>
> *Make use of the kitchen. Do not let the cistern run dry; I have asked Mistress Halver to top it from the well if you forget. Do not, under any circumstance, sign the Hardby property roll with a name you wish to keep your own.*
>
> *I will see you in Loftwick or I will not.*
>
> *— C.*

**Cache 2 — The linen-closet floorboard (third-floor linen closet, T6).** **Investigation DC 14** in the centre of the closet finds a loose board (a slightly different shading; a hollower note when tapped — **Perception DC 13** for the tap). Underneath, a small canvas purse with **200 gp in mixed coin** (Aerdi imperial, Yeomanry trade, Hardby civic) and a small folded note in Caelith's hand:

> *Do not be proud about taking this. Replace what you can when you can. — C.*

**The watchful neighbour.** **Doral Hurst** at **#15 Cooper's Tale** — retired Hardby Watch sergeant. Older man, friendly, waves from his front door if a PC steps outside. Says, friendly: *"You're the cellarer's apprentice? Good. The old place has been too quiet too long. Mistress Halver has the run of it usually, but it wanted noise."* He is **not a conspirator**; he is nominally watchful but warm. He will, however, notice everything that happens at #17 going forward. If loud combat erupts inside the house, he sends his teenage grandson to fetch the Watch within five minutes.

**Mistress Halver's gossip leads.** If a PC chats with her after the first day — over a wedge of cheese, a pound of butter, a question about the herb bed — she will share, if asked, what she has heard about the merchant quarter in the last few months. Sprinkle these across return visits; do not deliver them as a list.

- **Jaron Pell** — the C&V bonded clerk Caelith's letter named — is, in Halver's reading, **unhappy in his work.** A recruitment vulnerability the party should consider before Scene 8's Hardby-branch operations. (GM-only: possibly same family as Phase 5's Ostren Pell? The journals do not confirm.)
- **A senior C&V partner has come down from Rel Astra twice in the last six weeks**, each time for only a day. Described as *"the one who never lets anyone see his hands."* **GM-only: this is Tarlith Vhal Sereth.** Do not name him at the table.
- **A thin smoker-voiced man in his fifties** has been drinking at the **Coopered Wreck tavern on Long Quay**, alone, always with his back to the wall. *("I don't trust men who do that — my husband used to do that.")* **This is Tarsh.** Players who pursued Sarth in Phase 2 will recognise the description; if they didn't, they'll come back to this when they next hear his name.
- **Tamsin Moraven came through Hardby fifteen days ago.** Halver saw her get off a wagon at the **eastern caravan yard**, alone, looking tired. Halver waved; Tamsin didn't see her. Halver doesn't know what became of her after. **This is the strongest concrete Tamsin-recovery lead in Phase 3** — a recent location pin that points QP toward Black Cog Lane and CC toward Whitemoor, depending on what the other threads turn up.

**What the party leaves Scene 2 with:** a base of operations, two caches, Caelith's letter naming Jaron Pell / Veska / Brother Wenra Holst as the three Hardby resources, and four pieces of merchant-quarter gossip that quietly seed Scenes 4 (Veska), 5 (Tarsh), 6 (Tamsin), and 8 (the C&V branch). The Mossen Place is also where Castrian and Veska will agree to come privately, and where convalescence and operation-planning happen for the rest of the phase.

### Scene 3 — First Contact

The party's first deliberate move depends on approach:

- **Official Delegation:** They will hear within the first day that *Tamsin Moraven's body was recovered from the harbor three days ago*. Veska's office is shuttered; her former apprentice **Hella Voren** (a young scribe at the Quay clearing-house — common surname, no relation to Phase 1's "Voren") can be approached privately. The phrase opens her. She passes a sealed packet of Veska's notes and chop-rubbings, and a single folded smuggled letter from Tamsin.
- **Commercial Cover:** Castrian briefs them at the kitchen table. He knows no organized conspiracy, but two of his salon regulars (both C&V Hardby clerks) have stopped attending in six months, the harbor commission is being talked about more than usual, and several merchants have gained money strangely. They should meet Veska in business hours, Caelith's letter visible.
- **Quiet Pursuit:** Straight to Veska's office. They speak the phrase at the threshold; if she's alone she repeats it back, and they are in.

**The brass Hand-of-the-Duke token matters here, only in private.** Castrian, shown it, goes very quiet, asks *"Is Caelith well?"* — told yes, pours himself a careful single measure of brandy, drinks it, and says *"What do you need from me?"* Veska respects it immediately. Hardby civic officials in general do not — its authority is private knowledge.

### Scene 4 — The Veska Reveal (the load-bearing scene of Phase 3)

The most important investigative beat in the phase. Everything afterward unfolds from what Veska reads.

**Setup by approach:** Quiet Pursuit, evening session, takes most of a night. Commercial Cover, two business-hour sessions across two visits. Official Delegation, the reveal is read silently in the party's lodging from Hella's packet, in roughly the same words.

**Read aloud (in Veska's voice if present, in her notes' silent voice if not):**

> *"Set them on the table for me. The escrow first. Then the coin. Then the lacquered tube and the seed pouch. The ledger fragment goes here, between the lamp and my elbow. Yes. That is how I will read them.*
>
> *The escrow's house seal is the firm's, the public one. That tells you nothing. Anyone with the firm's wax could press it.*
>
> *The Hardby clearing-chop in the lower-left margin was applied by Solen Mereth. He runs the bond-loading office at the Hardby branch. I know his hand the way you know the shape of your own kitchen at night.*
>
> *The marginal endorsement — Hold for V., or his agent of record, against presentation — is in a different hand. Smaller. Older. I have seen it three times in the last four years and I have never been able to put a name to it from a single document. But — if you ask me whose hand this is, and you give me until the end of this week, I will tell you it is Tarlith Vhal Sereth's. And if I am wrong, I am wrong by one office. He is the senior partner at Cindren and Vhal Rel Astra who has not signed a letter at his own desk in eleven years. The firm pretends he is unwell. He is not unwell. He is hiding what he writes."*

She continues through:

- **The clipped silver coin.** The tower-and-waves countermark is the **Hardby branch's stamp** — recognizable by a chip on the lower-right wave that Veska herself notarized as a defect in 579 CY, when Solen brought her the replacement and asked her to certify the old one as destroyed. *It was not destroyed. It is in the upstairs strongroom of the Hardby branch tonight.*
- **The "Harbor Before Throne" vellum.** Aerdi Concordat of the Bay language — a timing clause: *when there is no king sitting and confirmed, the harbor councils run themselves and run their cities.* Someone is telling Solen that the seating of a harbor council before a throne is settled is the lever that will work.
- **The seed pouch.** Alchemically treated to *look* fresh. Display, not stock — for a paperwork operation where grain that never existed clears Hardby's weighbridge. *Tamsin Moraven was within a month of being able to prove it.*

She closes the ledgers, folds her hands, and names four people:

> *"One. Solen Mereth, head of bond-loading at the Hardby branch. He chopped the escrow. He kept the defect-stamp. He runs the second set of books at the branch level. He is the operational lever.*
>
> *Two. Tarlith Vhal Sereth, senior partner at Rel Astra. He wrote the marginal endorsement. He is the one whose name a court must hear if any of this is to mean anything outside this room.*
>
> *Three. Anver Resh, freelance broker, formerly Solen's clerk. You will find him at the Coopered Wreck most evenings. He hires people for things the firm cannot put in writing. He is the man Sarth was given by.*
>
> *Four. There is a fourth. I do not have the name. I have a hand — the hand that wrote one further annotation in the last set of records I saw, two months before they stopped letting documents I could see cross my desk. That hand is not Sereth's. It is not Solen's. It is not Resh's. It is not anyone in Hardby. It is the hand that the firm answers to. I will tell you, before you leave for Rel Astra, the things I do know about how that hand writes the letter 'E.' That is all I have. Make of it what you can."*

**This is the "E." beat. Veska does not name Eclavdra and cannot.** She offers three details about the formed letter — the loop, the serif, the angle of the descender — that may, much later, let the party recognize the hand again. Hold the thread. **Phase 3 does not name Eclavdra and does not even know to look for her.**

**What the party leaves Scene 4 with:** the three named people; **Veska's prepared notarial opinion** (the document Phase 4 is built on); the fourth-hand "E." observation; and the *"harbor before throne"* framing that Scene 7 will turn into operational law.

### Scene 5 — Tarsh at the Coopered Wreck

Available on Quiet Pursuit (best case) or Commercial Cover (more guarded). **Not directly available on Official Delegation** — on that path Tarsh has been moved upcountry and is identified only on paper.

The Coopered Wreck is a long, coal-smoke-dark sailors' tavern on the harbor district edge. Proprietor **Olfard Trent**, one-eyed retired bosun, runs the bar and remembers everything. Tarsh holds the leftmost back booth, arrives between the seventh and eighth bells, stays through the tenth or eleventh.

The party walks to his booth. Tarsh assumes they are hiring him until they prove otherwise: *"Sit down. Two of you. Tell me what brings you. The other three or four can stand at the bar. Olfard will pour them."*

Two ways to play it:

1. **Maintain the broker pretext (Deception).** Pretend to hire him; useful for getting payment chains, contacts, rates. **Insight DC 14** confirms he is genuinely available for hire and not on alert. **Deception DC 16** to maintain the pretext past five minutes; too many specific C&V questions tip him. On failure he stands: *"I think you are not here to hire me. I think you should leave my booth."*
2. **Direct confrontation.** Show him recovered evidence that names him. He sets the pipe down, lets out a long thin breath of smoke: *"Well. That is a paper I would have preferred not to be holding."* Then he listens.

**What he gives up, with leverage:** a one-way passport out of Hardby and a written guarantee his name does not appear in the indictment — **Caelith will honor this in writing if asked.** Resh provides:

- Confirmation that **Solen Mereth** runs the second set since 1580 and that Resh was his clerk.
- Identification of **Tarlith Vhal Sereth** as the senior partner who signs off (never met him; no one outside the inner senior partners has, in eleven years; can describe the seal).
- **Tamsin's location:** Black Cog Lane warehouse (QP), Whitemoor Estate two days east (CC), or — if asked after she is dead — confirmation that Solen ordered the killing and **Reyna Worth** did it.
- His payment chain: untraceable cash, weekly, courier rotates. Suspects the couriers are run by Worth.
- **Refused: his Rel Astra contact.** *"That man, is not someone I will trade for a passport. Take what I have given you and go."* That is a Phase 4 thread.

**He will not testify, sign, or appear in court.** *"I am giving you what is in my head. You will not also have what is in my hand."*

**Combat is discouraged.** CR 6 (hand crossbow, smoke pellets, Cunning Action). QP: no bodyguards in the tavern. CC: one bodyguard outside the back door (Bandit Captain). On combat in the Wreck, two paid Thugs at the bar defend him while he exits through Olfard's back room. Killing Tarsh: Pressure Clock +2, destabilizes Phase 4.

### Scene 6 — Tamsin Recovery

Run only the variant matching the party's approach.

**5a — Quiet Pursuit: Live Recovery at Black Cog Lane.** Tamsin is held in a foreman's office in a stone-and-timber warehouse on Black Cog Lane, owned through the shell company "**Greenwall & Mast**" (Zoria or Veska can confirm). Twelve days in captivity, alive, tired, unharmed, memorizing the schedule. Three rotating hired Thugs (two on at any time) plus one Cindren overseer, **Reyna Worth** (SRD Veteran), who arrives at the third bell each morning. *Worth will kill Tamsin on a rescue attempt* — neutralizing or distracting her is the central tactical problem. **Best approach:** night raid when only the two thugs are on, Worth at home. **Stealth DC 14** to the loading-dock, **Thieves' Tools DC 15** rear lock, **DC 12** Tamsin's door. Castrian or Resh can both arrange a distraction (fire two streets over, fake harbormaster summons, poisoned breakfast) to keep Worth out of the building for a day. Pressure Clock **+2** on a successful raid. Tamsin needs three days of rest before travel; hide her at Castrian's, Ailen's workshop cellar, or Veska's flat.

**5b — Commercial Cover: Live Recovery at Whitemoor Estate.** Tamsin was moved to the **Whitemoor Estate** — former wine-house turned coastal residence, two days east — four days before arrival. Held in an upstairs bedchamber, freedom of the upstairs corridor, fed properly. Six hired blades: **Olo Crask** (Bandit Captain, retired Aerdi marine, professional, will not kill Tamsin without explicit order) leading five Thugs. **Worth** present one day in three. Sealed-orders courier every five days; *arrive ahead of the next courier and the standing order is "hold safely."* Castrian can arrange travel via a former lover's wine-house on the next headland. Best approach: scout one day, infiltrate by sea on the second night — the dock is the weakest point. Pressure Clock **+2** on success. Return to Castrian's two days, then to Loftwick — consider sea passage to Saltmarsh.

**5c — Official Delegation: Body Recovered, Letter Smuggled.** Tamsin was murdered by Reyna Worth at the Black Cog warehouse three days before the party arrived, on Solen's order, on confirmation that an accredited delegation had filed for entry. Quick (single thrust), body in a barrel, dumped in the harbor, recovered by a fisherman. Watch certified as "harbor incident, foul play probable, jurisdiction limited." The party claims the body through the delegation's foreign-national death-claim standing. Her last letter was smuggled out by a young warehouse worker, **Lerra Voss**, who delivers it via Hella Voren and *disappears within a week.* **Read Tamsin's last letter aloud** (or pass as printed handout):

> *Vellin and Ailen — if you read this, I am not coming home.*
>
> *I want you to know that I knew what I was doing when I asked the questions, and I would ask them again. The grain that was paid for never existed. The men who paid for it knew it never existed and bought it anyway because the paperwork is a kind of weapon, and they wanted the weapon. I followed the weapon back through three counting houses to the firm, and the firm to a name in Rel Astra, and the name to a man who has been dead, on paper, for nine years. I do not know who the man is. I know only that he is the one I was asking about when they came for me.*
>
> *Vellin — there is a small lacquered box in the bottom of my wardrobe under the spare linen. Inside it, my filings on the South Province grain. Take them to the Hardby weighmaster Joren Krill, no — to Caelith Dunivar in Loftwick. Joren is not safe. Caelith is. He will know what to do with the filings. He may already know.*
>
> *Ailen — your work has been the great quiet joy of my life. I have not told you this often enough. The portrait you carved of your grandmother is on my desk. I look at it every morning. If I am gone, take it back; do not let it sit in this office.*
>
> *I love you both very much. I am sorry to have left you with this.*
>
> *— T.*

The letter also surfaces **Joren Krill** — Hardby weighmaster, on C&V's payroll. Tamsin almost trusted him; she crossed his name out. Flag him for the Phase 4 indictment list. **Treat this beat with the gravity it deserves.** Let the table sit with it.

### Scene 7 — Harbor Before Throne (the Gynarchy Registry)

The operational reveal. Best run after Veska's, with **Zoria Weis** as the bridge.

**Zoria** is an under-clerk in the Gynarchy's Office of Mercantile Records. Caelith names her in Letter 2; the phrase is *"The autumn ledger" / "Has been settled."* She is a debt being called, **not a friend.** She reports to the Gynarchy. *"Do not say anything to her you would not want eventually said to the Gynarchy proper."* She brings the party to a back reading room where the harbor-commission appointment registry is kept.

**Read aloud or paraphrase as she opens the volume:**

> *"Rel Astra's harbor commission has seven seats. Three of them — the First, Third, and Fifth — are filled by old-house appointment that the seated Overking must confirm. The other four are appointed by the harbormaster and the city's mercantile guilds. The three old-house seats are filled now, on paper. But the three current holders are dead.*
>
> *The First Seat: Lord Hessar Olm, deceased 580 CY of natural causes, no successor formally seated.*
>
> *The Third Seat: Lady Mirin Vesh, deceased 581 CY in a fall on her own staircase, no successor formally seated.*
>
> *The Fifth Seat: Master Ervan Dale, deceased 582 CY of a brief illness, no successor formally seated.*
>
> *The seats sit vacant. By Aerdi practice, the city's harbor business is conducted by the four mercantile-guild seats only, with the three old-house seats 'pending confirmation.' The first vacancy is two years old this autumn. The second is fourteen months old. The third is six months old.*
>
> *The Aerdy succession is a question. The Overking is old. When a new Overking is seated, he must confirm the three old-house harbor commissioners before he can collect harbor revenue. Confirmation requires the commissioners to exist. Currently they do not. Whoever has positioned candidates to be appointed to those three vacancies before a new Overking is named will hand him three pre-confirmed votes — controlling the eastern revenue. Or, if those three vote against confirmation, no harbor commission exists to confirm and Rel Astra's revenue stops feeding the imperial treasury until the matter is resolved. Either way, whoever owns those three seats owns the eastern question.*
>
> *That is what your slogan means. Cindren and Vhal Rel Astra is one of the old commercial families with a vote in the appointment convocation."*

**What the party should leave with:**

- The conspiracy is positioning to seat three candidates on Rel Astra's harbor commission before the next Overking is named.
- The three vacancies were created on schedule. *(GM-only: Mirin Vesh's "fall" was Worth's work; Ervan Dale's "brief illness" was the same procedural-killing template the investigation journal flags as "Reln Pollow's understudy in Rel Astra" — GM flavor, not a player reveal; Hessar Olm's "natural causes" were natural and the conspiracy got lucky on the first one.)*
- **Tarlith Vhal Sereth, as senior partner, is one of the men eligible to vote in the appointment convocation.** His signature on the Vandrell escrow is the signature of a man positioning himself to seat three commissioners.
- The party does not yet know *which* three candidates. Phase 4 names them.

### Scene 8 — The C&V Hardby Branch (the indictment package)

Where the party recovers the documents that, with Veska's opinion, make Phase 4 prosecutable. Three paths in, keyed to approach.

The building: two-story counting house on Counting House Lane in the merchants' quarter. Vault below; senior partner's empty office and strongroom above. Standing staff:

- **Mira Cindren** — junior partner, Veshen Cindren's cousin. Public face. **Not in the conspiracy** — kept ignorant for plausible deniability. Gives nothing useful by herself. A possible Phase 4 witness if C&V collapses.
- **Solen Mereth** — head of Bond-Loading. Compromised. *Professionally proud and turnable* on **Persuasion DC 22** with notarized evidence in hand (soft path — see below).
- **Halver Onn** — vault clerk. Quiet, incurious, holds one of the two vault keys. Not corrupt.

**Smoking-gun targets, priority order:**

1. **The second set of books** from Solen's office cabinet (or vault side cabinet). Three slim volumes, ~18 months, ~140 transactions. Required.
2. **The senior partner's signature impression** from the upstairs blotter. Combined with #1, names Sereth personally.
3. **The defect-stamp** from the upstairs strongroom. Physical proof of the retained, used, officially-destroyed stamp.

#### Path 1 — Burglary (Quiet Pursuit; possible but harder on CC)

Approaches: front door two locks **DC 16 each**, watch every twenty minutes; staff door (alley) one lock **DC 16**, Marsh sleeps inside (**Stealth DC 14**); Solen's office window, old bars **DC 14** to bend silently. ~90 minutes between Marsh's eleventh-bell round and his third-bell round.

- **Solen's office cabinet:** **DC 16** lock plus a needle trap (**Investigation DC 15** to spot, **Dex DC 14** to disarm; failure 4d4 poison, Poisoned 1 hour). Take the three volumes.
- **Vault (if books not in office):** two **DC 17** locks or both keys; side cabinet **DC 15**.
- **Strongroom upstairs:** **DC 16** lock. Defect-stamp is third from the left in the velvet case.
- **Senior partner's office upstairs:** take the blotter folio whole.
- **Cipher safe (optional):** **DC 19** without Solen's key.

**Marsh wakes early** on more than one mistake. He is armed (short sword, Veteran statline) but not eager to die. The brass token sends him back to his room; he writes the morning shift report honestly either way.

**After.** Discovered at second bell after dawn. **Pressure Clock +3.** Plan to leave Hardby within 48 hours.

#### Path 2 — The Sympathetic Clerk (Commercial Cover)

Three to five Hardby days through **Vella Tannin**. Steps: first contact (casual, bread-shop or Castrian's salon — Castrian has known her since he sponsored her brother's debt); rapport (**Insight DC 14** she is interested but afraid, **DC 17** her brother is the lever); the lever (cross-reference her brother's lost cargo with Veska; bring it to her privately, gently); the copy-read (one week of carbon-copied second-set entries, ~18 transactions, including the Sereth signature pattern in two — enough for Veska's opinion to name Sereth). She cannot take the originals.

**The party owes Vella two things, both important.** Extraction within 24 hours (Castrian hides her overnight; a dawn coastal galley to Saltmarsh; cousins receive her). *If left, she is murdered within ten days.* And an honest indictment that names her brother as a victim. Caelith will honor both.

**Failure mode:** rushing, threatening, or trying to buy her loses the path entirely. She reports the contact to no one — but she will not work with the party. Fall back to burglary (much harder under CC) or take what Veska's notes alone give.

#### Path 3 — The Subpoena (Official Delegation)

Brane files under Aerdi commercial-disclosure law. Three days' procedural delay. Burns two of Caelith's last political favors with the Brane faction. On execution the firm produces forty-three volumes of public ledgers — clean and useless — and Mira receives the party personally in the conference room. Coffee is served.

**The value:** during execution, legal access to specific rooms.

- **Senior partner's office.** May not seize but may take a **charcoal rubbing of the blotter** — Sereth's signature impression.
- **Strongroom.** Inspect the chops. *Identify the defect-stamp by sight — third from the left, Veska's chip.* Demand a notarized record of all chops present; comparison to Veska's earlier records names the discrepancy.
- **Vault.** Note the locked side cabinet (Mira: "sealed escrow under client privilege") and demand a notarized inventory of its existence. *Aerdi commercial law does not permit branch-level "sealed escrow" outside the partner-level vault, which is in Rel Astra.* The cabinet's existence is itself evidence.

**Yield:** the blotter rubbing, a notarized chop-discrepancy record, and a notarized record of an improper sealed-escrow cabinet. Combined with Veska's prior records and Phase 1/2 evidence, this is enough for an Aerdi court to issue a warrant against the Hardby branch and a writ of partner accountability against Tarlith Vhal Sereth. **The party leaves Hardby with a court-issued warrant** — the cleanest legal weapon they will carry into Rel Astra.

#### Soft path — the Solen Mereth turn

Independent of the three formal paths. Solen, confronted *in a private and procedurally correct setting* with notarized evidence the transactions are not what he believed, can be brought to slow resignation. **Persuasion DC 22 with Veska's opinion in hand**, modified by roleplay. Most viable on CC. He will not betray the firm actively, but he will: go home, not destroy the second set, not warn Sereth, write to his eldest son (a junior officer in the Hardby city watch) explaining where the office documents are. *The party gets the second set delivered to their lodgings by sealed packet the morning after he resigns.*

**Failure is dangerous.** Without clean evidence, bullying, or bribing: he says nothing, ends the meeting, writes immediately to Sereth. **Pressure Clock +2.** Tamsin (if alive) is moved further; Tarsh (if available) is recalled. *Do not attempt before Scene 4.*

### Scene 9 — Caelith's Continuing Letters (interleaved)

Use as transitions between scene days or morning-post beats. Sealed post by trusted courier, two to three days each way.

- **Letter 1 (within two days of arrival):** Phase 2 Pressure Clock segment 3 fired (Reln Pollow or whoever is dead); the Council motion of Civic Reconciliation Closure has been filed by Brane and will pass within a fortnight (Audit Hall withdraws clerical support in three days — subsequent correspondence is private only); Trina has visited the Moravens; the party should do nothing that puts Ailen on the road during a moment when the firm is considering whether her continued health serves them.
- **Letter 2 (about a week in):** Caelith has a name for his office leak. Sending it by separate sealed pouch via separate courier; *if both arrive within three days, open the pouch in private, read, burn.* If only the letter arrives within five days, the courier is dead and the pouch is in someone else's hands — *do not act on any communication that claims to be from him until he writes again with the seal you saw on the workbench.* Introduces Zoria Weis and the phrase *"the autumn ledger."*
- **Letter 3 (toward the end):** *Do not bring the original of Veska's opinion to Rel Astra.* Send it via the established channel; carry only a copy. Sets up the Concordat framework — seven seats, three old-house, *the conspirators do not need to seat all three; they only need to seat the three that vote together first.*

### Scene 10 — The Pressure Clock (always running)

Hardby's clock is faster than Loftwick's. **Put the six-segment face on the table.** When you advance, say which trigger fired.

**Triggers:** recognized in Hardby (QP only) **+2**; visit C&V openly more than twice (CC) **+1**; successful C&V burglary **+3**; burglary detected **+1 additional**; confront Solen without clean evidence **+2**; Wreck confrontation breaks into combat **+1**; kill Tarsh / Solen / Mira **+2**; Tamsin raid Black Cog or Whitemoor **+2**; OD subpoena filed **+1**; each full session day with no progress **+1**.

**Milestones:**

1. **Recognition.** A face the party did not notice notices them — former courier, retired watchman once on Pollow's payroll, a waterfront fence who knew Sarth. Reports to Solen or Tarsh by morning.
2. **Surveillance.** Two watchers on the party's lodging. **Perception DC 13** for one, **DC 16** for the second. The picture sharpens each day.
3. **Witness loss.** GM picks based on what would hurt: Vella, Hesren, Lerra, Hella, or Castrian. *Vella vanishes between work and lodging, body found in a barrel two days later. Hesren is "reassigned" to a coastal posting; the carriage does not arrive. Lerra is taken on a quiet street. Hella is dismissed on a fabricated misfiling charge. Castrian is roughed up and leaves for Saltmarsh within a week.*
4. **Hired blade.** A single contracted attempt on a single PC in public. Survivable, uncomfortable.
5. **Squad attack.** Five to seven hired blades (Bandit Captain leading Bandits and Thugs). Trying to recover the indictment package and kill the PC carrying it (or Tamsin if she is with the party). Withdraw on item recovery or >50% casualties.
6. **The harbor closes.** Firm pays the harbor watch to delay shipping and post-horses. The Lacquer Wave's next sailing "cancelled for repairs." Post-stable wants seven days' notice. Party must improvise an exit — fishing skiff, private mounts at triple, Castrian's wine-merchant friend's carriage, or on foot.

**Expected end state: 4–5 segments at departure.** Do not over-protect.

### Scene 11 — Departure for Rel Astra

Three routes. The choice is now speed-versus-stealth.

- **Sea — the Lacquer Wave.** Captain **Yorra Mel**, Hardby coastal galley, six to ten days, eight gp per passenger. Passenger lists go to Hardby's outer harbor authority, who share with Rel Astra. *C&V Rel Astra will know within hours of arrival.* Buys six days unreachable at sea, smuggling room in wool bales, arrival in the Old Harbor (the conspiracy's doorstep). Does not advance the clock at departure.
- **Land — the Old Coastal Road.** Five to seven days. Two gp per horse. Fastest if all goes well. *Day one is dangerous.* QP-level surprise: safe. CC-level alarm: one hired-blade encounter on day three. OD-level visibility: shadowed the whole way, a serious attempt on day four (diplomatic escort is also deterrent). Clock **+1 per day** for CC and OD parties.
- **Diplomatic Carriage (OD only).** Eight to ten days. Total visibility, absolute legal protection. *Buys a formal audience with Sereth at his Rel Astra offices* — no other route guarantees this. Lets the Path-3 warrant execute cleanly at the Old Harbor Ward. Clock **+2 at departure, +1 per three days en route.**

**Read the ending read-aloud at the moment the party leaves.** Route-agnostic; adjust details to match:

> *Hardby in your last hour is the same as Hardby in your first: coal smoke and salt and the sound of a clock-tower bell that runs five seconds slow against every other clock you have heard since Loftwick. Whoever first set Hardby's main bell, three centuries ago, set it slightly wrong and no one has ever bothered to fix it. The city has run by it ever since.*
>
> *You carry, in a pouch sewn into a wool bale or strapped under a saddle skirt or folded inside the lining of a diplomatic dispatch case, a copied indictment package: Veska Maelan's notarized opinion, three pages of carbon-copied second-set ledger entries (or, if you took the originals, the bound volumes themselves), Tarlith Vhal Sereth's signature impression rubbed up from the senior partner's blotter, and the chipped Hardby clearing-stamp that officially does not exist. The original of Veska's opinion is on its way back to Loftwick by separate courier; what you carry is your proof on the road.*
>
> *You also carry, more quietly, what you have learned. The conspiracy is not Cindren and Vhal. The conspiracy is positioning to seat three commissioners on Rel Astra's harbor before the next Aerdy Overking is named, so that whoever wins the eastern succession finds Cindren-aligned hands controlling the eastern revenue. There are three vacant seats. There are three candidates. You do not yet know who the candidates are.*
>
> *You do not yet know the name behind the single capital letter "E." You do not yet know whose hand wrote the only annotation Veska could not place. You do not yet know what is older than the conspiracy you have named.*
>
> *Rel Astra rises out of the morning haze on the eastern shore, six days after Hardby for the slow routes and three for the road. The city is older than Hardby, larger than Loftwick, and runs on a clock that nobody set wrong: the harbor's, which keeps Aerdi imperial time exactly. Tarlith Vhal Sereth keeps an office on the Goldsmiths' Quarter side of the Old Harbor Ward, in a four-story building you have never seen and have, for two phases now, been preparing yourselves to walk into.*
>
> *You arrive on the morning of the third week.*

## Branches & contingencies

- **The party kills Tarsh, Solen, or Mira.** Strongly discouraged. They lose verbal corroboration (Tarsh), legal standing (Solen), or an innocent witness (Mira). Pressure Clock +2. The firm assumes a third party in play and Phase 4 begins under fertile uncertainty.
- **The party confronts Solen without clean evidence.** Pressure Clock +2 immediately. Tamsin moved further, Tarsh recalled. Soft path closes. Fall back to burglary or subpoena.
- **The party is recognized on Quiet Pursuit before they finish.** Pressure Clock +2. The firm hardens — bought watch sergeants, mercenary squads. The burglary window narrows; settle for Vella or the soft Solen if you can.
- **The party never finds Vella on Commercial Cover.** Burglary still possible but harder (watcher on Solen's office at night, trap freshly tuned). Worst case, lean on Veska's earlier records and pretexted observations — weakest package but Phase 4 still proceeds.
- **The party leaves Ailen in Hardby while Tamsin is alive but not yet recovered.** *Do not tell her her mother is in danger* — she will try to help and die. If they leave her, route her through Caelith's courier network to Loftwick; Trina houses her.
- **The party fails to extract Hesren in time.** Murdered roughly two weeks after departure. Caelith's Phase 4 correspondence carries the news. Indictment still viable on documents.
- **The party fails to extract Vella after copy-read.** Murdered within ten days. Handle with appropriate gravity.
- **The party blunders through Zoria.** She reports to the Gynarchy. The Gynarchy quietly maneuvers around the party in Phase 4 — not hostile, but no longer a back-channel ally.
- **The party never visits the Gynarchy registry at all.** They leave without the operational harbor-before-throne reveal; Phase 4 must surface it in Rel Astra. Less satisfying. Ensure Caelith's letter explicitly references *"the autumn ledger."*
- **The party tries to expose Sereth by letter from Hardby (without going east).** The letter will be received politely and filed; nothing happens until they appear in Rel Astra to testify in person. A non-move. Steer the party east.

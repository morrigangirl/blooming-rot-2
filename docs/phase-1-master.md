# Phase 1 — Loftwick's Return — Master GM Walkthrough

*A spoon-fed run guide. Read top to bottom; the order on this page is the order at the table.*

---

## What this phase is for

Phase 1 is the campaign's opening evening and the morning after. The party has just fought and apparently destroyed a man named **Haskur Vandrell** in the wilderness; they are returning to the Yeomanry capital, **Loftwick**, to be honored for it. The phase has three jobs:

1. Show the players that **the man they killed survived by design** — someone pulled him back through a pre-laid magical contingency, and someone in **Rel Astra** paid for that contingency six weeks before the fight.
2. Introduce **Caelith Dunivar**, the Yeomanry's Keeper of Foreign Correspondence, who will be the party's anchor NPC for the rest of the adventure. By the end of Phase 1 the players should feel they have a relationship with him.
3. Get the party to **accept (or refuse) a private commission** to travel east and find out who underwrote Haskur. Refusing ends the campaign politely; accepting opens Phase 2.

Phase 1 is investigative, not combat-heavy. **DCs cap at 16.** Party tier: **2 (levels 5–10).**

## Before you run it

Read these in this order:

- `packs/_source/phase-1-journals/journal-return-to-loftwick.json` — the scene sequence and complications.
- `packs/_source/phase-1-journals/journal-caelith-dunivar.json` — Caelith's profile, his two read-aloud speeches, and the evidence list.
- `packs/_source/phase-1-journals/journal-little-palace.json` — the building map (M = main floor, U = upper floor, C = cellar). Memorize the room codes for **M4 Refectory**, **U2 Caelith's Civic Office**, and **C5 Strong Room** — those are the three rooms Phase 1 actually uses.
- `packs/_source/welcome/journal-welcome.json` — table-of-contents handout the players see.

You need to have decided three things before play:

- **Who the Duke is.** Caelith refers only to "the Duke" — the real name is yours to drop later. Phase 1 does not require it.
- **Which first-night complication you'll use** (see Scene 6 below). One is enough. Two if the players never go to sleep.
- **Whether you'll allow the party to refuse the commission.** The rules support it. If you allow it, Phase 1 is the final session. Most groups won't refuse; the speech is calibrated to make refusal feel possible but unattractive.

## Scene-by-scene, in order

### Scene 1 — The Approach (afternoon, day 1)

The party crests the rise above Loftwick, three days back from the wilderness fight. **Read the Opening read-aloud** (it's on the *Opening — Return to Loftwick* page in the Return to Loftwick journal). The key beats: pale limestone walls, a republic that prefers function to majesty, two militia riders meet them at the milestone and *fall in at their flanks rather than ahead* — they're being escorted, not paraded. The party is routed to the **Lesser Gate**, not the West (triumphal) Gate.

At the gate, **Caelith Dunivar** steps forward with both palms raised — the old Yeomanry sign of peace and witness. He says: *"Heroes. You have spared the Yeomanry a long bad winter. Loftwick is grateful. Walk with me."* He doesn't introduce himself by full title yet. He's late fifties, lean, gray-haired, scholar's black coat, brass quill pin (twenty-year service award).

**No checks here.** The scene is mood-setting. The thing for the players to notice — and you should describe it deliberately — is that the welcoming committee waited for the *older man in the scholar's coat* to speak first.

### Scene 2 — The Walk to the Little Palace

Caelith walks the party along the Civic Way past the Audit Hall, the Council Chamber, the Lesser Treasury. He asks each PC **at most two questions**, small and observational, not interrogative. The journal gives you three samples — use them or improvise in the same key:

- *"Did the man you fought speak before he fled?"*
- *"Was anything in his bearing — accent, vocabulary, a hand-gesture — out of place for a westerner?"*
- *"Did he wear a ring?"*

He **answers nothing** about Haskur except *"the matter is open."*

**Insight DC 14:** his focus is on details, not outcomes — this is unusual for a welcoming committee. He is auditing them.

### Scene 3 — Public Welcome at the Little Palace

The Little Palace is the Yeomanry's guest house for foreign-credit visitors. On its red-carpeted steps, Caelith reads the **Public Congratulations** read-aloud (it's a page on the Caelith Dunivar journal). Verbatim:

> *"By the privileges of the Council and the gratitude of every yeoman household, we name your party Friends of the Common Defense, and direct that your names be entered in the register of this season's preserved actions. Lodging will be provided, accounts kept open for your supply, and your testimony — when offered — will be heard by full assembly. Loftwick does not forget her debts."*

A council page presents each PC with a **sealed wooden token (Friend of the Common Defense)**. Two scribes record everything. A reporter from *the Folio of the Mere* asks for a quote; politely refuse for them if the players don't think to.

**Insight DC 13:** Caelith is stage-managing, not lying. The choreography you should make sure the players notice — Caelith placed himself in front of the other officials, named the party first, and used the older constitutional phrasing (*"Common Defense," "preserved actions"*) that flags this as going into the durable record, not the daily one. He's protecting them with paperwork.

### Scene 4 — Settling In

Steward **Elsbet Vael** (mid-forties, quiet, formidable) shows the party to assigned rooms. Bath, fresh linen, a tray. Dinner is at the seventh bell in the **Refectory (M4)**. Free time for the party to interact with staff if they wish — the kitchen is M6, the page rooms M10. Cook **Brennan Kepf** is a soft-voiced gentle man in his fifties. Kitchen-girl **Mira Holt** has just started this week; Elsbet, if asked, mentions only that "Mira is new."

**No checks required.** Use this to let the party explore the building, examine their tokens, talk to each other.

### Scene 5 — Private Audience (the load-bearing scene of Phase 1)

After dinner, Caelith asks the party to join him in his **Civic Office on the second floor (U2)**. Door closed. No scribes. On the desk between him and them sits a small brass token — a quill above a closed gate — but he does not yet say what it is. (That's the **Hand of the Duke** sigil; he doesn't name it until Phase 2.)

**Read the Private Warning aloud.** This is the speech that pivots the campaign. Verbatim, from the Caelith Dunivar journal:

> *"What I said on the steps is true and sufficient. What I am about to say is also true — but not for public ears.*
>
> *The man you fought is named Haskur Vandrell. I know this because his name has crossed my desk twice in the last eight months — once attached to a writ of safe passage we did not issue, and once as a guest of record at a banquet we did not authorize.*
>
> *He was reduced to nothing in your fight, and yet he is somewhere now, breathing, recovering. That tells me he was not prepared by amateurs. Someone underwrote his survival.*
>
> *I have looked at what he left behind in the strongroom downstairs. There is a letter of credit in there. The instrument is real. The hand that countersigned it is in Rel Astra. The date on it is six weeks before the night you fought him. Whoever sent that letter knew his timeline.*
>
> *I will not pursue this through my office. I am not certain my office is closed at every door. I would prefer, instead, that someone whose name is already in our register — already a Friend of the Common Defense — were to travel east on a private commission, and write to me, and only to me, when they understood what was waiting for him there.*
>
> *If you decline, I will understand. The Yeomanry has paid you enough already. If you accept, I will say nothing about it tomorrow on the steps."*

**Caelith says this exactly once. Do not repeat it for a PC who steps out.** After the speech, he stands, takes a candle, and leads the party down the spiral stair to the **Strong Room (C5)**.

#### Scene 5b — The Strong Room (the seven items)

Caelith unlocks the strong room himself. Items are kept in an open chest. He answers factual questions, offers no theories, and **does not allow items to leave the room**. Walk the party through the seven items in this order:

| # | Item | What players see | Key check + reveal |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Battered Spellbook** | Cracked black leather, half the pages torn at the binding | Investigation DC 12: tears are deliberate, gaps were ritual contingencies carried on his person. Arcana DC 14: utility spells only — *comprehend languages, detect thoughts, sending,* one *counterspell*. This is a working copy. |
| 2 | **Sealed Wallet** | Three letters: one opened (a Hardby wine merchant declining a meeting), two sealed (one plain blot, one a **crane in flight above three coins**) | Investigation DC 13: the crane-and-coins is a private banking-house mark, recognized in eastern ports. History DC 15: specifically Rel Astran, used by counting houses handling non-aristocratic capital. |
| 3 | **Signet Ring** | Heavy brass (not silver), same crane-and-coins on the bezel | Investigation DC 12: worn evenly. He wore it often. Not a souvenir. |
| 4 | **Letter of Credit** *(the central clue)* | Folded vellum, two pages, formal Common with Old Aerdi numerals. Countersigned twice — Rel Astra (crane-and-coins) and Hardby (Yeomanry trade-clearing chop) | Investigation DC 10: authorizes drawing against escrow for "freight, victual, crew, and unforeseen passage" against a chartered hull. DC 14: dated **six weeks before the fight**. DC 16: marginal endorsement in a different hand — *"Hold for V., or his agent of record, against presentation."* If pressed for the escrow figure: **on the order of 10,000 gp** — ship-and-crew scale. |
| 5 | **Ritual Focus** | Palm-sized dark-wood length, concentric grooves, beeswax and salt residue | Arcana DC 13: focus for divination/abjuration ritual work. Not offensive. *(In Phase 2's daylight re-examination it will turn out to be burned — wax sigil of an unknown school of magic. Don't surface this in Phase 1.)* |
| 6 | **Brass Map Tube** | Three rolled charts — two commercial sea-route (Aerdi coast to Tilvanot), one hand-drawn caravan route Hardby-to-Loftwick with a small "R." mark next to a roadhouse south of Loftwick | Investigation DC 14: the "R." is recent, in the spellbook's annotation hand. *(Phase 2 will find a second tube inside this one — don't surface that yet.)* |
| 7 | **Unusual Coin** | Silver, heavy, not Yeomanry mint. Galley on one face, eye-and-pen on the reverse | History DC 12: a Rel Astran trade slug — not legal tender, used by certain counting houses as a token of account. Possessing one suggests a privileged relationship. |

Caelith asks for a **yes / no / defer** before midnight. *Defer* means "answer at breakfast."

### Scene 6 — First-Night Complications (between Scene 5 and Scene 7)

Pick **one** from the d6 table on the *Optional First-Night Complications* page. Roll or choose for the moment you need. None require resolution before Phase 2; each opens a thread.

1. **The Misseal.** A page-boy delivers a sealed letter to the most senior-looking PC. The seal imitates Caelith's office mark (three stars in a triangle) but is just slightly off-center. Inside: a polite invitation to "private breakfast" tomorrow with an unnamed councilman. Shown the seal, Caelith raises an eyebrow but says nothing. *Use this to set up Brane's faction-1 pressure in Scene 7.*
2. **The Cook's Niece.** Brennan Kepf knocks softly on a guest's door near midnight. He asks whether the party knows yet what is in the strongroom. His niece's husband does small caravan work out of Hardby; he has heard names; he will not give them up easily. Insight DC 13: afraid for his family, not for himself. *Signals that the working class is downstream of the same conspiracy.*
3. **The Early Watch.** A militia relief arrives at the third bell of morning instead of the fourth. Papers in order, sergeant unfamiliar to Elsbet. The papers are real; the timing is a clerical error — the kind a careful enemy could create on purpose.
4. **The Magpie at the Window.** A magpie taps on a guest's window at the second bell, copper ring on red thread tied to its right leg, no inscription. Bird wheels west when the window closes. *Detect Magic:* faint divination residue. *An arcane beat for divination PCs.*
5. **The Burning Page.** Faint smoke from the kitchen-alley midden at dawn. Half-burned vellum: *"...the Vandrell name is closed at this office. Continue all correspondence under the agreed pseudonym..."* Handwriting is not Brennan's, not Mira's, not Caelith's. Insight DC 14 + visitor's register: belongs to a clerk not currently in residence. *Confirms a leak in Caelith's own office without naming the leaker. (This is the Pell thread that finally surfaces in Phase 5.)*
6. **The Anonymous Note.** Plain hand on cheap paper, slid under a door: *"He paid in advance. So will the next one."* *Plants the successor-already-underwritten idea before any explicit exposition.*

### Scene 7 — Morning Pivot (seventh bell, day 2)

Breakfast in the Refectory. Elsbet brings the morning post.

**First, a hand-delivered card from Councilman Ortwell Brane** inviting the party to "a brief private statement" at his offices that afternoon. Caelith enters during breakfast and **quietly tells the party they may decline.** He does not explain. *(GM: Brane is the public-closure faction; Caelith does not want them spending their first morning reciting a controlled script. If the party goes, no real harm — Brane is a polite obstacle, not a villain — but Caelith's read-aloud below shifts to a less private register, and you lose the intimacy of the offer.)*

**Then, the regular post envelope.** Among the routine mail, a single envelope bearing the **same crane-and-coins seal** as Haskur's signet ring and one folded sheet in clear, careful hand:

> *"We hope your visit to Loftwick is brief."*

No way to trace it. **This closes Phase 1.** The party now knows two things: they are already known to someone, and that someone is in Rel Astra and does not want them to come east.

#### The forward hook (delivered at the same breakfast)

Once the envelope is on the table, Caelith asks again if the party will take the private commission. If they accept, he gives them three things on the spot — and explicitly **not** to write to his office; only to him personally:

1. **A letter of introduction** to **Veska Maelan**, a trade-clearing notary in Hardby who can read the Rel Astran countersignature better than Caelith can.
2. **A small sealed pouch of Yeomanry trade slugs** — a few hundred gp equivalent, redeemable at Yeomanry-aligned trading posts.
3. **A name only — "Holyn Sevarian"** — without explanation. He says: *"If the credit is real, his counting house will know. He is not necessarily an enemy. Begin by treating him as a witness."*

**Phase 2 begins when the party leaves the Refectory.**

## NPCs to know in Phase 1

- **Caelith Dunivar.** Keeper of Foreign Correspondence (real role, public). Hand of the Duke (real role, secret — not revealed until Phase 2). CR 8, AC 17, HP 127, social-combat specialist with *Cutting Words* (psychic-damage social attack) and Sneak Attack. **He will not fight the party.** If a player turns on him, he disengages with Cunning Action and the campaign closes.
- **Trina Alvere.** *Not present in Phase 1.* She arrives at the start of Phase 2 as Caelith's invited advisor. Mention her name only if a player has a reason to learn it early. (GM-only: she is a Warlock pacted to Iggwilv-as-Tasha; the campaign does not surface this for many phases.)
- **Elsbet Vael.** Little Palace steward, mid-forties, formidable in a quiet way. She runs the household and notices everything; she is not a player in the conspiracy.
- **Brennan Kepf.** Cook, fifties, soft-voiced, terrified for his caravan-driver in-laws. A Complication-2 NPC.
- **Mira Holt.** Kitchen-girl, new this week. *Not* a plant; Elsbet hired her honestly. Players who suspect her are wrong, but you may let them spend an evening confirming that.
- **Ortwell Brane.** Councilman, public-closure faction. Doesn't appear in person in Phase 1 unless the party accepts his invitation. The card is enough.
- **Haskur Vandrell.** Off-stage. The man the party fought. *(Actor sheet exists for him in `packs/_source/phase-1-actors/`; you will not use it in Phase 1.)*

Stat blocks for all of these live in `packs/_source/phase-1-actors/`.

## How Phase 1 ends

The trigger is **the breakfast envelope**, full stop. The phase closes the moment the party reads "We hope your visit to Loftwick is brief." Phase 2 begins the **next morning** — they sleep one more night at the Little Palace while Caelith arranges the morning evidence review.

The hand-off: tell the players that the evidence will be brought up out of the cellar and laid out in daylight on the Refectory table. Tell them Caelith has asked someone — *"a private citizen I trust"* — to be there too. (That's Trina; do not name her yet.)

## Branches & contingencies

- **The party refuses the commission.** Caelith pays them in full, says he will not contact them again, and the campaign ends. (Most groups will not refuse, but the rules support it as a real exit.)
- **The party accepts Brane's morning invitation.** No mechanical penalty. Brane wastes their morning with a polished closure-faction script and a sincere thank-you. They show up at the Refectory afternoon-of for the evidence review instead of morning-of. Phase 2 still begins on schedule.
- **The party tries to leave Loftwick that night.** Caelith does not stop them. The crane-and-coins envelope still finds them on the road. Phase 2 begins en route, with the Refectory evidence review replaced by a wayhouse review (less atmospheric — try to avoid this).
- **A PC turns on Caelith in the Strong Room.** He disengages, the Yeomanry militia escorts the offender out, and the rest of the party can choose to continue without them. Do not run combat with Caelith; he will outlast it and the campaign will close.
- **The leak (Burning Page complication) is pursued aggressively in Phase 1.** Caelith will gently ask the party to leave it alone; he wants to find the leaker without revealing he is looking. Push the thread to Phase 5 — that's where it has paid off.

## Carry to Phase 2

Make a private note of the following — these are seeds that mature later:

- **Which first-night complication you used.** It feeds Phase 2 or Phase 5 depending on which.
- **Whether the players noticed the "R." mark on the caravan map** (Item #6). If yes, you have a Phase 2.5 side-thread available; if no, drop it.
- **Whether the party trusts Caelith.** If they're suspicious, Phase 2's morning evidence review and Caelith's full Hand-of-the-Duke disclosure get more weight. If they trust him from the start, Phase 2 plays slightly faster.
- **Whether they accepted the commission cleanly, accepted reluctantly, or deferred to morning.** This shapes Caelith's tone in Phase 2 (more cautious if reluctant, more direct if cleanly accepted).
- **The crane-and-coins seal is unnamed at the end of Phase 1.** Players know the mark but not the firm. They learn "Cindren & Vhal" in Phase 2's Records Alcove. Do not let yourself slip and name the firm early.
